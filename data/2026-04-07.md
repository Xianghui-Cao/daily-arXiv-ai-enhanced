### [1] [Lattice studies of chimera baryons in Sp(4) gauge theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04433)
*Jong-Wan Lee,Ed Bennett,Luigi Del Debbio,Niccolò Forzano,Ryan C. Hill,Deog Ki Hong,Ho Hsiao,C. -J. David Lin,Biagio Lucini,Alessandro Lupo,Maurizio Piai,Davide Vadacchino,Fabian Zierler*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores chimera baryons, which are fermion bound states made of two fundamental (hyper)quarks and one in the antisymmetric representation. In composite Higgs models with top partial compositeness, these baryons act as partners to the top quark, contributing to its mass. The authors conduct non-perturbative lattice calculations for the chimera baryon spectrum within a Sp(4) gauge theory framework, both in the quenched approximation and with dynamical fermions.

Motivation: The motivation is to understand the role of chimera baryons in composite Higgs models, particularly as partners to the top quark, and to determine their low-lying spectrum using non-perturbative lattice techniques.

Method: The method involves performing non-perturbative lattice calculations for the spectrum of chimera baryons. The study uses a specific realization of composite Higgs models based on Sp(4) gauge theory. The calculations are done in the quenched approximation and with dynamical fermions, utilizing a new spectral density analysis technique for different lattice parameters.

Result: The results include numerical data for the chimera baryon spectrum obtained in the continuum and massless limits for the quenched case, and the measured spectrum and matrix elements for the dynamical fermions case across various lattice parameters.

Conclusion: The conclusion would likely summarize the findings on the chimera baryon spectrum and the implications for the top quark mass in the context of composite Higgs models, as well as the effectiveness of the new spectral density analysis technique.

Abstract: We study chimera baryons, fermion bound states composed of two (hyper)quarks transforming in the fundamental and one in the antisymmetric representation of a non-Abelian gauge group. While in QCD they coincide with ordinary baryons, in composite Higgs models (CHMs) with top partial compositeness, spin-1/2 chimera baryons serve as partners of the top quark and are responsible for its large mass. We perform non-perturbative lattice calculations of the low-lying spectrum of the chimera baryons, in a specific realization of CHMs based on a Sp(4) gauge theory. In the quenched approximation, we present the numerical results in the continuum and massless limits. Then, for dynamical fermions, we measure the spectrum and matrix elements by employing a newly developed spectral density analysis for several choices of the lattice parameters.

### [2] [A Precision Test of First Row CKM Unitarity from Lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04715)
*Ramón Merino*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper discusses the importance of precise CKM matrix elements for testing physics beyond the Standard Model and reviews current efforts by Fermilab Lattice and MILC collaborations in a correlated analysis using HISQ on MILC configurations with SChPT for chiral-continuum extrapolation.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to probe physics Beyond the Standard Model (BSM) through high-precision determinations of CKM matrix elements, particularly focusing on resolving the observed deficit in the first row unitarity relation.

Method: The method involves using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) on the $N_f=2+1+1$ MILC configurations and applying Staggered Chiral Perturbation Theory (SChPT) for a correlated analysis of lattice inputs necessary for extracting CKM matrix elements $|V_{ud}|$ and $|V_{us}|$ without nuclear inputs.

Result: The result is an ongoing effort by the Fermilab Lattice and MILC collaborations to improve the precision of CKM matrix elements $|V_{ud}|$ and $|V_{us}|$ through advanced lattice QCD calculations and chiral perturbation theory, which will contribute to more stringent tests of the CKM matrix's unitarity.

Conclusion: The conclusion highlights the progress made by the collaborations in refining the theoretical predictions for CKM matrix elements, which are critical for BSM physics searches, and emphasizes the need for further work to achieve higher accuracy in these fundamental parameters.

Abstract: High-precision determinations of Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix elements are essential probes of physics Beyond the Standard Model (BSM). Current precision tests show a deficit in the first row unitarity relation. At the current level of precision, the only relevant CKM matrix elements that contribute to this test are $|V_{ud}|$ and $|V_{us}|$. Without resorting to nuclear inputs, they can be extracted from the combination of the experimental decay width of kaon and pion leptonic decays, along with the theoretical calculation of their decay constants; combined with the decay width of semileptonic kaon decays, with the computation of the corresponding form factor at zero momentum transfer. We review current efforts by the Fermilab Lattice and MILC collaborations towards a correlated analysis of the lattice inputs needed for this test using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) on the $N_f=2+1+1$ MILC configurations along with Staggered Chiral Perturbation Theory (SChPT) as a functional form for the chiral-continuum limit.

### [3] [Toward Quantum Simulation of SU(2) Gauge Theory using Non-Compact Variables](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04837)
*Emanuele Mendicelli,Georg Bergner,Masanori Hanada*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper introduces three advancements in the orbifold lattice approach for simulating SU(N) gauge theories, which notably reduce the circuit depth and qubit requirements, and are validated through Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) in (2+1) dimensions.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to address the challenges of simulating lattice gauge theories on quantum computers by improving the orbifold lattice approach, aiming for a more scalable method that reduces both the circuit depth and the number of qubits required for simulation.

Method: The authors propose two new simplified Hamiltonians, an optimized encoding scheme for the SU(2) theory, and a modification to the Hamiltonian to decrease the need for large scalar masses. They also use Monte Carlo simulations to benchmark these improvements.

Result: The results show that the proposed improvements lead to a significant reduction in the resources needed for quantum simulations, with preliminary evidence supporting the effectiveness of the noncompact variables approach for scalability.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from the research is that the enhancements to the orbifold lattice approach provide a promising framework for the scalable quantum simulation of gauge theories, as evidenced by the successful application to SU(2) gauge theory in (2+1) dimensions.

Abstract: Simulating lattice gauge theories on quantum computers presents unique challenges that drive the development of novel theoretical frameworks. The orbifold lattice approach offers a scalable method for simulating SU($N$) gauge theories in arbitrary dimensions. In this work, we present three improvements: (i) two new simplified Hamiltonians, (ii) an encoding of the SU(2) theory with smaller number of qubits, and (iii) a reduction in the requirement for large scalar masses to reach the Kogut-Susskind limit, achieved via the inclusion of an additional term in the Hamiltonian. These advancements significantly reduce circuit depth and qubit requirements for quantum simulations. We benchmarked these improvements using Monte Carlo simulations of SU(2) in (2+1) dimensions. Preliminary results demonstrate the effectiveness of these developments and further validate the use of noncompact variables as a promising framework for scalable quantum simulations of gauge theories.
