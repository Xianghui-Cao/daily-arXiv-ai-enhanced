### [5] [Heavy-Meson Bag Parameters using Gradient Flow](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28517)

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper showcases the use of gradient flow with short flow-time expansion (GF+SFTX) for renormalizing four-quark operator matrix elements and bag parameters, crucial for neutral heavy-meson mixing and lifetimes. Method: The authors employ the gradient flow combined with the short flow-time expansion (GF+SFTX) as a renormalization technique. They use six RBC/UKQCD 2+1-flavor domain-wall fermion ensembles to calculate flowed bag parameters for a charm-strange system and then match them to the MS̅ scheme using perturbative SFTX coefficients up to next-to-next-to-leading order in QCD. We employ a multi-scale matching strategy and a renormalization-group improved flow-time evolution which allows for a reliable estimate of systematic uncertainties. For a fictitious neutral $D_s$ meson, we obtain the $ΔQ=2$ $\overline{\text{MS}}$ bag parameter ${\cal B}^{\overline{\text{MS}}}_1(3\,{\rm GeV})=0.7673(123)$, consistent with existing short-distance $D^0$ mixing determinations. We provide conversion formulae to re-express these results for an arbitrary choice of evanescent operators. These results demonstrate that GF+SFTX can deliver precise determinations of dimension-six four-quark operators and establish a framework for future lattice computations including more complex operator bases, where the challenge of power-divergent mixing is shifted to the continuum and handled in the SFTX.
