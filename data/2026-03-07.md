### [1] [Constrained Symplectic Quantization: Disclosing the Deterministic Framework Behind Quantum Mechanics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05072)
*Martina Giachello,Francesco Scardino,Giacomo Gradenigo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper introduces and applies constrained symplectic quantization, a method that allows for the sampling of real-time quantum observables in Minkowski spacetime, demonstrating its effectiveness through numerical tests on a 1D quantum harmonic oscillator. The method overcomes previous limitations by establishing an equivalence with the Feynman path integral, thus providing a direct link to real-time Green functions.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to overcome the structural limitations of the original symplectic quantization approach, which included an ill-defined non-interacting limit and a lack of direct correspondence between its correlation functions and those from the Feynman path integral. The aim is to provide a practical route for sampling real-time quantum observables beyond Euclidean-time importance sampling.

Method: The authors introduce constrained symplectic quantization, a reformulation where fields and action are analytically continued, and constraints are imposed on the intrinsic time Hamiltonian flow. This selects stable deterministic trajectories and defines convergent holomorphic integration cycles. They then apply this method to the quantum harmonic oscillator on a 1D real-time lattice and test various observables numerically.

Result: The results show agreement between numerical and exact results for one- and two-point functions. The method successfully reconstructs real-time features such as an oscillatory propagator, the discrete energy-gap spectrum, and the evolution of eigenstate probability densities. These findings support the claim that constrained symplectic quantization can sample real-time quantum observables effectively.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that constrained symplectic quantization provides a viable and practical method for sampling real-time quantum observables. It establishes an exact equivalence with the Feynman path integral at the level of the generating functional, offering a direct connection to real-time Green functions and overcoming the limitations of the original framework.

Abstract: Symplectic quantization is a functional approach to quantum field theory that allows sampling of quantum fluctuations directly in Minkowski space time by means of a generalized Hamiltonian dynamics in an extra time variable $τ$ which, at large times, samples a microcanonical ensemble. In a previous work we showed that, for an interacting scalar theory in 1+1 dimensions, this framework captures genuine real time features that are inaccessible to Euclidean simulations. That original formulation suffers from two structural limitations, an ill defined non interacting limit and the lack of a direct correspondence between its correlation functions and those generated by the Feynman path integral. To solve these problems we introduced constrained symplectic quantization, a holomorphic reformulation in which fields and action are analytically continued and constraints are imposed on the intrinsic time Hamiltonian flow. The constraints select stable deterministic trajectories and they define convergent holomorphic integration cycles for the corresponding microcanonical measure. In the continuum limit we establish exact equivalence with the Feynman path integral at the level of the generating functional, thus providing a direct link between intrinsic time correlators and real time Green functions. In this contribution, we apply the method to the quantum harmonic oscillator on a real-time 1-dimensional lattice. Testing various observables, we find agreement between numerical and exact results for one- and two-point functions, and we reconstruct characteristic real-time features such as an oscillatory propagator, the discrete energy-gap spectrum, and the evolution of eigenstate probability densities. These tests provide numerical evidence that constrained symplectic quantization can sample real-time quantum observables and offers a practical route beyond Euclidean-time importance sampling.

### [2] [Discretisation effects of gradient flows in QCD-like theories on the lattice](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05155)
*Pietro Butti,Michele Della Morte,Benjamin Jäger,Sofie Martins,J. Tobias Tsang*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper discusses large-scale lattice studies of a specific type of Yang-Mills theory, focusing on topological properties and discretisation effects for different values of $N_C$. It finds that simulations may be affected by discretisation effects of around 10%.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to explore the Corrigan--Ramond large-$N_C$ limit of Yang-Mills theory coupled with a two-index antisymmetric fermion, which could lead to SUSY Yang-Mills. The study aims to understand topological charges and the impact of different discretisations on the results.

Method: The researchers are generating ensembles for $N_C=4,5,6$ with lattice spacings between $0.11 - 0.08$ fm. They use a gluonic definition of the topological charge and gradient flow to analyze the effect of various discretisations of the kernel action, comparing Wilson and over-improved flows like DBW2. Additionally, they estimate discretisation effects through ratios of different reference flow times.

Result: The analysis reveals quantitative differences between Wilson and over-improved flows, such as DBW2, and suggests that current simulations might have discretisation effects of approximately 10%.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that while the study has made progress in understanding the topological properties and the influence of discretisation in the context of the large-$N_C$ Yang-Mills theory, there is an observed 10% level of discretisation effects in their simulations, indicating a need for further refinement or correction.

Abstract: Recent software advances now allow large-scale lattice studies of the Corrigan--Ramond large-$N_C$ limit of Yang-Mills theory coupled with a two-index antisymmetric fermion, providing a path to SUSY Yang-Mills. We are currently generating ensembles for $N_C=4,5,6$ for lattice spacings in the range $0.11 - 0.08$ fm. We report on two aspects of our work: the study of topological properties as well as estimates of discretisation effects. The first aspect is relevant since naively, fractional topological charges might be expected in our simulations. Using a gluonic definition of the topological charge combined with gradient flow, we perform an analysis of the effect of different discretisations of the kernel action, from which we identify and interpret quantitative differences between Wilson and over-improved flows such as DBW2. The second aspect is addressed by considering ratios of different reference flow times. We conclude that our current simulations might be affected by discretisation effects of order 10\%.

### [3] [Intrinsic Width of the flux tube in 2+1 dimensional Yang-Mills theories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05323)
*Lorenzo Verzichelli,Michele Caselle,Elia Cellini,Alessandro Nada,Dario Panfalone*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents updated results on the intrinsic width of the flux tube profile in (2+1)-dimensional Yang-Mills theory with an SU(2) gauge group, showing it remains constant at low temperatures and increases near the deconfinement temperature.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To identify and understand the behavior of the intrinsic width of the flux tube as a characteristic length scale in (2+1)-dimensional Yang-Mills theory, particularly its dependence on temperature and relation to the phase transition.

Method: The researchers examined the profile of the flux tube across a wide range of temperatures, focusing on the exponentially decaying tails to determine the intrinsic width. They also analyzed how this width changes with respect to the flux tube's length and the approach to the deconfinement temperature, using the Svetitsky-Yaffe mapping to interpret their findings.

Result: The intrinsic width of the flux tube was found to be independent of the flux tube's length and exhibited a constant value at low temperatures. As the system approaches the deconfinement temperature, the intrinsic width shows a growing trend, which is consistent with expectations from the universality class of the phase transition.

Conclusion: The study concludes that the intrinsic width of the flux tube is a well-defined characteristic length scale that remains constant at low temperatures and grows as the deconfinement temperature is approached, providing insights into the nature of the confining force in (2+1)-dimensional Yang-Mills theory.

Abstract: We present our updated results on the intrinsic width of the profile of the flux tube in (2+1)-dimensional Yang-Mills theory with SU(2) gauge group. We identify the intrinsic width as the characteristic length scale of the exponentially decaying tails of the profile of the flux tube. Inspecting a broad range of temperature, we check that this length does not depend on the length of the flux tube. Our estimations of the intrinsic width show a constant value at low temperature and a growing trend approaching the deconfinement temperature that can be understood from the universality class of the phase transition via the Svetitsky-Yaffe mapping.

</details>
