### [1] [Spectrum-Generating Algebra in Higher Dimensional Gauge Theories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05763)
*Thea Budde,Jiangjing Dong,Marina Krstić Marinković,Joao C. Pinto Barros*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: Researchers have identified and verified the existence of Quantum Many-Body Scars in spin-1 Quantum Link Models through an approximate spectrum-generating algebra, using a dualization process to simplify the original gauge theory. This work is expected to help guide quantum simulators towards exploring significant physical phenomena.

Motivation: The motivation behind this paper is to explore non-equilibrium properties of strongly interacting gauge theories, which are difficult to study with classical simulation methods. The recent advancements in quantum simulations offer a new avenue for studying these properties, particularly in two spatial dimensions, thus making it possible to uncover previously inaccessible features of such systems.

Method: The researchers utilized an approximate spectrum-generating algebra for a pure gauge plaquette ladder. They also employed a dualization process to map the original complex gauge theory onto a more tractable constrained spin chain, which allowed for the analysis of the model and the prediction of Quantum Many-Body Scars.

Result: The results show that by applying the proposed method, the researchers were able to predict and confirm the presence of Quantum Many-Body Scars within the spin-1 Quantum Link Models. Additionally, they suggested a set of observables that can be used to detect the approximate spectrum-generating algebra, which will be useful for future quantum simulations.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from this research is that there is now a viable approach to detecting and studying Quantum Many-Body Scars in spin-1 Quantum Link Models, which could provide valuable insights into the behavior of strongly interacting gauge theories. Furthermore, the identification of specific observables will assist in guiding quantum simulators to investigate interesting physical regimes.

Abstract: Non-equilibrium properties of strongly interacting gauge theories are often intractable with classical simulation methods. Due to recent developments of quantum simulations, studies of their properties in two spatial dimensions are becoming accessible. By demonstrating the existence of an approximate spectrum-generating algebra for a pure gauge plaquette ladder, we predict and verify the existence of Quantum Many-Body Scars in spin-1 Quantum Link Models. The analysis of the model is facilitated by a dualization process that maps the original gauge theory to a constrained spin chain. Was it not for the constraint, the system would have an exact spectrum-generating algebra. We propose a set of observables for diagnosing an approximate spectrum-generating algebra, which is expected to guide quantum simulators toward interesting physical regimes.
