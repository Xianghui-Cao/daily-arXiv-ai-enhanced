### [1] [RI/SMOM renormalization for lattice QCD: bilinear and three-quark operators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13750)
*Bernd A. Kniehl,Oleg L. Veretin*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper reviews the perturbative matching between RI/SMOM and MS schemes, providing conversion factors for bilinear operators and summarizing matching and anomalous dimensions for three-quark operators, all in the context of lattice QCD simulations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide accurate conversion factors and matching conditions that are essential for the interpretation of lattice QCD simulation results, especially for baryon distribution amplitudes and structure functions, by using a symmetric subtraction point relevant for these simulations.

Method: The authors employ perturbation theory to derive the matching conditions at the symmetric subtraction point. They calculate the conversion factors up to three-loop level for bilinear operators and summarize two- and three-loop level results for three-quark operators, including anomalous dimensions and matching conditions, all given in Landau gauge.

Result: The results include three-loop conversion factors for local quark currents and twist-two moments of structure functions, as well as two-loop RI/SMOM matching and three-loop anomalous dimensions for baryonic operators, specifically for N=0 and N=1 Mellin moments, which facilitate more precise lattice studies of baryon distribution amplitudes.

Conclusion: The conclusion would be that the provided conversion factors and matching conditions enable improved accuracy in lattice QCD studies, particularly for the analysis of baryon distribution amplitudes and structure functions, through the use of the RI/SMOM scheme at the symmetric point.

Abstract: We review perturbative matching between the regularization-invariant symmetric MOM (RI/SMOM) and MS schemes at the symmetric subtraction point, which is relevant for lattice QCD simulations. For bilinear operators we summarize three-loop conversion factors for local quark currents and for the n=2,3 twist-two moments of structure functions. For three-quark operators we summarize two-loop RI/SMOM matching for N=0 baryonic operators and three-loop anomalous dimensions together with two-loop matching for the N=1 Mellin moment, enabling improved lattice studies of baryon distribution amplitudes. All numerical results quoted here are given in Landau gauge.

</details>


### [2] [Renormalizing a three-flavor lattice calculation of the two-photon contribution to $K_L\toμ^+μ^-$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14077)
*En-Hung Chao,Norman Christ,Ceran Hu*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper discusses the computation of a long-distance contribution to the rare $K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-$ decay using lattice QCD and an effective three-flavor theory, while also addressing the determination of additional low-energy constants that depend on the charm quark mass through a four-flavor lattice QCD calculation.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to improve the accuracy of the Standard Model prediction for the rare $K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-$ decay by better understanding the long-distance effects and the impact of the charm quark mass on the low-energy constants in the effective theory.

Method: The authors use lattice QCD simulations with a three-flavor effective theory and propose a method for determining the additional low-energy constants that are dependent on the charm quark mass by performing a four-flavor lattice QCD calculation on a small volume with heavier than physical $u$ and $d$ quark masses.

Result: The result is a proposed method for computing the long-distance contribution to the rare $K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-$ decay, which includes the determination of the necessary low-energy constants that account for the effects of the charm quark mass.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the additional low-energy constants in the three-flavor effective theory can be determined from a practical four-flavor lattice QCD calculation, which allows for a more accurate prediction of the rare $K_L\to\mu^+\mu^-$ decay rate within the Standard Model.

Abstract: The Standard Model prediction for the rare $K_L\toμ^+μ^-$ decay depends critically on the long-distance contribution coming from the exchange of two photons. Such a contribution can be computed using lattice QCD and an effective three-flavor theory including only the $u$, $d$ and $s$ quarks, provided terms falling as the inverse square of the omitted charm quark mass, $1/m_c^2$ are neglected. Because of the missing Glashow-Iliopoulos-Maiani cancelation, this three-flavor theory contains additional low-energy constants that depend on $m_c$. Here we show how these constants can be determined from a practical four-flavor lattice QCD calculation performed on a small volume with $u$ and $d$ quark masses that are heavier than physical.

</details>
