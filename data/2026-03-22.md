### [1] [$Ξ_b \to Ξ$ form factors from lattice QCD and Standard-Model predictions for $Ξ_b \to Ξμ^+μ^-$ and $Ξ_b \to Ξγ$ decays](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18438)
*Callum Farrell,Stefan Meinel*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents the first lattice QCD determination of form factors for rare decays, including $\Xi_b \to \Xi\ell^+\ell^-$ and $\Xi_b \to \Xi\gamma$. It uses 2+1 flavors of domain-wall fermions, anisotropic clover action for the bottom quark, and advanced fitting techniques to control uncertainties. The results are used to predict branching fractions and angular observables for specific decays.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To provide a theoretical foundation for understanding rare decays involving $\Xi_b$ baryons by determining their form factors through lattice QCD, which can be used to make predictions for experimental measurements and test the Standard Model.

Method: Lattice QCD calculations with 2+1 flavors of domain-wall fermions, an anisotropic clover action for the bottom quark, and three-point functions at various source-sink separations. The form factor dependence on momentum transfer, pion mass, and lattice spacing is fitted using modified $z$ expansions, with dispersive bounds and asymptotic-behavior constraints applied.

Result: Obtained form factors for $\Xi_b \to \Xi$ transitions, enabling predictions for the branching fractions and angular observables of $\Xi_b^- \to \Xi^- \gamma$ and $\Xi_b^- \to \Xi^- \mu^+\mu^-$ decays within the Standard Model.

Conclusion: The calculated form factors contribute to the theoretical prediction of rare decay processes, providing a basis for future experimental tests and potentially revealing new physics beyond the Standard Model.

Abstract: We present the first lattice QCD determination of the $Ξ_b \to Ξ$ vector, axial-vector, and tensor form factors, which are relevant for the theory of rare decays including $Ξ_b \to Ξ\ell^+\ell^-$ and $Ξ_b \to Ξγ$. The calculation is performed with 2+1 flavors of domain-wall fermions at three different lattice spacings and pion masses in the range from approximately 430 to 230 MeV. The bottom quark is implemented using an anisotropic clover action. Three-point functions with a wide range of source-sink separations and model averaging are used to extract the ground-state contributions. We fit the dependence of the form factors on the momentum transfer, the pion mass, and the lattice spacing using modified $z$ expansions that account for subthreshold branch cuts, and apply dispersive bounds and asymptotic-behavior constraints to achieve controlled uncertainties in the full semileptonic kinematic region. Using our form factor results, we present Standard-Model predictions for the $Ξ_b^- \to Ξ^- γ$ and $Ξ_b^- \to Ξ^- μ^+μ^-$ branching fractions and two angular observables.

### [2] [Doubly Bottom and Bottom-Strange Tetraquarks in the Isoscalar Channel](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18667)
*Bhabani Sankar Tripathy,Nilmani Mathur,M. Padmanath*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study investigates the existence of tetraquark bound states in the doubly bottom and bottom-strange systems. It uses advanced computational methods on lattice QCD, and finds strong evidence for a bound state in the doubly bottom tetraquark but not in the bottom-strange tetraquark.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to search for possible tetraquark bound states within the isoscalar channel, specifically focusing on doubly bottom and bottom-strange tetraquarks, which could provide insights into the nature of strong interactions and the spectrum of hadrons.

Method: The researchers performed calculations on four ensembles with dynamical quark fields up to the charm quark, using lattices with different spacings. They employed overlap action for light and strange quarks, NRQCD for heavy bottom quarks, and used the variational method along with Lüscher's scattering amplitude analysis to account for finite volume effects.

Result: The study found strong evidence for a deeply bound state in the doubly bottom tetraquark channel, indicating the potential existence of such a particle. However, no conclusive evidence was found for the existence of a bound state in the bottom-strange tetraquark system.

Conclusion: The research concludes that there is strong evidence supporting the existence of a doubly bottom tetraquark bound state, while the status of the bottom-strange tetraquark as a bound state remains inconclusive.

Abstract: We present our recent investigation on doubly bottom and bottom-strange tetraquarks in the isoscalar channel in search of a possible tetraquark bound state. The calculations are performed on four ensembles with dynamical quark fields up to the charm quark generated by the MILC Collaboration with various lattice spacings. Two volumes have been used to account for finite volume effects. Overlap action has been employed to calculate light and strange quark propagators and NRQCD formulation is utilized for heavy bottom quarks. Finite volume energy has been calculated using the variational method followed by rigorous scattering amplitude analysis à la Lüscher. We find strong evidence for a deeply bound state in the doubly bottom tetraquark channel, but no conclusive evidence for the existence of a bottom-strange tetraquark.

<details>

Method: The method involves using lattice computations to investigate hadronic screening masses, including baryonic modes and preliminary non-static mesonic modes, at temperatures ranging from the GeV scale up to the electroweak scale. The results are then compared with predictions from the perturbative expansion in the three-dimensional effective theory.

Result: The results show that there are persistent higher-order effects, even at asymptotically large temperatures, and these include non-perturbative effects that persist up to the electroweak scale. This provides evidence for the complex microscopic structure of QCD at extreme temperatures.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that by comparing lattice results with perturbative predictions, we gain new insight into the nature of strong interactions at high temperatures, revealing the importance of non-perturbative contributions to the behavior of QCD at extreme conditions.

Abstract: Novel theoretical and computational strategies have opened the possibility of exploring thermal QCD at the non-perturbative level at unprecedented temperatures, reaching from the GeV scale up to the electroweak scale. A number of observable quantities are now being investigated in this regime. Key ones are the hadronic screening masses, which encode the correlation length of the medium and thus the extent to which strong interactions are screened in a thermal environment. In these proceedings we present recent lattice results for hadronic screening masses, including baryonic modes and preliminary non-static mesonic modes. These results can be compared with predictions from the perturbative expansion in the three-dimensional effective theory valid at asymptotically large temperatures. The comparison reveals persistent higher-order effects including those of non-perturbative origin, up to the electroweak scale, shedding new light on the microscopic structure of QCD at extreme temperatures.

Abstract: The $R$-ratio is a phenomenological observable of great relevance, both in itself and in applications such as the dispersive approach to the muon anomalous magnetic moment. Abstract: Spectral reconstruction is a well studied numerically ill-posed problem which arises due to the relation of the Euclidean correlator to the spectral function via an inhomogeneous Fredholm equation of the first kind. Several different methods are on the market to resolve this issue, each taking different approaches and assumptions. ### [6] [Investigating the role of tetraquark operators in lattice QCD studies of the $a_0(980)$ and $κ$ resonances](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.19192)
*Andrew D. Hanlon,Daniel Darvish,Sarah Skinner,John Meneghini,Ruairí Brett,John Bulava,Jacob Fallica,Colin Morningstar,Fernando Romero-López,André Walker-Loud*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study examines the role of tetraquark operators in lattice QCD for scalar mesons, finding that at least one tetraquark operator is necessary to reliably determine the spectrum and revealing an additional energy level in the Kη subsystem with implications for understanding the κ and a0(980) resonances.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To investigate the importance of tetraquark operators in the analysis of scalar mesons (κ and a0(980)) within lattice QCD, and to explore their impact on the finite-volume stationary states' spectrum and the scattering K-matrix parametrization.

Method: The researchers used Hermitian correlation matrices with single-meson, meson-meson, and tetraquark interpolating operators to extract the spectrum of finite-volume stationary states. They worked with a specific ensemble having m_π ≈ 230 MeV and m_πL ≈ 4.4, and they explored hundreds of local and extended tetraquark operators to determine the spectrum in each symmetry channel.

Result: The inclusion of tetraquark operators was found to be essential for reliable determinations of the spectrum, as it revealed an extra energy level below the Kη threshold in the Kη sub-system. This finding has implications for the scattering K-matrix and the properties of the κ and a0(980) resonances.

Conclusion: Tetraquark operators play a crucial role in the accurate determination of the spectrum of scalar mesons within lattice QCD, and their inclusion leads to the discovery of additional energy levels, which can provide more insight into the nature of scalar meson resonances such as the κ and a0(980).

Abstract: The role of tetraquark operators in studying the isodoublet strange $κ$ and isovector nonstrange $a_0(980)$ scalar mesons in lattice QCD is examined using an ensemble with $m_π\approx230$ MeV and spatial extent $L$ such that $m_πL\approx4.4$. Hermitian correlation matrices using both single-meson, meson-meson, and tetraquark interpolating operators are used to extract the spectrum of finite-volume stationary states in the appropriate symmetry channels. Hundreds of local and extended tetraquark operators are explored. Determinations of the spectrum in each channel are found to be unreliable without the inclusion of at least one tetraquark operator. For example, the inclusion of tetraquark operators with isospin 1/2 and strangeness 1 quantum numbers reveals the existence of an additional energy level in the $Kη$ sub-system below the $Kη$ threshold. The implications of this on parametrizing the scattering $K$-matrix through a well-known quantization condition to extract properties of the $κ$ and $a_0(980)$ scalar meson resonances are discussed.
