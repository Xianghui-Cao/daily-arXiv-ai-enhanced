### [1] [Non-perturbative determination of the QCD Equation of State up to the electroweak scale](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09330)
*Michele Pepe*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a non-perturbative determination of the QCD Equation of State for 3 massless quark flavors over a wide temperature range, showing that non-perturbative contributions are necessary for accurate description even at high temperatures. The method used, which can also be applied to QCD with more quark flavors, combines shifted boundary conditions with a constant physics line based on a renormalized coupling.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To accurately determine the QCD Equation of State (EoS) for three massless quark flavors across a broad temperature range, including very high temperatures near the electroweak scale, and to compare this non-perturbative EoS with perturbative predictions, highlighting the need for non-perturbative terms in the high-temperature regime.

Method: A combination of shifted boundary conditions and the identification of lines of constant physics using a non-perturbatively defined renormalized coupling, allowing for the exploration of the previously uncharted high-temperature region of the QCD phase diagram.

Result: The non-perturbative QCD Equation of State was determined for a wide range of temperatures, from the electroweak scale down to 3 GeV, and it was found that an accurate description of the EoS requires non-perturbative contributions even at very high temperatures.

Conclusion: The study demonstrates that non-perturbative effects are essential for the precise determination of the QCD Equation of State, especially at high temperatures, and provides a general methodology that is applicable to QCD with different numbers of quark flavors.

Abstract: The QCD Equation of State with $N_f=3$ massless quark flavours is determined non-perturbatively over a broad range of temperatures, extending from the electroweak scale down to 3 GeV, and smoothly connecting to the low-temperature regime. The comparison with perturbative predictions shows that, even at temperatures approaching the electroweak scale, the Equation of State can be accurately described only by adding terms beyond the known perturbative series, including non-perturbative contributions. The strategy that allows this investigation in the previously unexplored high-temperature regime combines shifted boundary conditions with a determination of the lines of constant physics based on the running of a non-perturbatively defined renormalized coupling. This methodology is general and can be applied to QCD with four or five massive quark flavours.

### [3] [First-Principles Determination of the Proton-Proton Fusion Matrix Element from Lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09554)
*Zi-Yu Wang,Xu Feng,Bo-Hao Jian,Lu-Chang Jin,Chuan Liu*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the proton-proton fusion matrix element, including Lellouch-Luscher finite-volume corrections and accounting for two-nucleon rescattering. The study indicates small nonvanishing 2-body current contributions and highlights the challenges in determining the 2-body low-energy constant L_{1,A} with high precision.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a first-principles determination of the proton-proton fusion matrix element, which is a key weak reaction in stellar energy production, using lattice QCD calculations at a pion mass of approximately 432 MeV. The goal is to understand the underlying physics and improve the accuracy of the theoretical predictions for this fundamental process.

Method: The authors use a lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) approach to calculate the pp fusion matrix element, incorporating Lellouch-Luscher finite-volume corrections that account for two-nucleon rescattering. They employ bi-local nucleon-nucleon interpolating operators and a variational analysis to suppress excited-state contamination and to determine the two-nucleon energy spectrum and scattering parameters through Luscher's finite-volume formalism.

Result: The result of the calculation before including rescattering effects yields <d|J|pp>/g_A = 0.984(10), suggesting a small but non-zero 2-body current contribution. The inclusion of rescattering effects via the Lellouch-Luscher factor significantly modifies the 2-body contribution, and the large uncertainties in the two-nucleon scattering parameters greatly affect the finite-volume corrections. The value of the 2-body low-energy constant L_{1,A} is determined as 6.0(7.1) fm^3, which is consistent with phenomenological extractions within naturalness.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from this work is that while it is feasible to perform ab initio lattice QCD calculations for weak two-nucleon reactions, such as proton-proton fusion, there are significant intrinsic challenges, particularly in the precise determination of the 2-body low-energy constant L_{1,A}. The findings establish a basis for future studies at or near the physical pion mass, despite the large uncertainties in the current results.

Abstract: Proton-proton fusion is the fundamental weak reaction initiating stellar energy production, and a first-principles determination of its matrix element remains a long-standing goal of nuclear theory. We present a lattice QCD calculation of the pp fusion matrix element at m_pi~432 MeV. We implement Lellouch-Luscher (LL) finite-volume (FV) corrections within a 2+J->2 framework, accounting for two-nucleon (2N) rescattering, to relate FV matrix elements to infinite-volume counterparts. Excited-state contamination is suppressed using bi-local nucleon-nucleon interpolating operators and a variational analysis with three lowest momenta. This enables determination of 2N energy spectrum and scattering parameters via Luscher's FV formalism. Before including rescattering effects in the LL factor, we obtain <d|J|pp>/g_A = 0.984(10), where g_A is the axial charge. The deviation from unity indicates a small nonvanishing 2-body current contribution. Our analysis shows that rescattering effects in LL factors substantially modify the 2-body contribution, while large uncertainties in 2N scattering parameters propagate strongly into FV corrections. Thus, precise determination of the 2-body low-energy constant L_{1,A} remains highly challenging with current lattice inputs. Despite the large uncertainty, L_{1,A}=6.0(7.1) fm^3 is compatible, at the level of naturalness, with phenomenological extractions. This work demonstrates feasibility and intrinsic challenges of ab initio lattice QCD calculations of weak 2N reactions, and establishes a foundation for future studies at or near the physical pion mass.

### [4] [Phase diagram of 4D SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at $θ=π$ via imaginary theta simulations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09604)
*Akira Matsumoto,Mitsuaki Hirasawa,Jun Nishimura,Atis Yosprakob*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: Researchers used simulations with an imaginary theta parameter and analytic continuation to explore the CP symmetry in 4D SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, overcoming the sign problem. They implemented the stout smearing technique and reweighting method to investigate the topological property of the gauge field and detect CP symmetry breaking.

Motivation: The motivation is to explore whether the CP symmetry of 4D SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at θ=π is spontaneously broken in the confined phase and restored at the deconfining temperature, despite the difficulty in direct simulation due to the sign problem.

Method: The researchers simulated the theory using an imaginary theta parameter, performed analytic continuation to real theta values, and employed the hybrid Monte Carlo simulation with the stout smearing technique. They also used the reweighting method to study the smearing-time dependence of observables and the scaling behavior of the system.

Result: Preliminary results indicate a clear scaling behavior of the observable with respect to the smearing step parameters, and the order parameter for CP symmetry was computed, showing evidence of CP breaking and hints towards the restoration temperature.

Conclusion: The study provides initial evidence for CP symmetry breaking and its potential restoration at the deconfining temperature, suggesting further investigation into the phase diagram of 4D SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at θ=π.

Abstract: It has been speculated that the CP symmetry of 4D SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at $θ=π$ is spontaneously broken in the confined phase, and it is recovered precisely at the deconfining temperature. The direct simulation of the theory at $θ=π$ is, however, difficult due to the sign problem. We therefore simulate the theory with an imaginary theta parameter and perform analytic continuation to the real theta to explore the phase diagram. We implement the stout smearing technique in the hybrid Monte Carlo simulation to recover the topological property of the gauge field. The smearing-time dependence of the observable is investigated using the reweighting method with respect to the smearing step parameters, and a clear scaling behavior is observed. The order parameter of the CP symmetry is then computed in the scaling region to detect symmetry breaking. We report preliminary results on the expected CP breaking and restoration temperature.
