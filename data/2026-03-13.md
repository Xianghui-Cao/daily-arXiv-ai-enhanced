### [1] [$\bar{D}$-meson Nucleon Scattering from Lattice QCD at the Physical Point](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12251)
*Wren Yamada,Yan Lyu,Kotaro Murakami,Takumi Doi*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The first lattice QCD study of s-wave scattering between D-meson and nucleon at the physical point, using (2+1)-flavor configurations. The interaction potential for both isospin I=0 and I=1 channels was derived, showing a short-range repulsion and a shallow long-range attraction. The I=0 channel is more attractive than the I=1, and no pentaquark state was found in the s-wave D̄N system.

Motivation: To investigate the s-wave scattering of the D̄-meson and the nucleon using lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at the physical quark masses, to understand the interaction potential and phase shifts, which could provide insight into the possible existence of pentaquark states.

Method: The HAL QCD method was applied to the four-point correlation function of the D̄N system to extract the leading-order potential, which was used to calculate the s-wave phase shifts for low-energy D̄N scattering with isospin I=0 and I=1.

Result: The s-wave phase shifts indicate a weakly attractive I=0 channel with a positive scattering length of 0.246 ± 0.105 fm and a repulsive I=1 channel with a negative scattering length of -0.086 ± 0.050 fm. No bound states were observed, suggesting the absence of a pentaquark state in the s-wave D̄N system.

Conclusion: The study provides evidence for a short-range repulsive core and a shallow attractive pocket in the D̄N interaction potential, with the I=0 channel being more attractive than the I=1. The results suggest that there is no pentaquark state present within the s-wave D̄N system.

Abstract: We report the first lattice QCD study of the $s$-wave scattering of the $\bar{D}$-meson and the nucleon at the physical point, utilizing (2+1)-flavor configurations generated by the HAL QCD collaboration with a pion mass of $m_π\simeq 137$ MeV and a lattice spacing of $a\simeq0.084$ fm. By applying the HAL QCD method to the four-point correlation function of the $\bar{D}N$ system, we obtain a leading-order potential of the derivative expansion of the interaction kernel, which is then used to extract the $s$-wave phase shifts of low-energy $\bar{D}N$ scattering. Both the isospin $I=0$ and $I=1$ channels have a short-range repulsive core and a shallow attractive pocket in the intermediate to long-range region, though the $I=0$ channel is more attractive than the $I=1$ channel. We also observe that the $\bar{D}N$ potential exhibits more attraction than the $KN$ potential, which is its analog in the strange sector. In terms of the $s$-wave phase shifts, the $I=0$ channel shows a weak attractive behavior in the low-energy region with a positive scattering length of $0.246 \pm 0.105 (_{-0.051}^{+0.084})$ fm, whereas the $I=1$ channel shows repulsion with a negative scattering length of $-0.086 \pm 0.050 (_{-0.001}^{+0.037})$ fm. No bound states are found in both isospin channels, indicating the absence of a pentaquark state in the $s$-wave $\bar{D}N$ system.
