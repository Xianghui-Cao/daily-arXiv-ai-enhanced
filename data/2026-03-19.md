### [1] [$K π$ scattering as a step towards $B \to K^* \ell^+ \ell^-$ from Lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17900)
*Felix Erben,Matthew Black,Peter Boyle,Matteo Di Carlo,Vera Gülpers,Maxwell T. Hansen,Nelson Pitanga Lachini,Rajnandini Mukherjee,Antonin Portelli,J. Tobias Tsang*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a new exploratory lattice calculation for the B to K* l+ l- decay, using a variational determination of finite-volume Kπ states and 1+J to 2 finite-volume formalism. The study is conducted on an RBC/UKQCD domain-wall fermion ensemble with controlled heavy-quark dynamics, allowing for a broad range of heavy-to-light transitions into resonant final states. Initial two-point results for the K* to Kπ system are provided, and future work will extend the calculation to lower q^2 and include charmonium resonance effects.

Motivation: The motivation behind this paper is to provide precise and systematically improvable hadronic input from lattice QCD for rare b to s l+ l- decays, which are highly sensitive tests of the Standard Model. In particular, the B to K* l+ l- channel requires a first-principles treatment of the resonant Kπ final state and controlled heavy-quark dynamics.

Method: The method used in this paper involves a new exploratory lattice calculation that combines a variational approach to determine finite-volume Kπ states with the 1+J to 2 finite-volume formalism. The computation is performed on an RBC/UKQCD domain-wall fermion ensemble with a lattice spacing inverse of approximately 2.7 GeV, using a dual heavy-quark strategy that includes both a relativistic heavy-quark action and domain-wall heavy masses extrapolated from charm. All correlation functions are computed using (stochastic) distillation techniques.

Result: The results of this paper include the initial two-point function outcomes for the K* to Kπ transition, which show the accessible kinematic region and allow for a controlled study at high q^2. The authors also discuss the potential for extending the calculation to lower q^2 values and incorporating the impact of charmonium resonances in future work.

Conclusion: The conclusion of this paper is that the new lattice QCD calculation provides a promising framework for studying the B to K* l+ l- decay with systematic control over heavy-quark dynamics and access to resonant Kπ final states. The current setup supports a wide range of heavy-to-light transitions and paves the way for further research, including the exploration of lower q^2 regions and the inclusion of charmonium resonance effects.

Abstract: Rare $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ decays provide some of the most sensitive tests of the Standard Model and require precise and systematically improvable hadronic input from lattice QCD. For the phenomenologically important channel $B\to K^*\ell^+\ell^-$ this entails a first-principles treatment of a resonant $Kπ$ final state together with controlled heavy-quark dynamics. We present the status of a new exploratory lattice calculation that combines a variational determination of finite-volume $Kπ$ states with the $1+J\to2$ finite-volume formalism to access the relevant matrix elements. The computation is carried out on an RBC/UKQCD domain-wall fermion ensemble with $a^{-1} \approx 2.7\,\mathrm{GeV}$ and employs a dual heavy-quark strategy, using both a relativistic heavy-quark action tuned to the physical $b$ mass and domain-wall heavy masses extrapolating from charm. All correlation functions are computed using (stochastic) distillation, providing a versatile setup that supports a broad range of heavy-to-light transitions into resonant final states. We show first two-point results for the $K^*\leftrightarrow Kπ$ system and discuss the accessible kinematic region, which allows for a controlled study at high $q^2$. The outlook for extending the calculation to lower $q^2$ and for incorporating effects from charmonium resonances is outlined.
