*Samuel Kováčik,Matej Hrmo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: An improved algorithm for analyzing matrix models, which moves a cluster of eigenvalues and successfully converges to the true vacuum state, overcoming the issue of simulations getting stuck in vacuous states.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to address the problem of simulations, particularly those using the Metropolis algorithm, getting stuck in vacuous states, which hinders the exploration of the relevant configuration space in matrix models used to express physical models.

Method: The paper introduces an algorithm that facilitates the movement of a cluster of eigenvalues within the simulation, designed to improve convergence to the true vacuum state and thus enhance the analysis of matrix models.

Result: The proposed algorithm effectively overcomes the challenge of simulations being trapped in non-informative states and demonstrates successful convergence to the true vacuum state, thereby allowing for a more thorough exploration of the configuration space.

Conclusion: The new algorithm provides a significant improvement in the analysis of matrix models by ensuring that the simulation can move beyond vacuous states, leading to better and more reliable outcomes in the study of related physical systems.

Abstract: Various physical models can be expressed in terms of matrices. A valuable tool for analysing matrix models is numerical simulations, often the Metropolis algorithm with various improvements. The downside of this approach is that the simulation may become stuck in a vacuous state, and probing the relevant parts of the configuration space might be difficult. Here, we propose an algorithm that moves around a cluster of eigenvalues and show that it converges to the true vacuum state.

  <summary>Details</summary>

Result: The 2dVoid model demonstrated the ability to reliably identify the location of center vortices when they have moderate visibility. Additionally, scalability to higher dimensions was shown to be feasible through tiling strategies, which also help in reducing the costs associated with training the model.

Conclusion: The 2dVoid model successfully identifies center vortices in 2D SU(2) lattices and the use of tiling strategies suggests a promising path for extending the technique to higher dimensions, potentially aiding in the broader study of confinement in lattice gauge theories.

Abstract: As a first step towards machine identification of confining objects in thermalized lattice gauge configurations, we present our 2dVoId model for center vortex identification on pure SU(2) lattices in $D = 2$ dimensions. We create a training set by inserting thin Z2 vortices at various locations on a zero action lattice, and then distort those configurations by applying random SU(2) gauge transformations, noise, and by thickening the vortices via cooling. For moderate vortex visibility, our model is able to reliably identify the location of center vortices. We additionally demonstrate scalability through tiling strategies, which will enable generalization to higher dimensions while reducing training costs.

*Alessandro De Santis,Dominik Erb,Harvey B. Meyer*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the charged-neutral pion mass splitting, using a Pauli-Villars regulated photon propagator to avoid power-law finite-volume effects. The final result is in good agreement with experimental data, validating the formalism and providing insights for electromagnetic corrections to other observables.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to validate a recently proposed framework for calculating the charged-neutral pion mass splitting using lattice QCD, which employs a Pauli-Villars (PV) regulated photon propagator. This approach aims to simplify the treatment of the infinite-volume limit and to provide a controlled setting for electromagnetic corrections.

Method: The method involves using CLS ensembles to perform the lattice QCD calculation, studying finite-volume effects, taking the continuum limit, and extrapolating to the physical point for several values of the UV cutoff scale Λ. The Cottingham formula is used to decompose the results into elastic and inelastic contributions at a fixed Λ.

Result: The final result after removing the cutoff scale Λ is M_{π^+} - M_{π^0} = 4.56(22) MeV, which is consistent with the experimental measurement, thus supporting the validity of the new formalism and its potential for application to other observables.

Conclusion: The study successfully validates the use of the Pauli-Villars regulated photon propagator in lattice QCD calculations for the charged-neutral pion mass splitting, demonstrating the feasibility of this approach for electromagnetic corrections and offering a foundation for future work on similar observables.

Abstract: We present a lattice QCD calculation of the charged-neutral pion mass splitting $M_{π^+} - M_{π^0}$ at $\mathcal{O}(α_\mathrm{em})$ using a recently proposed framework based on a Pauli-Villars (PV) regulated photon propagator defined in the continuum and infinite-volume limit, with $Λ$ acting as an additional UV cutoff scale. The use of this propagator avoids power-law finite-volume effects, allowing for a straightforward treatment of the infinite-volume limit. We perform the calculation using CLS ensembles, studying finite-volume effects, the continuum limit and the extrapolation to the physical point for several values of the scale $Λ$. By means of the Cottingham formula, we further decompose the result into elastic and inelastic contributions at fixed $Λ$. Our final result, after removing the cutoff scale $Λ$, is $M_{π^+} - M_{π^0} = 4.56(22)$ MeV, in good agreement with the experimental measurement. This calculation serves as a validation of the formalism in a well-controlled setting and offers useful insights into the application of electromagnetic corrections to other observables.

### [6] [Mellin Moments of the Unpolarized Gluon PDF in the Proton from Nonlocal Operators in Lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30193)
*Joseph Delmar,Krzysztof Cichy,Martha Constantinou,Yong Zhao*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a lattice QCD determination of the Mellin moments of the unpolarized gluon parton distribution function in the proton, using an Nf=2+1+1 ensemble and addressing systematic effects. The work provides a ratio of higher-order gluon moment to the gluon momentum fraction, with uncertainties considered.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a precise determination of the gluon parton distribution function within the proton, which is crucial for understanding the internal structure of the proton and for high-energy physics experiments.

Method: The method involves using lattice QCD, specifically employing nonlocal gluon operators coupled to momentum-boosted proton states, and analyzing the short-distance operator product expansion (OPE) of the reduced gluon Ioffe-time distribution.

Result: The researchers extracted the ratios of higher-order gluon moments to the gluon momentum fraction, focusing on the stability of these moments under scale evolution and providing a determination of the ratio ⟨x^3⟩_g/⟨x⟩_g at 2 GeV, with associated uncertainties.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the study successfully determined the ratio of the third Mellin moment to the first, with a thorough investigation of systematic uncertainties, contributing to the knowledge of the gluon parton distribution function.

Abstract: We present a lattice QCD determination of the Mellin moments of the unpolarized gluon parton distribution function in the proton. The analysis is based on matrix elements of nonlocal gluon operators coupled to momentum-boosted proton states. The calculation relies on an $N_f=2+1+1$ ensemble of maximally twisted mass fermions with clover improvement and the Iwasaki-improved gauge action, at a pion mass of approximately 260 MeV. Working within the short-distance operator product expansion (OPE) of the reduced gluon Ioffe-time distribution, we extract ratios of higher-order gluon moments, $\langle x^n\rangle$ with $n>1$, to the gluon momentum fraction, $\langle x\rangle$. We investigate systematic effects associated with the truncation of the order of moment in the OPE, the choice of minimum and maximum Wilson-line separations entering the analysis, and the treatment of mixing with the quark-singlet under perturbative matching. The stability of the extracted moments is further studied under scale evolution using DGLAP equations, allowing us to assess uncertainties related to perturbative truncation by varying the scale. Our work provides a determination of the ratio $\langle x^3\rangle_g/\langle x\rangle_g$ at a scale of 2 GeV, with uncertainties that account for both statistical and the dominant theoretical systematic uncertainties.
