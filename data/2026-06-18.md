### [1] [Hybrid renormalization in lattice calculation of baryon LCDAs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18777)
*Mu-Hua Zhang*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The author presented recent advancements in lattice calculations of baryon light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) at Baryons 2025, employing a novel hybrid renormalization scheme for octet baryons that improves the reliability of quasi-distribution amplitudes (quasi-DAs).


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To improve the accuracy and reliability of baryon light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) through advanced lattice calculations and a new renormalization technique.

Method: Implemented a novel hybrid renormalization scheme for octet baryons, performed calculations on $N_f=2+1$ ensembles with specific fermions and gauge action across three different lattice spacings to remove linear divergences and obtain consistent quasi-DAs.

Result: Obtained smooth, self-consistent quasi-DAs that provide a strong basis for further extraction of baryon LCDAs using LaMET. The results are expected to be reported at the continuum limit and physical pion mass in the future.

Conclusion: The use of a hybrid renormalization scheme has led to significant progress in the determination of baryon quasi-DAs, setting the stage for more accurate extractions of LCDAs and providing a robust framework for future studies.

Abstract: At the 2025 International Conference on the Structure of Baryons (Baryons 2025), I presented our recent progress in lattice calculations of baryon light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs). In Ref.[1], we implemented a novel hybrid renormalization scheme for octet baryons, leading to reliable determinations of quasi-distribution amplitudes (quasi-DAs).
  The calculations were performed on $N_f=2+1$ ensembles with stout-smeared clover fermions and a Symanzik-improved gauge action at three lattice spacings, $a = 0.052,0.077,0.105$ fm. The hybrid renormalization removes linear divergences in lattice matrix elements and yields smooth, self-consistent quasi-DAs, providing a solid foundation for LaMET-based extractions of baryon LCDAs. Results at the continuum limit and physical pion mass will be reported in the near future.

### [2] [The Collins-Soper kernel from a vacuum soft function](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19221)
*Anthony Francis,C. -J. David Lin,Wayne Morris,Yong Zhao*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The Collins-Soper kernel is accurately computed using a new method on the Euclidean lattice, showing good agreement with theoretical expectations and revealing saturation at large transverse separations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To develop a precise method for calculating the Collins-Soper kernel, which is fundamental to understanding the rapidity evolution of soft functions in QCD, by employing space-like Wilson lines with complex-directional vectors on the Euclidean lattice.

Method: The calculation is performed using pure gauge theory on the lattice, with the soft function obtained from vacuum configurations. The method utilizes space-like Wilson lines with complex-directional vectors, allowing for high statistical precision in the determination of the soft function and its rapidity dependence through Collins-Soper evolution.

Result: The extracted Collins-Soper kernel has comparable errors to those from state-of-the-art lattice calculations based on hadronic observables and demonstrates a saturated behavior at large transverse Wilson-line separations, indicating a novel feature of the non-perturbative regime of QCD.

Conclusion: The new lattice method provides a powerful tool for the precise extraction of the Collins-Soper kernel, which can be used to improve our understanding of the rapidity evolution of soft functions, and it suggests that the kernel may exhibit saturation in the non-perturbative region.

Abstract: The Collins-Soper kernel is calculated from a vacuum soft function using space-like Wilson lines with complex-directional vectors on the Euclidean lattice. Our pure gauge calculations with this method achieve high statistical precision in computing the soft function, whose rapidity dependence is well described by Collins-Soper evolution across a wide range of rapidity differences. The extracted kernel contains errors comparable to those achieved in state-of-the-art lattice calculations based on hadronic observables, but exhibits saturated behavior at large transverse Wilson-line separations.

</details>
