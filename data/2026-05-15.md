*Ivan Horváth*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper introduces a regularized formula for the glue scalar density (gluon condensate) using Dirac spectral density, and discusses its implications on quark scalar densities, IR phase, symmetry breaking, and the UV-IR relation in QCD.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to better understand the relationship between glue and quark scalar densities, the connection to the infrared (IR) phase, the distinction between anomalous and spontaneous symmetry breaking, and the interplay between ultraviolet (UV) and IR aspects of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD).

Method: The author derives a regularized formula for the glue scalar density by utilizing the Dirac spectral density as described in the reference [arXiv:2509.03509].

Result: The new formula provides insights into the connection between glue and quark scalar densities, sheds light on the IR phase, clarifies the difference between anomalous and spontaneous symmetry breaking, and elucidates the relationship between UV and IR scales in QCD.

Conclusion: The derived formula for the glue scalar density offers a deeper understanding of the fundamental aspects of QCD, including the interplay between different scalar densities, the nature of symmetry breaking, and the UV-IR connection.

Abstract: I derive the regularized formula for glue scalar density (gluon condensate) in terms of Dirac spectral density [arXiv:2509.03509], and elaborate on its uses and meaning. Particular attention is given to understanding of what this new formula reveals about the relation between glue and quark scalar densities, how it relates to IR phase, how it clarifies the distinction between anomalous and spontaneous ways of breaking symmetries, and what it says about the relation between UV and IR in QCD.

### [2] [Extraction of spectral densities from lattice correlators: decoupling signal from noise](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14652)
*Alessandro Lupo,Nazario Tantalo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents an alternative method to extract smeared spectral densities from Euclidean correlators, which avoids the Backus-Gilbert regularization by decomposing the solution and truncating the sum to optimize between signal and noise. This method is evaluated for its performance and can also complement the existing Backus-Gilbert approach.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation of this paper is to provide a new approach to the extraction of smeared spectral densities that does not depend on the Backus-Gilbert regularization, aiming to improve the treatment of the inverse problem by optimizing the balance between signal and noise through a different decomposition and truncation strategy.

Method: The authors propose a method where the solution is decomposed into a sum of terms, akin to singular value decomposition, with the key insight that terms contributing the most to statistical noise contribute the least to the central value. The focus then shifts to determining the optimal truncation point for this summation to maximize the signal before the noise becomes overwhelming. This method is analyzed both independently and as a supplementary tool for the stability analysis in the Backus-Gilbert method.

Result: The results show that the proposed method effectively addresses the inverse problem by providing a clear criterion for truncation, thus allowing for a good balance between capturing the signal and controlling the noise. It demonstrates potential as both a standalone procedure and as a complementary technique to the Backus-Gilbert method for extrapolating unbiased results.

Conclusion: The paper concludes that the new method offers a viable and potentially superior alternative to the Backus-Gilbert regularization for extracting smeared spectral densities, with the added benefit of aiding in the stability analysis required for obtaining reliable results.

Abstract: We expand the treatment of the problem of the extraction of smeared spectral densities from Euclidean correlators introduced in [Phys. Rev. D 99, 094508], providing an alternative which does not rely on the Backus-Gilbert regularization. This is possible due to the observation that the solution can be decomposed into a sum of terms, in the spirit of the singular value decomposition, where those with the largest contribution to the statistical noise happen to contribute the least to the central value of the smeared spectral density. The analysis of the systematics of the inverse problem is then shifted to finding the optimal truncation of such summation, so that the signal is saturated before the noise explodes. We scrutinise the performance and systematics of this approach either as a standalone procedure, or to complement the stability analysis required to extrapolate the unbiased result in the Backus-Gilbert regulated version of the solution.

### [3] [Analyzing the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model with the Worldvolume HMC method](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14965)
*Masafumi Fukuma,Yusuke Namekawa*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method is successfully applied to the two-dimensional Hubbard model, overcoming the sign problem and providing reliable predictions for physical observables in conditions where standard methods fail.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation of this paper is to address the severe sign problem encountered in the two-dimensional Hubbard model when the system is doped. The authors aim to show that their method, WV-HMC, can predict physical observables with controlled statistical errors in a regime where traditional quantum Monte Carlo methods are ineffective due to the sign problem.

Method: The researchers apply the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method to the two-dimensional Hubbard model on an 8x8 lattice at a specific temperature and interaction strength. This method is used as an alternative to the standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo, which fails under these conditions because of the sign problem.

Result: The application of the WV-HMC method resulted in successful predictions of physical observables with controlled statistical errors on the 8x8 lattice, at a temperature T/t = 1/6.4 and interaction strength U/t = 8.0, demonstrating its effectiveness in overcoming the sign problem.

Conclusion: The study concludes that the WV-HMC method is effective for simulating the two-dimensional Hubbard model in regimes where the sign problem severely hinders other simulation techniques, such as the standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo, thus opening up new possibilities for studying doped systems.

Abstract: We apply the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method [arXiv:2012.08468] to the two-dimensional Hubbard model, which is known to suffer from a severe sign problem when the system is doped (away from half filling). We show that the method predicts physical observables with controlled statistical errors on an $8 \times 8$ lattice at temperature $T/t = 1/6.4 \approx 0.156$ and interaction strength $U/t = 8.0$ ($t$ is the hopping amplitude), for which the standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo fails.

</details>
