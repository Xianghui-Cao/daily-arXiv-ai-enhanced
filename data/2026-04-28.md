### [1] [Lattice field theories with a sign problem](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24290)
*Gert Aarts,Dénes Sexty*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper reviews the sign problem in QCD and related field theories, discussing potential solutions such as holomorphic extensions, complex Langevin dynamics, and the use of dual variables and tensor renormalisation group. It also explores the feasibility of these methods for lattice systems and highlights the promise shown by machine learning approaches.

Motivation: The motivation is to address the sign problem that hinders the determination of the QCD phase diagram using lattice QCD, by reviewing and exploring potential solutions and their applicability to lattice systems.

Method: The paper adopts a review approach, encompassing a range of methods including holomorphic extensions (Lefschetz thimbles, holomorphic flow, contour deformations), complex Langevin dynamics, and the introduction of new degrees of freedom (dual variables and the tensor renormalisation group). It also evaluates the potential of machine learning techniques in this context.

Result: The review provides an overview of various approaches to potentially solve or control the sign problem, regardless of its severity, and assesses their feasibility for application in lattice systems. It also points out the promising directions where machine learning could contribute to solving the sign problem.

Conclusion: The conclusion emphasizes the importance of developing and testing new methodologies to tackle the sign problem in QCD and suggests that while there are several promising avenues, further research is needed to fully resolve the issue, especially in the context of real-time dynamics and lattice QCD.

Abstract: The sign problem obstructs the determination of the QCD phase diagram in the temperature-baryon chemical potential plane using lattice QCD. We review the sign problem in QCD and related field theories, including applications to real-time dynamics. We focus on approaches where the sign problem can potentially be solved or controlled, irrespective of its severeness. These include holomorphic extensions -- Lefschetz thimbles, holomorphic flow, contour deformations, and complex Langevin dynamics --, and the introduction of new degrees of freedom -- dual variables and the tensor renormalisation group. We also highlight directions in which machine learning approaches have shown promise. Since many methods are first tested in simpler models, we provide an outlook on their feasibility for lattice systems.
