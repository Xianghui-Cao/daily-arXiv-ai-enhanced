### [1] [Diffusion Models for Sampling Near Criticality in Lattice Field Theories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08505)
*Yang-yang Tan,Gert Aarts,Diaa E. Habibi,Biagio Lucini,Lingxiao Wang*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores the use of generative diffusion models as denoisers for 2D and 3D lattice φ⁴ theory, showing that these models can accurately reproduce scalar observables and momentum-space propagators. The authors also introduce diagnostics and an HMC-referenced ESS to evaluate the learned drift. They demonstrate cross-volume generalization with a fully convolutional architecture, allowing for accurate sampling on larger lattices without retraining.

Motivation: The motivation is to develop and validate generative diffusion models as effective samplers for lattice field theories, particularly in the context of 2D and 3D φ⁴ theory, and to explore their capability to generalize across different lattice sizes.

Method: The method involves using generative diffusion models as reverse-SDE samplers, which are then validated against ensembles generated by Fourier-accelerated HMC combined with Wolff cluster updates. The researchers also introduce local diagnostics and an HMC-referenced ESS to assess the performance of the learned models. A fully convolutional neural network architecture is used to enable cross-volume training and generalization to unseen lattice sizes.

Result: The results show that the generative diffusion models can reproduce scalar observables and the momentum-space propagator G(|k|) with minor residual biases. The cross-volume trained model successfully generalizes to a larger, unseen lattice size (L=64) while improving or matching the performance of in-distribution training, except for some critical observables in the broken phase.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that generative diffusion models can be effective denoising samplers for lattice φ⁴ theory, and that cross-volume generalization is a viable approach for large-volume sampling, with the score learned from small-lattice configurations being transferable to larger volumes.

Abstract: We investigate generative diffusion models as denoising samplers for two- and three-dimensional lattice $φ^4$ theory across the symmetric, near-critical, and broken phases. Validated against ensembles generated by Fourier-accelerated HMC combined with Wolff cluster updates, the reverse-SDE sampler reproduces scalar observables and the momentum-space propagator $G(|k|)$, with residual bias concentrated in the zero-mode and, in three dimensions, the action density. We introduce two local diagnostics and an HMC-referenced effective sample size (ESS), which probe the learned drift directly, through a Metropolis-adjusted Langevin acceptance rate, and through observable-level bias and variance. Exploiting a fully convolutional architecture with weights shared across different volumes ($V=L^D$), we show that cross-volume training transfers to unseen sizes, matching or slightly improving in-distribution training in the two-dimensional symmetric and broken phases. A three-dimensional model trained on $L \in \{4, 8, 16, 32\}$ reproduces the propagator and most scalar observables at the unseen lattice size $L = 64$ across the phase diagram, with the residual susceptibility excess in the broken phase as the main exception, and improves several critical observables relative to in-distribution $L = 64$ training. This establishes cross-volume generalization as a viable mechanism for large-volume sampling, and the score learned from many cheap small-lattice configurations transfers to the target volume without retraining.
