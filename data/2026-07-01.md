### [1] [Higher-order hopping-parameter expansion by human-AI collaboration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31492)
*Masakiyo Kitazawa,Tatsuya Wada*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents efficient algorithms for computing higher-order terms in the hopping-parameter expansion, with computational costs significantly lower than a reference calculation. The development of these algorithms was facilitated by collaboration between human researchers and AI coding agents.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop more efficient methods for evaluating higher-order terms in the hopping-parameter expansion of $\textrm{Tr}\ln M$ on $SU(N_\textrm{c})$ gauge configurations, which are computationally intensive when using traditional methods.

Method: The authors developed algorithms that use a trie data structure to compute high-order terms, specifically up to $κ^{12}$, with reduced computational cost compared to a single staple evaluation. They also verified the correctness of their algorithms against a reliable but expensive reference calculation.

Result: The developed algorithms were able to evaluate the $κ^8$, $κ^{10}$, and $κ^{12}$ terms at computational costs of approximately 20, 460, and 8900 times that of a single staple evaluation, respectively, demonstrating significant efficiency gains over traditional methods.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the new algorithms provide an efficient way to compute higher-order terms in the hopping-parameter expansion, and that the collaboration between human researchers and AI coding agents was crucial to this achievement.

Abstract: We develop efficient algorithms for evaluating higher-order terms in the hopping-parameter expansion of $\textrm{Tr}\ln M$ on $SU(N_\textrm{c})$ gauge configurations. The resulting algorithms, which exploit a trie data structure for the computation of high-order terms, evaluate the $κ^8$, $κ^{10}$, and $κ^{12}$ terms at computational costs of approximately $20$, $460$, and $8900$ times that of a single staple evaluation, respectively. The correctness of the algorithms is verified by comparison with a computationally expensive but reliable reference calculation. We emphasize that collaboration between human researchers and AI coding agents was essential to the development of these algorithms.
