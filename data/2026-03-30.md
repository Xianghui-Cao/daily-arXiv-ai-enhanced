*Tetsuya Onogi,Tatsya Yamaoka*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores conserved charges in the staggered fermion Hamiltonian within 3+1 dimensions, demonstrating that these charges generate axial SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R transformations for low-energy degrees of freedom, with implications for anomalies.

Method: The authors decompose staggered fermions into Majorana components and use lattice translation symmetries to construct a set of conserved non-singlet charges. They then analyze the algebra of these charges and their commutation relations on the lattice.

Result: It was found that despite the non-trivial non-commutativity of the charges on the lattice, they generate axial SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R transformations for the low-energy degrees of freedom when approaching the continuum limit. The study also discusses potential implications for anomalies.

Conclusion: The constructed conserved charges exhibit non-commutative properties on the lattice but transform as expected under axial symmetry in the continuum limit, suggesting a connection to anomalies which could be further explored.

Abstract: We study conserved charges of the staggered fermion Hamiltonian in 3+1 dimensions. By decomposing staggered fermions into Majorana components and exploiting lattice translation symmetries, we construct a set of conserved non-singlet charges. We analyze their algebra andshow that, although the charges exhibit nontrivial non-commutativity on the lattice, they generate axial SU(2)_L \tines SU(2)_R transformations for low-energy degrees of freedom in the continuum limit. ### [2] [The Axial Charge in Hilbert Space and the Role in Chiral Gauge Theories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26104)
*Tatsuya Yamaoka*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores the Hamiltonian formulation of 1+1-dimensional staggered fermions and reconstructs vector and axial charge operators within the Wilson fermion formalism. It shows that these operators, which commute with the Hamiltonian, lead to a lattice theory with exact axial symmetry and allows for the study of Symmetric Mass Generation in specific models.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop a lattice theory that preserves exact axial symmetry while recovering vector symmetry in the continuum limit, and to apply this framework to the study of Symmetric Mass Generation (SMG) in 3-4-5-0 models.

Method: The authors use the Hamiltonian formulation and Wilson fermion formalism to construct vector and axial charge operators. They then formulate a gauge theory where the axial U(1)_A symmetry is promoted to a gauge symmetry, and they build a Hamiltonian using the eigenstates of the axial charge operator.

Result: The result is a lattice theory with well-defined integer chirality states and exact axial symmetry, which can accommodate symmetry-preserving interaction terms with quantized chiral charges. The SMG mechanism is studied within this framework, though further numerical work is needed to confirm its realization in interacting systems.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the constructed lattice theory with exact axial symmetry provides a promising approach to studying the SMG mechanism, and it may be extended to other models with similar symmetries, pending further numerical investigation.

Abstract: We investigate the Hamiltonian formulation of 1+1-dimensional staggered fermions and reconstruct the vector and axial charge operators, originally identified by Arkya Chatterjee et al., within the Wilson fermion formalism. These operators commute with the Hamiltonian and reduce, in the continuum limit, to the generators of the vector and axial $\mathrm{U}(1)$ symmetries. A notable feature of the axial charge operator is that it acts locally on operators and possesses quantized eigenvalues. Its eigenstates can therefore be interpreted as fermion states with well-defined integer chirality, analogous to those in the continuum theory. This structure enables the formulation of a gauge theory in which the axial $\mathrm{U}(1)_A$ symmetry is promoted to a gauge symmetry. We construct a Hamiltonian in terms of the eigenstates of the axial charge operator, thereby preserving exact axial symmetry on the lattice while recovering vector symmetry in the continuum limit. As applications, we study the implementation of the Symmetric Mass Generation (SMG) mechanism in the 3-4-5-0 models. Our framework admits symmetry-preserving interaction terms with quantized chiral charges, although further numerical investigation is required to confirm the realization of the SMG mechanism in interacting systems.

Main category: hep-lat

Conclusion: The study provides a significant improvement in the precision of the strange electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon, offering a more reliable approach without the need for chiral extrapolations.

Abstract: We present the strange electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon using lattice QCD simulations with degenerate light, a strange, and a charm quark in the sea with masses tuned to their physical values. For the first time, the strange electromagnetic form factors are computed at the continuum limit using only ensembles simulated with physical quark masses, eliminating the need for chiral extrapolations and their associated systematic uncertainty. We obtain the momentum transfer dependence of the form factors using the $z$-expansion and provide the strange electric and magnetic radii, as well as the strange magnetic moment. When combining our statistical errors and systematic uncertainties stemming from the momentum transfer dependence fit, our errors are an order of magnitude smaller than those associated with experimental determinations of the strange electromagnetic form factor.
