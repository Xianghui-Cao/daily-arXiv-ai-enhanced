### [1] [Topological structure of the entanglement radius of Yang-Mills flux tubes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10255)
*Rocco Amorosso,Sergey Syritsyn,Raju Venugopalan*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper expands on previous research by providing a detailed analysis of the entanglement radius (ξ0) in (2+1)D Yang-Mills theory, specifically focusing on how the geometry of the entanglement region affects the flux tube entanglement entropy (FTE^2).


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To further understand the intrinsic thickness (entanglement radius ξ0) of flux tubes and its impact on the entanglement entropy when the entangling region is of a similar magnitude to ξ0, adding to the topological structure knowledge of color flux tubes.

Method: The study employs lattice geometries for the entanglement region V, where the length that cross-cuts the flux tube is comparable to the entanglement radius ξ0, to examine the FTE^2 contributions from color degrees of freedom within the flux tube.

Result: Findings reveal new details about the topological structure of the entanglement radius of color flux tubes, supporting and expanding upon the conclusions of the referenced work (arXiv:2601.17199).

Conclusion: The research provides additional evidence for the existence of a characteristic entanglement radius ξ0 and its significance in determining the non-zero contribution of FTE^2, enhancing our understanding of the topological properties of flux tubes in (2+1)D Yang-Mills theory.

Abstract: We expand on recent work arXiv:2601.17199 demonstrating the existence of a novel entanglement radius $ξ_0$ characterizing flux tube entanglement entropy (FTE$^2$) in (2+1)D Yang-Mills theory. This physical scale corresponds to the intrinsic thickness of the flux tube that must be fully severed by an entangling region for color degrees of freedom in the flux tube to contribute non-zero FTE$^2$. We consider here geometries of the entanglement region $V$ on the lattice where the length of the region cross-cutting the flux tube is of the same magnitude as $ξ_0$. Our results further the conclusions of arXiv:2601.17199 by adding detailed new information on the topological structure of the entanglement radius of color flux tubes.

### [2] [Electromagnetic pion mass splitting using PV-regulated photon propagator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10496)
*Alessandro De Santis,Dominik Erb,Harvey B. Meyer*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a method using a Pauli-Villars regulated photon propagator in the continuum and infinite volume to improve the calculation of electromagnetic corrections, such as the charged/neutral pion mass difference, with lattice QCD simulations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to address the need for sub-percent precision in hadronic observables calculations within lattice QCD, which necessitates the inclusion of strong isospin-breaking and electromagnetic effects. Current methods often suffer from power-law suppressed finite-size effects and lack straightforward crosschecking capabilities.

Method: The authors propose employing a Pauli-Villars regulated photon propagator that is defined in the continuum and infinite volume, which can be used to more accurately compute leading-order electromagnetic corrections. This method is applied to the charged/neutral pion mass difference using CLS ensembles.

Result: The result is an improved methodology for calculating electromagnetic corrections, which can reduce finite-size effects and provide a more direct comparison with phenomenology and other calculations, potentially enhancing the precision of lattice QCD computations.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the proposed method offers a promising approach to refine the determination of electromagnetic corrections in lattice QCD, particularly for observables like the HVP or nucleon masses, and it demonstrates its application to the pion mass difference.

Abstract: Several hadronic observables are nowadays computed in lattice QCD with a sub-percent precision which requires the inclusion of strong isospin-breaking and electromagnetic effects. Most of the methods that implement the photon propagator in finite-volume lead to power-law suppressed finite-size effects and do not allow for a straightforward crosscheck against phenomenology and other calculations. Both issues can be avoided by working with a Pauli-Villars regulated photon propagator defined directly in the continuum and infinite volume. This methodology can be profitably exploited to improve the determination of leading-order electromagnetic corrections to several observables such as the HVP or nucleon masses. In this work we apply the strategy to the charged/neutral pion mass difference using CLS ensembles.

</details>
