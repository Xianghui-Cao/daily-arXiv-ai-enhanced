### [1] [Enhanced Sampling Techniques for Lattice Gauge Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01287)
*Timo Eichhorn,Gianluca Fuwa,Christian Hoelbling,Lukas Varnhorst*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper addresses the issue of topological freezing in lattice QCD and 4D SU(N) gauge theories by employing Metadynamics with bias potentials to reduce autocorrelation times. It explores strategies for accelerating the creation of these potentials, their extrapolation across different volumes, and examines other algorithmic improvements like extended HMC trajectories and variants.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to overcome the challenge of topological freezing in certain theoretical models, which hinders the efficiency of conventional update algorithms due to high action barriers between topological sectors.

Method: The authors use Metadynamics and related enhanced sampling techniques with tailored bias potentials to facilitate transitions between topological sectors, test methods to speed up the formation of these potentials, and explore the scalability from smaller to larger system sizes. They also investigate additional algorithmic enhancements such as longer HMC (Hybrid Monte Carlo) trajectories and variations of the HMC method.

Result: The results indicate that Metadynamics with appropriate bias potentials can significantly decrease the integrated autocorrelation times of topological charge and related observables, and that there are effective ways to accelerate the generation of bias potentials and to extrapolate them to larger systems. The study also suggests that other algorithmic modifications, such as using longer HMC trajectories and HMC variants, can further improve the simulation efficiency.

Conclusion: The paper concludes that Metadynamics and associated enhanced sampling methods, along with specific algorithmic adjustments, provide a promising approach to overcoming topological freezing, thereby improving the computational efficiency and accuracy of simulations in theories with topological sectors.

Abstract: In theories with topological sectors, such as lattice QCD and four-dimensional SU(N) gauge theories with periodic boundary conditions, conventional update algorithms suffer from topological freezing due to large action barriers separating distinct sectors. With appropriately constructed bias potentials, Metadynamics and related enhanced sampling techniques can mitigate this problem and significantly reduce the integrated autocorrelation times of the topological charge and associated observables. We test strategies to accelerate the buildup of bias potentials and the possibility of extrapolating potentials from small to large volumes. We also investigate the effectiveness of orthogonal algorithmic improvements, such as longer HMC trajectories and HMC variants, which may benefit conventional simulations as well.

### [3] [Taste-splitting mass and edge modes in $3+1$~D staggered fermions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02078)
*Tatsuhiro Misumi,Tetsuya Onogi,Tatsuya Yamaoka*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores the symmetry structure of the 3+1D staggered fermion Hamiltonian and its implications for anomalies, identifying various bilinear mass terms and their preserved symmetries. It highlights a one-link mass term that preserves a larger residual symmetry related to the Onsager algebra, and through a kink profile analysis, demonstrates the emergence of two-flavor massless Dirac fermions on a 2+1D domain wall, with the realization of the parity anomaly in the boundary theory.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the symmetry structure of the 3+1D staggered fermion Hamiltonian and how it affects anomalies, particularly focusing on the different types of local, charge-conserving mass terms and their implications for the system's symmetries and the resulting physical phenomena.

Method: The authors identify the spin-flavor structures of the bilinear mass terms in the staggered fermion system and determine which symmetries are preserved by each. They then analyze a kink profile of a one-link mass term that preserves a larger symmetry, leading to a gapped 3+1D bulk and the appearance of massless Dirac fermions on a 2+1D domain wall. The low-energy limit and the action of bulk conserved charges on the domain wall are also examined.

Result: The results show that one of the one-link mass terms preserves a larger residual symmetry, which is associated with the Onsager algebra. In the presence of a kink profile of this mass term, the 3+1D bulk becomes gapped, and two-flavor massless Dirac fermions emerge as localized modes on the 2+1D domain wall. The flavor SU(2) symmetry and space reflection symmetry at the boundary do not allow for a symmetric mass gap, indicating the presence of a parity anomaly in the boundary theory.

Conclusion: The study concludes that the parity anomaly in the 2+1D boundary theory, along with the boundary flavor symmetry, originates from the ultraviolet staggered-fermion Hamiltonian rather than being an emergent property of the infrared physics, providing a deeper understanding of the connection between the bulk and boundary theories in the context of lattice fermions.

Abstract: We investigate the symmetry structure of the $3+1$ D staggered fermion Hamiltonian and its implications for anomalies. Since the spin and flavor degrees of freedom of Dirac fermions are distributed over the lattice, in addition to the standard on-site mass term, the staggered fermion system also admits one-, two-, and three-link bilinear terms within a unit cube as local, charge conserving mass terms with different spin and flavor dependence. We identify the spin flavor structures of all those bilinear mass terms and determine the symmetries preserved by each of them. Among them, one of the one-link mass terms preserves a larger residual symmetry associated with conserved charges that generate the Onsager algebra. Motivated by this structure, we consider a kink profile of the one-link mass and analyze the resulting domain-wall system. In the low-energy limit, the $3+1$ D bulk becomes gapped, while two-flavor massless Dirac fermions appear as localized modes on the $2+1$ D domain wall. We show that the bulk conserved charges act on the wall as generators of a flavor $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ symmetry, and that no symmetric mass gap is allowed for the boundary theory when this $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ symmetry and space reflection symmetry are both imposed. This realizes the parity anomaly of the boundary theory and shows that the boundary flavor symmetry and anomaly descend from the ultraviolet staggered-fermion Hamiltonian rather than emerging only in the infrared.

### [4] [Non-perturbative Renormalization of the EMT in Full QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02122)
*Pavan,Olaf Kaczmarek,Guy D. Moore,Christian Schmidt*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper discusses the construction and renormalization of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) for lattice QCD, focusing on its application in determining shear viscosity coefficients. It also compares several thermodynamic quantities at different β values with an imaginary chemical potential, aiming for a continuum limit.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to address the challenges in extending the well-defined EMT from the continuum to lattice QCD, especially in the context of broken space-time symmetries due to the discrete nature of the lattice. The aim is to properly define and renormalize the EMT for use in calculating transport coefficients like shear viscosity and to compare thermodynamic properties under specific conditions.

Method: The authors construct the EMT for both pure-gauge theory and full QCD on the lattice, with a focus on the renormalization process necessary for determining the coefficients relevant to shear viscosity. They conduct a comparative analysis of thermodynamic quantities such as the trace anomaly, number density, pressure, energy density, and enthalpy density using an imaginary chemical potential across multiple β values at approximately the same temperature.

Result: The results include the successful construction and renormalization of the EMT for lattice QCD, which allows for the determination of shear viscosity coefficients. Additionally, the comparison of thermodynamic quantities provides insights into their behavior at different β values with an imaginary chemical potential, contributing to the understanding of the system's approach to the continuum limit.

Conclusion: The conclusion emphasizes the importance of the constructed EMT for future studies in lattice QCD, particularly in the calculation of transport coefficients and the study of thermodynamic properties. The work paves the way for more accurate determinations of physical observables and a better understanding of the transition to the continuum limit.

Abstract: The energy-momentum tensor (EMT) is the conserved current corresponding to space-time translation symmetry. Its applications are remarkably diverse, ranging from the thermodynamics to the calculation of transport coefficients. While the EMT is well-defined in the continuum up to a total derivative, with its coefficients fixed by Ward identities, its extension to lattice QCD is not straightforward. The primary challenge arises from the breaking of continuous space-time symmetries by the discrete lattice regulator. Although the EMT can be constructed on the lattice in a way that yields the correct continuum limit, the operators are not uniquely defined. In this proceeding, we construct the EMT for both pure-gauge theory and full QCD, discussing its renormalization in the specific context of determining the coefficients required for shear viscosity. In this context, we present a comparative analysis of the trace anomaly, number density, pressure, energy density and enthalpy density with imaginary chemical potential for multiple $β$ values at approximately the same temperature, aimed for the continuum limit.
