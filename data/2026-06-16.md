### [1] [Spatial String Tension at High Temperatures and Quantitative Tests of Dimensionally Reduced Effective Theories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15564)
*Vishal Rao,Peter Petreczky,Prasad Hegde*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper calculates the spatial string tension in 2+1 flavor QCD across a temperature range using specific methods and compares the results with effective theories to examine dimensional reduction.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to test the onset of dimensional reduction in QCD by comparing the calculated spatial string tension with predictions from dimensionally reduced effective theories (EQCD and MQCD).

Method: The authors use spatial Wilson loops with HYP smearing, the Highly Improved Staggered Quark action for fermions, and the tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action for gluons, at two lattice spacings corresponding to different temporal extents, to calculate the spatial string tension in 2+1 flavor QCD over a specified temperature range.

Result: The results of the spatial string tension calculations are obtained within the given temperature range and compared against the expectations from EQCD and MQCD, providing insights into the validity of dimensional reduction in QCD at high temperatures.

Conclusion: The conclusion would summarize the findings regarding the agreement or discrepancy between the calculated spatial string tension and the predictions from the effective theories, thereby commenting on the onset of dimensional reduction in 2+1 flavor QCD.

Abstract: We calculate the spatial string tension in 2+1 flavour QCD in $(3{+}1)$ dimensions within a temperature range of $[166\,\mathrm{MeV},\,1000\,\mathrm{MeV}]$ using spatial Wilson loops with HYP smearing. We use the Highly Improved Staggered Quark action for fermions and the tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action for gluons at two lattice spacings corresponding to temporal extents $N_τ= 8$ and $10$. We then compare our results with dimensionally reduced effective theories at high temperatures (EQCD and MQCD) to test the onset of dimensional reduction in QCD.

### [2] [Learning the generating functional for variance reduction in lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15986)
*Ryan Abbott,Yang Fu,Daniel C. Hackett,Gurtej Kanwar,Fernando Romero-López,Phiala E. Shanahan*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper introduces a method using machine-learned normalizing flows to significantly reduce the variance of correlation functions in lattice gauge field theory, with applications in Quantum Chromodynamics and Yang-Mills theory demonstrating up to three orders of magnitude improvement.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to improve the efficiency and accuracy of calculations in quantum field theory, particularly for N-point correlation functions of bosonic operators, by reducing their variance through a novel approach that utilizes machine learning.

Method: The authors employ machine-learned normalizing flows to encode a representation of the generating functional, which allows them to systematically decrease the noise in estimators of correlation functions within lattice gauge field theory calculations.

Result: The application of this methodology to glueball correlation functions and Wilson loops in both Quantum Chromodynamics and Yang-Mills theory resulted in a variance reduction of up to three orders of magnitude, indicating a substantial enhancement in the precision of these calculations.

Conclusion: The proposed method, based on machine-learned normalizing flows, effectively reduces the variance of correlation function estimators, leading to more precise results in quantum field theory computations and potentially enabling more complex or accurate studies in the future.

Abstract: The generating functional in quantum field theory provides the natural framework for constructing correlation functions as derivatives with respect to source operators. We present a methodology that leverages machine-learned normalizing flows to reduce the variance of arbitrary $N$-point correlation functions of bosonic operators in lattice gauge field theory calculations by encoding a representation of the generating functional. We show that it is possible to systematically approach noiseless estimators of correlation functions in this framework. We demonstrate this methodology with applications to calculations of glueball correlation functions and Wilson loops in Quantum Chromodynamics and Yang-Mills theory. The results show up to three orders of magnitude variance reduction.

### [3] [Direct calculation of parton distributions in momentum space from lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16877)
*Anthony V. Grebe,Daniel C. Hackett,Michael L. Wagman,Rui Zhang,Yong Zhao*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a method to compute Coulomb-gauge quasi-parton distributions in momentum space on a finite lattice, which overcomes the inverse problem in coordinate-space methods. The authors also extend their framework to higher dimensions and produce the first 3D image of the pion from lattice QCD.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop an alternative approach for computing quasi-parton distributions that avoids the formal inverse problem present in coordinate-space methods and to extend the computation to higher dimensions to gain a more detailed understanding of the pion structure.

Method: The researchers use a momentum-space approach for calculating Coulomb-gauge quasi-parton distributions on a finite lattice, leveraging the commutativity of renormalization and Fourier transform. They also extend this method to three dimensions to obtain a 3D image of the pion structure.

Result: The momentum-space pion quasi-distributions are found to be consistent with the results obtained by Fourier transforming the coordinate-space data, suggesting that the inverse problem is not a significant issue at the current volume. Additionally, they successfully produced the first 3D image of the pion from lattice QCD calculations.

Conclusion: The new momentum-space approach provides a viable alternative for computing quasi-parton distributions and has been validated against existing coordinate-space methods. The extension to 3D imaging offers a novel perspective on the internal structure of the pion.

Abstract: Coulomb-gauge quasi-parton distributions can be computed directly in momentum space on a finite lattice, enabled by the commutativity of their renormalization and Fourier transform. This approach removes the formal inverse problem in coordinate-space methods. Our momentum-space pion quasi-distributions agree with coordinate-space results Fourier transformed with asymptotic extrapolation, indicating that the formal inverse problem in the latter is not a concern at this volume. We further extend the framework to higher dimensions and obtain the first 3D image of the pion directly from lattice QCD.

</details>
