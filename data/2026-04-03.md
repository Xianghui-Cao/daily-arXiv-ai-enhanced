### [1] [Tackling inverse problems for PDFs from lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01996)
*Alexander Rothkopf*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The presentation ties together recent progress in extracting parton distribution functions (PDFs) in lattice QCD with efforts in solving the inverse problem through spectral function reconstruction.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To connect and highlight the advancements in understanding parton distribution functions within the context of lattice QCD, alongside the ongoing work on the inverse problem related to spectral function reconstruction.

Method: The method involves a review and synthesis of recent research findings in lattice QCD regarding PDFs, and their relation to the techniques used for spectral function reconstruction as part of the inverse problem.

Result: The result is an overview that bridges the gap between the latest developments in PDF extraction and the traditional challenges associated with the inverse problem in QCD.

Conclusion: The conclusion emphasizes the importance of integrating knowledge from both areas to further advance our understanding of QCD and its applications.

Abstract: In this kick-off presentation for the "Recent developments in QCD" session at Baryons 2025 I will tie together the recent progress made on the extraction of parton distribution functions (PDFs) in lattice QCD and the long standing efforts in solving the inverse problem in the form of spectral function reconstruction.

### [2] [Taste-splitting mass and edge modes in $3+1$~D staggered fermions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02078)
*Tatsuhiro Misumi,Tetsuya Onogi,Tatsuya Yamaoka*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores the symmetry of the 3+1D staggered fermion Hamiltonian and its implications for anomalies, finding that a specific one-link mass term preserves a larger residual symmetry. It demonstrates the emergence of two-flavor massless Dirac fermions on a 2+1D domain wall and shows that the boundary flavor symmetry and anomaly originate from the UV staggered-fermion Hamiltonian.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the symmetry structure of the 3+1D staggered fermion Hamiltonian and how it relates to anomalies, particularly in the context of additional bilinear terms that can be introduced due to the distribution of spin and flavor degrees of freedom over the lattice.

Method: The authors analyze the symmetries preserved by different bilinear mass terms within the unit cube of the staggered fermion system, focusing on the one-link mass term that preserves a larger residual symmetry. They then consider a kink profile of this one-link mass and study the resulting domain-wall system, showing the emergence of massless Dirac fermions on the 2+1D domain wall and their behavior under conserved charges.

Result: The result is the identification of the spin-flavor structures of the bilinear mass terms, the determination of the preserved symmetries, and the demonstration that the parity anomaly of the boundary theory originates from the ultraviolet staggered-fermion Hamiltonian. The bulk conserved charges act as generators of an SU(2) flavor symmetry on the 2+1D domain wall, which cannot support a symmetric mass gap when both the SU(2) and space reflection symmetries are imposed.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the boundary flavor symmetry and anomaly, which are typically considered as infrared phenomena, actually descend from the ultraviolet structure of the staggered-fermion Hamiltonian, suggesting a deeper connection between the UV and IR physics of such systems.

Abstract: We investigate the symmetry structure of the $3+1$ D staggered fermion Hamiltonian and its implications for anomalies. Since the spin and flavor degrees of freedom of Dirac fermions are distributed over the lattice, in addition to the standard on-site mass term, the staggered fermion system also admits one-, two-, and three-link bilinear terms within a unit cube as local, charge conserving mass terms with different spin and flavor dependence. We identify the spin flavor structures of all those bilinear mass terms and determine the symmetries preserved by each of them. Among them, one of the one-link mass terms preserves a larger residual symmetry associated with conserved charges that generate the Onsager algebra. Motivated by this structure, we consider a kink profile of the one-link mass and analyze the resulting domain-wall system. In the low-energy limit, the $3+1$ D bulk becomes gapped, while two-flavor massless Dirac fermions appear as localized modes on the $2+1$ D domain wall. We show that the bulk conserved charges act on the wall as generators of a flavor $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ symmetry, and that no symmetric mass gap is allowed for the boundary theory when this $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ symmetry and space reflection symmetry are both imposed. This realizes the parity anomaly of the boundary theory and shows that the boundary flavor symmetry and anomaly descend from the ultraviolet staggered-fermion Hamiltonian rather than emerging only in the infrared.

</details>
