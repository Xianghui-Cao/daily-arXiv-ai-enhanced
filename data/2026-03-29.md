### [1] [Heavy quark masses from step-scaling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25295)
*Simon Kuberski,Alessandro Conigli,Patrick Fritzsch,Antoine Gérardin,Jochen Heitger,Gregorio Herdoíza,Carlos Pena,Hubert Simma,Rainer Sommer*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a method for determining the masses of charm and bottom quarks using a heavy-quark step-scaling strategy, with renormalization in small volumes allowing direct simulation of relativistic bottom quarks. The method connects to large-volume simulations for reliable control over low-energy hadronic physics, achieving good precision with systematic uncertainties that complement those from traditional large-volume methods.

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a new and complementary approach to determining the masses of charm- and bottom-quark with potentially different and subdominant systematic uncertainties compared to standard large-volume determinations.

Method: The method involves the use of a heavy-quark step-scaling strategy, which includes renormalization in small volumes where relativistic bottom quarks can be simulated directly. A sequence of finite-volume simulations is used to connect this calculation to large-volume CLS ensembles, where light and strange quark masses are at their physical values. The B-scale is reached by interpolating between relativistic heavy-quark data and the static limit, except in the smallest volume.

Result: The result is the determination of charm- and bottom-quark masses with good precision and a set of systematic uncertainties that differ from and complement those of standard large-volume determinations.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the heavy-quark step-scaling strategy provides a viable and precise way to determine the charm- and bottom-quark masses, offering a method with distinct systematic uncertainties that can be used alongside traditional large-volume calculations.

Abstract: We present a determination of the charm- and bottom-quark masses using the heavy-quark step-scaling strategy. Renormalization is performed in small volumes where relativistic bottom quarks can be simulated directly. A sequence of finite-volume simulations connects this calculation to large-volume CLS ensembles, where simulations at physical light and strange quark masses provide reliable control over low-energy hadronic physics. In all but the smallest volume, the B-scale is reached by interpolating between relativistic heavy-quark data and the static limit. The resulting quark masses are obtained with good precision, with subdominant systematic uncertainties that differ from, and thus complement, those of standard large-volume determinations.
