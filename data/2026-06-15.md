### [1] [Stochastic Path Sampler For Lattice Field Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13790)
*Shiyang Chen,Moxian Qian,Gert Aarts,Biagio Lucini,Kai Zhou*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A new Stochastic Path Sampler (SPS) is introduced for lattice field theory, which improves sampling efficiency near phase transitions or the continuum limit. SPS uses a variational free-energy principle to enhance process reversibility and provides high-quality sampling with shorter autocorrelation times compared to HMC in 2D φ^4 theory.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to address the inefficiency of Markov chain Monte Carlo simulations when dealing with critical slowing down near phase transitions or the continuum limit in lattice field theory. The goal is to create a more efficient sampler that can work with unnormalized target distributions without the need for training data.

Method: The method involves developing a Stochastic Path Sampler (SPS) based on nonequilibrium thermodynamics. It establishes a balance between prior and target distributions by minimizing the path-space variational free energy, which enhances the reversibility of the processes. The forward process generates independent proposals, and an Independence Metropolis-Hastings step corrects them.

Result: In 2D φ^4 theory, the SPS demonstrates comparable sampling quality to Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC), but with significantly reduced autocorrelation time in the critical region, indicating a more efficient sampling process.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the SPS offers a promising, data-free approach to constructing efficient proposal mechanisms for lattice field theory, inspired by stochastic quantization and driven by a variational free-energy principle, which is particularly effective in the critical region.

Abstract: In lattice field theory, target distributions are known only up to normalization, (\tildeπ(φ)\propto e^{-S(φ)}), while the partition function is intractable. Markov chain Monte Carlo simulations often become inefficient near phase transitions or the continuum limit due to critical slowing down. In this work, we propose a novel sampler based on nonequilibrium thermodynamics, called Stochastic Path Sampler (SPS), which can generate configurations for the unnormalized target distribution without requiring training data. The central idea of SPS is to establish a trajectory-level balance for learnable forward and backward stochastic dynamics between two equilibrium states, namely the prior and target distributions. This is achieved by minimizing the path-space variational free energy, equivalently an entropy-production upper bound, defined by the log-ratio of forward and auxiliary backward trajectory measures, thereby enhancing the reversibility of the forward and backward processes. The learned forward process provides independent proposals, which are subsequently corrected by an extended-space Independence Metropolis--Hastings step. In two-dimensional (φ^4) theory, we demonstrate that our neural sampler can achieve the same sampling quality as HMC but with a much shorter autocorrelation time in the critical region. This sampler offers a stochastic-quantization-inspired route to data-free proposal construction for lattice field theory by leveraging a variational free-energy principle derived from path-space irreversibility.

### [2] [Hadron spectra of finite-density QC$_2$D](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13974)
*Kei Iida,Etsuko Itou,Kotaro Murakami,Daiki Suenaga*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper examines the chemical-potential dependence of hadron spectra in two-color QCD using lattice simulations, finding a mass hierarchy in the hadronic and superfluid phases, and indications of chiral symmetry restoration at high density.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To investigate the chemical-potential dependence of hadron spectra in two-color QCD, including contributions from disconnected diagrams, and to explore the implications for the mass hierarchy and chiral symmetry restoration at high density.

Method: First-principles lattice simulations were used to compute two-point correlation functions for all allowed hadronic operators, with the inclusion of disconnected diagrams. The effective masses were extracted from these correlators, and the behavior of mesons and diquarks was analyzed in both the hadronic (normal vacuum) and superfluid phases, as well as across different densities.

Result: In the hadronic phase, a mass hierarchy similar to that in three-color QCD was found, while in the superfluid phase, this hierarchy changed with increasing density. The Nambu-Goldstone mode associated with spontaneous breaking of $U(1)_B$ was confirmed to be nearly massless, and by comparing correlators for chiral partners, there were indications of chiral symmetry restoration at high density.

Conclusion: The research provides insights into the hadron spectra and their changes with chemical potential in two-color QCD, indicating a modification of the mass hierarchy in the superfluid phase and the presence of chiral symmetry restoration at high density.

Abstract: We investigate the chemical-potential dependence of hadron spectra in two-color QCD using first-principles lattice simulations. We compute two-point correlation functions for all allowed hadronic operators by newly including the contributions from disconnected diagrams, and extract the corresponding effective masses. In the meson sector, the mass hierarchy in the hadronic phase (normal vacuum) is found to be $m_π\lesssim m_η < m_σ\mathrm{(noisy)} < m_ρ\sim m_ω\ll m_{a_1}$, which is similar to that in three-color QCD. In the superfluid phase, this hierarchy is modified, and with increasing density it changes to $m_σ\mathrm{(noisy)} < m_{a_1} < m_ρ< m_π\sim m_η \mathrm{(noisy)} \ll m_ω \mathrm{(noisy)}$. In the diquark sector, the ordering remains as $m_{NG} \lesssim m_{I=0, S} < m_{I=1, AV} < m_{I=0, PS} \lesssim m_{I=0, V}$ in both phases, and the Nambu--Goldstone mode associated with spontaneous breaking of $U(1)_B$ is confirmed to be nearly massless. Furthermore, by comparing correlators for chiral partners, we find indications of chiral symmetry restoration at high density.

### [3] [Finite-volume effects on smeared spectral densities](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14349)
*Francesca A. Bresciani,Mattia Bruno,Maxwell T. Hansen*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper derives a universal expression for the leading finite-volume effects of the smeared vector-vector spectral density in a periodic cubic spatial volume, which can be useful for controlling the infinite volume extrapolation. The derivation is done using two distinct approaches and may be applicable to other cases.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a reliable method for estimating and controlling the finite-volume effects when extrapolating smeared spectral densities to an infinite volume, which is important for lattice QCD calculations.

Method: Two distinct approaches are used: 1) building on previous work for finite-volume effects on Euclidean two-point functions, and 2) starting from the Lellouch-Lüscher-Meyer expression for the spectral decomposition of the correlator. Both methods lead to a universal representation of the leading finite-volume effects in terms of the pion form factor.

Result: The results show that the finite-volume effects are exponentially suppressed with certain smearing kernels, and the leading effects have a universal form. The study also provides numerical estimates based on various kernels and interaction models, suggesting a scaling regime where the finite-volume effects can be reliably estimated.

Conclusion: The derived universal expression for the leading finite-volume effects can help in controlling the infinite volume extrapolation of smeared spectral densities, and it establishes a general framework that could be applied to other channels beyond the vector channel.

Abstract: Using two distinct approaches, we derive a universal expression for the leading finite-volume effects of the smeared vector-vector spectral density (proportional to the smeared hadronic $R$-ratio) in a periodic cubic spatial volume of side length $L$. First, building on the results of previous work for finite-volume effects on Euclidean two-point functions, we show that the $L$ dependence is exponentially suppressed for a certain class of smearing kernels, and that the leading effects can be expressed universally in terms of the pion form factor. The same representation is then derived starting from the Lellouch-Lüscher-Meyer expression for the spectral decomposition of the correlator. The results may prove useful for controlling the $L \to \infty$ extrapolation of smeared spectral densities, in particular by defining a scaling regime in which the finite-volume effects are dominated by the leading terms in a large $L$ expansion and thus can be reliably estimated. To illustrate this point, we also present numerical estimates based on various kernels and models of particle interactions. Despite focusing on the vector channel, our derivation defines a general framework applicable to other cases as well.

### [4] [Electric charge fluctuations from lattice QCD in the continuum limit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14491)
*Szabolcs Borsányi,Zoltán Fodor,Jana N. Guenther,Paolo Parotto,Attila Pásztor,Claudia Ratti,Volodymyr Vovchenko,Chik Him Wong*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study measures electric charge fluctuations, χ2^Q and for the first time, χ4^Q in the continuum limit using a 4HEX action. It finds discrepancies with the HRG model that cannot be fully reconciled by incorporating light meson interactions. A new measurement at LHC is proposed to further investigate.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To compare theory and experiment, it is necessary to measure electric charge fluctuations (χn^Q), which have been challenging to obtain on the lattice due to significant cutoff effects. The motivation is to accurately measure these fluctuations, specifically χ2^Q and χ4^Q, and to reconcile them with theoretical models, such as the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, or to propose a new experimental approach if discrepancies persist.

Method: A 4HEX action is utilized to calculate the electric charge fluctuations χ2^Q and, for the first time, χ4^Q in the continuum limit. The researchers then compare these results with the predictions of the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. They also attempt to account for the observed discrepancies by including light meson interactions in the HRG model through the S-matrix formalism.

Result: The research finds that there is disagreement between the calculated charge fluctuations and the HRG model, which remains even after considering finite volume effects. When light meson interactions are included in the HRG model, the agreement for χ4^Q improves, but the agreement for χ2^Q worsens. The authors suggest measuring the ratio χ4^Q/χ2^Q at the LHC to explore this discrepancy further.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that while the 4HEX action has allowed for the calculation of χ2^Q and the first-time calculation of χ4^Q in the continuum limit, the results do not align well with the HRG model, even after adjustments. This suggests that either the HRG model requires further refinement or that there may be additional physics at play. The proposed LHC measurement could provide insights into the source of the tension.

Abstract: Electric charge fluctuations $χ_n^Q$ allow comparisons between theory and experiment, but are elusive on the lattice due to severe cutoff effects. We use a 4HEX action to obtain $χ_2^Q$ and, for the first time ever, $χ_4^Q$ in the continuum limit. We find disagreement with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, which we cannot explain with finite volume effects. We include light meson interactions in the HRG model via the S-matrix, reducing the tension for $χ_4^Q$, but worsening the agreement for $χ_2^Q$. We propose measuring the ratio $χ_4^Q/χ_2^Q$ at the LHC to investigate this tension.

### [6] [Zeros of the partition function for 12 flavor QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14642)
*Anas Saleh,Michael Hite,Diego Floor,Yannick Meurice*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study investigates a 4D SU(3) lattice gauge theory with 12 staggered fermions, using the Ferrenberg-Swendsen method to reconstruct the density of states and calculate partition function zeros. It supports the hypothesis of a line of first-order transitions ending at a second-order phase transition in the 4D Ising universality class, and suggests a scaling relation for the gap with the real axis.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To investigate the nature of phase transitions in a 4D $SU(3)$ lattice gauge theory with 12 staggered fermions and to determine if there is a line of first-order transitions that ends at a second-order phase transition, which is expected to be in the 4D Ising, mean field, universality class.

Method: The researchers used the Ferrenberg-Swendsen method to reconstruct the density of states from plaquette distributions and calculated the zeros of the partition function in the complex $\beta$ plane for various bare quark masses and hypercube sizes. They analyzed the $L$ dependence of the zeros and fitted it with two and three-parameter models to support their hypothesis about the phase transition.

Result: For $m_q = 0.02$, the results strongly indicate a first-order phase transition, while for other masses, the evidence suggests they are above the critical mass $m_q^c$. The infinite volume gap for the lowest zero was found to scale as $a \simeq A(m_q - m_q^c)^B$ with $m_q^c \sim 0.05$ and $B \sim 1$. The Lee-Yang edge was found to scale roughly like $m_\sigma^2$, where $m_\sigma$ is the mass of the $0^{++}$ scalar particle.

Conclusion: The analysis provides strong support for the existence of a line of first-order transitions in the $(m_q, \beta)$ plane, terminating at a second-order phase transition in the 4D Ising universality class. The scaling of the gap with the real axis (Lee-Yang edge) is consistent with previous spectroscopic studies.

Abstract: We consider a four dimensional $SU(3)$ lattice gauge theory with 12 staggered fermions having identical masses and an unimproved action. Using sets of plaquette distributions for various inverse bare couplings $β$, we reconstruct the density of states with the Ferrenberg -Swendsen method and calculate the zeros of the partition in the complex $β$ plane with bare quark masses $m_q$ = 0.02, 0.06, 0.08 and 0.1 for hypercubes of linear size $L$= 4, 6, 8, 10, and 12. Our hypothesis is that there is a line of first order transitions in the $(m_q,β)$ plane ending at a second order phase transition. We expect this transition to be in the 4D Ising, mean field, universality class. We fit the $L$ dependence of the zeros with the lowest imaginary part using two ($y = bL^{-d}$) and three ($y = a + bL^{-d}$) parameter fits. For $m_q$ = 0.02 the results provide strong support for a first order phase transition ($d=3.98(6)$, and $a$ statistically compatible with 0). The results also indicate, with less statistical significance for $m_q=0.06$, that the three other masses are above the critical value $m_q^c$. In addition, we suggest that the infinite volume gap for the lowest zero $a$, can be represented as $a\simeq A(m_q-m_q^c)^{B}$ with $m_q^c\sim 0.05$ and $B\sim 1$. Given that there are only three data points with significant error bars, it is difficult to rule out the mean field value $B=3/2$. Combining this result with spectroscopic results by Jin and Mawhinney, indicates that the gap with real axis (Lee-Yang edge) scales roughly like $m_σ^2$, where $m_σ$ is the mass of the $0^{++}$ scalar which is also the lowest excitation.

</details>
