### [1] [An efficient Wavelet-Based Hamiltonian Formulation of Quantum Field Theories using Flow-Equations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14594)
*Mrinmoy Basak,Debsubhra Chakraborty,Nilmani Mathur*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper introduces an effective Hamiltonian formulation for quantum field theories, using Daubechies wavelets and similarity renormalization group (SRG) flow-equation methods to reduce dimensionality and decouple degrees of freedom. This approach is applied to the free scalar field theory, transforming it into a system of coupled localized oscillators, and enabling the calculation of the low-lying energy spectrum with reduced computational cost by focusing on the lowest-resolution block of the Hamiltonian.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop an efficient framework for analyzing quantum field theories that can reduce the complexity of the problem by lowering the dimensionality of the Hamiltonian and systematically separating out the degrees of freedom at different scales, which would allow for more manageable calculations of the physical properties of the theories, such as the energy spectrum.

Method: The method involves formulating the quantum field theories with an effective Hamiltonian using a Daubechies wavelet basis in position space, and then applying the flow-equation methods from the similarity renormalization group (SRG) to decouple the degrees of freedom associated with different resolutions. The free scalar field theory is used as a case study, where the Hamiltonian is reorganized into fixed-resolution blocks and interaction blocks, and SRG is used to achieve a block-diagonalized Hamiltonian.

Result: The result is a reformulation of the free scalar field theory into a set of coupled localized oscillators, with the Hamiltonian organized into resolution-specific blocks. By applying the SRG flow equation, the authors are able to decouple the different resolution modes and obtain the low-lying energy spectrum from the effective lowest-resolution block, which significantly reduces the computational resources required for the calculations.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from this work is that the combination of Daubechies wavelet basis and SRG flow-equation methods provides an effective way to analyze quantum field theories by simplifying the Hamiltonian structure and allowing for the extraction of the low-energy spectrum with increased efficiency, as demonstrated through the application to the free scalar field theory.

Abstract: We propose an effective Hamiltonian formulation of quantum field theories using a Daubechies wavelet basis in position space. Combined with flow-equation methods of the similarity renormalization group (SRG), this approach provides an efficient framework for analyzing quantum field theories by reducing the dimensionality of the Hamiltonian and systematically decoupling degrees of freedom across scales. As an application, the free scalar field theory has been reformulated within this framework to calculate the low-lying energy spectrum of the theory. These basis elements are known to transform the free scalar field theory into a theory of coupled localized oscillators, each of which is labeled by a location and a resolution index. In this representation, the Hamiltonian is naturally organized into fixed-resolution blocks, alongside blocks associated with the interactions between different resolutions. To decouple the different resolution modes and obtain a block diagonalized Hamiltonian with each block associated with a fixed resolution, the flow equation approach of SRG is applied. Finally, we demonstrate that with increasing resolution, the low-energy spectrum can be extracted from the effective lowest-resolution block of the Hamiltonian, leading to a significant reduction in computational cost.

### [2] [A minimal implementation of Yang-Mills theory on a digital quantum computer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15132)
*Georg Bergner,Masanori Hanada,Emanuele Mendicelli*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a minimal implementation of SU(N) pure Yang-Mills theory in 3+1 dimensions for digital quantum simulation, which is designed to enable quantum advantage with logarithmic scaling and simplified Hamiltonians. It also introduces methods to improve convergence to the infinite mass limit and uses Monte Carlo simulations to validate the accuracy.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop a practical framework for the quantum simulation of non-Abelian gauge theories that can achieve quantum advantage, by minimizing resource requirements and improving the theoretical models used in simulations.

Method: The method involves the use of an orbifold lattice simulation protocol with logarithmic scaling, further simplifying the Hamiltonians, and testing new methods to improve the convergence to the infinite mass limit. For the SU(2) case, the method takes advantage of the embedding of SU(2) into R^4. The accuracy of these improvements is benchmarked using Monte Carlo simulations of the Euclidean path integral.

Result: The results show that the proposed methods can reduce resource requirements and improve the convergence to the infinite mass limit, thus supporting the noncompact-variable-based approach as a viable framework for simulating non-Abelian gauge theories on a quantum computer.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the introduced simplifications and analytical improvements to the SU(N) pure Yang-Mills theory, along with the validation through Monte Carlo simulations, provide a more efficient and accurate framework for quantum simulation, advancing the field towards achieving quantum advantage.

Abstract: We present a minimal implementation of SU($N$) pure Yang-Mills theory in $3+1$ dimensions for digital quantum simulation, designed to enable quantum advantage. Building on the orbifold lattice simulation protocol with logarithmic scaling in the local Hilbert-space truncation, we introduce further simplified Hamiltonians. Furthermore, we test simple methods that improve the convergence to the infinite mass limit, thereby removing the requirement of a large scalar mass to obtain the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian. For the SU(2) theory, we can cut the resource requirement further by utilizing the embedding of $\mathrm{SU}(2)\cong\mathrm{S}^3$ into $\mathbb{R}^4$. Monte Carlo simulations of the Euclidean path integral were used to benchmark the accuracy of these new analytical improvements to the theory. These results provide further support for the noncompact-variable-based approach as a practical framework for quantum simulation of non-Abelian gauge theories.

### [3] [Charmonium radiative transitions to dileptons from lattice QCD: The case of $h_c \to η_c \ell^+\ell^-$ and $χ_{c1} \to J/ψ\,\ell^+\ell^-$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15256)
*D. Bečirević,R. Di Palma,R. Frezzotti,G. Gagliardi,V. Lubicz,F. Sanfilippo,N. Tantalo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents the first fully dynamical lattice QCD predictions for dilepton decay rates in $h_c$ and $χ_{c1}$ charmonium transitions, including differential distributions and angular observables, with results showing good compatibility with experimental data for $χ_{c1}$ decays but a discrepancy for the $h_c \to η_c e^+ e^-$ decay rate.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide benchmark predictions for future experimental studies by using lattice QCD to study dilepton production in charmonium transitions, specifically focusing on the $1^{+-} \to 0^{-+}$ and $1^{++} \to 1^{--}$ processes. The work aims to fill the gap in the literature by providing fully dynamical calculations at the physical quark masses and multiple lattice spacings, allowing for a controlled continuum extrapolation.

Method: The method involves computing hadronic matrix elements using gauge field configurations generated by the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration with $N_f = 2+1+1$ dynamical Wilson--Clover twisted-mass fermions at four different lattice spacings. The simulations are conducted with physical dynamical $u$, $d$, $s$, and $c$ quark masses, except for the coarsest lattice, where the lightest sea quark mass corresponds to a slightly heavier pion mass. A controlled continuum extrapolation is performed to obtain the final results.

Result: For the $h_c$ decays, the authors report $Γ(h_c \to η_c e^+ e^-) = 5.45(19)~\mathrm{keV}$ and $Γ(h_c \to η_c μ^+ μ^-) = 0.635(22)~\mathrm{keV}$. For the $χ_{c1}$ decays, they find $Γ(χ_{c1} \to J/ψe^+ e^-)= 2.869(90)~\mathrm{keV}$ and $Γ(χ_{c1} \to J/ψμ^+ μ^-) = 0.1993(72)~\mathrm{keV}$. The prediction for the $h_c \to η_c e^+ e^-$ decay rate is approximately $3σ$ larger than the BESIII result, while the $χ_{c1}$ decay results are in good agreement with experimental data. The paper also provides predictions for differential decay widths as functions of the dilepton invariant mass, $q^2$, and for angular observables.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from this work is that the lattice QCD predictions for dilepton decay rates in $h_c$ and $χ_{c1}$ charmonium transitions, including their differential distributions and angular observables, have been successfully calculated. These predictions serve as benchmarks for future experimental studies, although there is a notable discrepancy with the BESIII result for the $h_c \to η_c e^+ e^-$ decay, which may indicate new physics or require further investigation.

Abstract: We present a lattice QCD study of dilepton production in charmonium transitions, specifically focusing on the $1^{+-} \to 0^{-+}$ and $1^{++} \to 1^{--}$ processes: $h_c \to η_c \ell^+ \ell^-$ and $χ_{c1} \to J/ψ\ell^+ \ell^-$, where $\ell = e, μ$. The relevant hadronic matrix elements are computed using gauge field configurations generated by the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration with $N_f = 2+1+1$ dynamical Wilson--Clover twisted-mass fermions at four lattice spacings. Simulations are performed at physical dynamical $u$, $d$, $s$, and $c$ quark masses, except for the coarsest lattice, where the lightest sea quark mass corresponds to a slightly heavier pion mass. A controlled continuum extrapolation is carried out. In the continuum limit for the $h_c$ decays, we obtain $Γ(h_c \to η_c e^+ e^-) = 5.45(19)~\mathrm{keV}$, and $Γ(h_c \to η_c μ^+ μ^-) = 0.635(22)~\mathrm{keV}$. For the $χ_{c1}$ decays, we find: $Γ(χ_{c1} \to J/ψe^+ e^-)= 2.869(90)~\mathrm{keV}$, and $Γ(χ_{c1} \to J/ψμ^+ μ^-) = 0.1993(72)~\mathrm{keV}$. Our results for the $χ_{c1}$ decays show good compatibility with experimental data. However, our prediction for the $h_c \to η_c e^+ e^- $ decay rate is approximately $3σ$ larger than the BESIII result. We also present predictions for the differential decay widths as functions of the dilepton invariant mass, $q^2$, and for angular observables sensitive to longitudinal transition form factors, which are inaccessible in radiative decays with real photon emission. These results constitute the first fully dynamical lattice QCD predictions for dilepton decay rates in $h_c$ and $χ_{c1}$ charmonium transitions, including their differential distributions and angular observables. They provide benchmark predictions for future experimental studies.
