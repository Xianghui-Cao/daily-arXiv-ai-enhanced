### [1] [Quantum simulation of baryon scattering in SU(2) lattice gauge theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06716)
*João Barata,Juan Hormaza,Zhong-Bo Kang,Wenyang Qian*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores real-time hadronic scattering in a (1+1)-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory with fermions using tensor-network methods, revealing predominantly elastic dynamics for B=0 and B=2 baryon numbers, while the B=1 sector shows unique entanglement behavior.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to study hadronic scattering processes in a simplified model of QCD, which can provide insights into the non-perturbative regime of strong interactions, specifically focusing on the dynamics across different global baryon number sectors.

Method: The method involves the use of tensor-network techniques applied to the Hamiltonian formulation of the SU(2) lattice gauge theory, enabling the simulation of real-time scattering processes without the need for gauge fixing. The study analyzes local observables, entanglement entropy, and the information lattice to characterize the scattering events.

Result: Results show that at strong coupling, meson-meson (B=0) and baryon-baryon (B=2) collisions are predominantly elastic, whereas the meson-baryon (B=1) collisions result in entangled wavepackets with one state becoming delocalized and the other propagating ballistically.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the (1+1)-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory with fermions exhibits rich scattering dynamics, including novel entanglement phenomena in the mixed baryon number sector, providing a new perspective on hadronic interactions in the strong coupling limit.

Abstract: We present a first real-time study of hadronic scattering in a $(1+1)$-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory with fundamental fermions using tensor-network techniques. Working in the gaugeless Hamiltonian formulation, we investigate scattering processes across sectors of fixed global baryon number $B = 0, 1, 2$, corresponding respectively to meson--meson, meson--baryon, and baryon--baryon collisions. At strong coupling, the $B = 0$ and $B = 2$ channels exhibit predominantly elastic dynamics closely resembling the U(1) Schwinger model. The mixed $B = 1$ sector displays qualitatively new behavior: meson and baryon wavepackets become entangled during the collision, with the slower state becoming spatially delocalized while the faster one propagates ballistically. We characterize these processes through local observables, entanglement entropy, and the information lattice.

Abstract: We present the status of calculations of the form factors of the most relevant heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light decay channels. Using seven $N_f = 2+1+1$ HISQ ensembles, with lattice spacings ranging from 0.15 fm down to 0.06 fm, we calculate the form factors of the decays, including correlations among them. More than half of our ensembles feature physical pion masses, and the heavy quarks are simulated at their physical masses using the Wilson-clover action with the Fermilab interpretation. Even though we have recently seen huge qualitative and quantitative leaps in the characterization of heavy-to-heavy decays, these advances have failed to translate into improvements for the inclusive vs exclusive question, or the matter of the Lepton Flavor Universality ratios. In particular, in the $B\to D^{\ast}\ellν$ channel, the current situation of the lattice-QCD form factors is far from clear. Further, the latest lattice-QCD results on the heavy-to-light form factors display unexplained tensions that must urgently be resolved. The work presented here is an attempt to address these issues.

*Julian Mayer-Steudte*

Main category: hep-lat

Motivation: The motivation is to overcome the poor signal-to-noise ratio of Wilson loops at large Euclidean times when extracting the static quark-antiquark potential from lattice QCD. The aim is to improve the overlap with the ground state and to find excited states more efficiently.

Method: A method using neural-network parametrization of trial states, constructed with gauge-equivariant layers, is introduced. These networks are optimized through a loss function designed to favor both ground and excited states.

Result: The approach allows for automatic acquisition of interpolators for the ground and excited states within the quenched theory.

Conclusion: The new method provides a promising way to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of identifying both ground and excited states in the context of lattice QCD.

Abstract: The extraction of the static quark-antiquark potential from lattice QCD suffers from the poor signal-to-noise ratio of Wilson loops at large Euclidean times. To overcome this, smearing methods or the Coulomb gauge are used to improve the ground-state overlap with respect to the straight Wilson line trial state within the Wilson loop. To find excited states, complicated shapes are introduced to generate specific quantum numbers. Here, we introduce a neural-network parametrization of trial states, constructed with gauge-equivariant layers and optimized with a loss function that favors ground and excited states. In the quenched theory, we automatically obtain the interpolators for the ground and excited states.

### [6] [The Roberge-Weiss transition as a probe for conformality in many-flavor QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07284)
*Massimo D'Elia,Marco Nacci,Kevin Zambello*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper proposes a new method to determine the onset of the conformal window for QCD with $N_f$ massless flavors by investigating the Roberge-Weiss transition temperature $T_{RW}$. The study, conducted on QCD with $N_f = 8$ flavors, suggests that $T_{RW}$ vanishes in the chiral limit, indicating that $N_f = 8$ is within the conformal window.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To identify the onset of the conformal window for QCD with $N_f$ massless flavors, and to propose an effective method to determine it from lattice simulations. The motivation is driven by the need to understand the phase structure of QCD, particularly when the number of quark flavors approaches the critical value where asymptotic freedom is lost.

Method: The method involves the investigation of the Roberge-Weiss (RW) transition temperature $T_{RW}$ at specific values of the imaginary baryon chemical potential. Lattice simulations are performed using stout improved staggered fermions and the tree-level improved Symanzik pure gauge action, with varying Euclidean temporal extents ($N_t = 8, 10, 12, 16, 24$).

Result: For QCD with $N_f = 8$ flavors, the evidence shows that the RW transition temperature $T_{RW}$ vanishes in the chiral limit, which is interpreted as an indication that $N_f = 8$ is already inside the conformal window. This finding supports the use of $T_{RW}$ as a tool for identifying the onset of the conformal window.

Conclusion: The proposed method based on the Roberge-Weiss transition temperature $T_{RW}$ is effective for determining the onset of the conformal window. The results suggest that with $N_f = 8$ flavors, the theory is already in the conformal window, providing a new approach to map out the phase diagram of QCD.

Abstract: We consider the problem of identifying the onset of the conformal window for QCD with $N_f$ massless flavors in the fundamental representation, and propose a new effective method to determine it from lattice simulations. This method is based on the investigation of the so-called Roberge-Weiss transition temperature $T_{RW}$, which is encountered at specific values of the imaginary baryon chemical potential, and can also be interpreted as the inverse of the critical spatial size at which charge conjugation is spontaneously broken in a finite box. Since $T_{RW}$ corresponds to a genuine phase transition for any value of the quark masses, it is a well-defined quantity; we argue that the critical $N_f$ at which $T_{RW}$ vanishes in the chiral limit coincides with the onset of the conformal window. We implement our proposal by investigating QCD with $N_f = 8$ flavors, discretized via stout improved staggered fermions and the tree-level improved Symanzik pure gauge action, at Euclidean temporal extents $N_t = 8, 10, 12, 16, 24$. In this case, we find evidence that $T_{RW}$ already vanishes in the chiral limit, indicating that $N_f = 8$ is already in the conformal window.
