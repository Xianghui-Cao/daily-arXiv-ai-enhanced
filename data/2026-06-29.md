### [1] [Sampling the Schwinger Model with Gauge-Equivariant Diffusion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27481)
*Octavio Vega,Aida X. El-Khadra*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores a diffusion-based method to accelerate sampling in the $N_f = 2$ lattice Schwinger model, using a U(1)-equivariant score-based generative model. It shows that this approach can produce unbiased estimates for observables and potentially improve upon HMC by reducing topological freezing near critical parameters.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to address the critical slowing down problem in Lattice Field Theories (LFT) by employing a generative model for more efficient ensemble generation.

Method: A U(1)-equivariant score-based generative model is trained to sample gauge link configurations from the marginal Schwinger model. Model likelihoods are computed to estimate observables, and the performance is compared against Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC).

Result: The results show that the generative model provides unbiased estimates for observables, which are in agreement with those from MCMC simulations, and it demonstrates an improvement over HMC, particularly in terms of reduced topological freezing near critical parameters.

Conclusion: The study concludes that the diffusion-based approach, through the use of a U(1)-equivariant score-based generative model, is a promising method for accelerating sampling in the $N_f = 2$ lattice Schwinger model, offering potential advantages over traditional methods like HMC.

Abstract: We present a first study of a diffusion-based approach to accelerated sampling of the $N_f = 2$ lattice Schwinger model. Our work is inspired by recent and growing successes in developing such generative models for ensemble generation in LFT to overcome the well-known critical slowing down problem. We train a U(1)-equivariant score-based generative model to sample gauge link configurations from the marginal Schwinger model. By computing model likelihoods, we obtain unbiased estimates for observables that closely match those produced by MCMC simulations. We also demonstrate improvement over HMC as measured qualitatively by a reduction in topological freezing near critical parameters.

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper introduces a new method using diagonal Kenney-Laub (KL) iterates to approximate the matrix sign function in the overlap Dirac operator of lattice QCD, which is found to be more efficient and better at preserving chiral symmetry than the Chebyshev polynomial approach.

Method: The method employs diagonal Kenney-Laub (KL) iterates, which are evaluated through their partial fraction decomposition, on quenched lattices with Wilson and Brillouin operators as kernels. The performance is assessed by measuring the violation of the Ginsparg-Wilson relation and the critical bare quark mass for increasing orders of approximation.

Result: Results show that the KL iterate method delivers enhanced chiral symmetry preservation and computational efficiency compared to the traditional Chebyshev polynomial approach when applied to the overlap Dirac operator in lattice QCD simulations.

Abstract: We propose a formulation of the overlap Dirac operator in lattice QCD that employs diagonal Kenney-Laub (KL) iterates to approximate the matrix sign function. KL iterates require no prior spectral information about the kernel operator and, when expressed via their partial fraction decomposition, offer a practical and efficient approximation scheme. We evaluate this approach in a proof-of-concept implementation using quenched lattices at $β=6.2$ and two Dirac operator discretizations as a kernel, namely the Wilson and the Brillouin operators. By examining the approximate overlap operator's violation of the Ginsparg-Wilson relation and the critical bare quark mass for increasing approximation order, we find that KL iterates deliver enhanced chiral symmetry preservation and computational efficiency compared to the Chebyshev polynomial approach.

### [3] [The QCD energy-momentum tensor on the lattice: non-perturbative renormalization with $N_f=3$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28035)

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a method for constructing the traceless components of the energy-momentum tensor on the lattice for QCD with 3 flavors, ensuring their correlation functions satisfy Ward identities in the continuum limit. It uses Wilson-plaquette and O(a)-improved Wilson actions, defines bare gluonic and fermionic components, and determines renormalization constants non-perturbatively through Monte Carlo simulations.

Motivation: The motivation is to accurately construct the traceless components of the energy-momentum tensor on the lattice for QCD, which is essential for maintaining the correct symmetries and Ward identities in the continuum limit, particularly when dealing with 3 flavors of quarks.

Method: The method involves defining the theory using specific lattice actions, splitting the irreducible nonet representation into triplet and sextet representations, and employing non-perturbative methods to determine the necessary renormalization constants. The determination of these constants is achieved by imposing discretized versions of continuum Ward identities under certain conditions, followed by Monte Carlo simulations.

Result: The result is the successful determination of the renormalization constants for both triplet and sextet representations with an accuracy of a few percent over a range of bare coupling constant squared values (0 ≤ g_0^2 ≤ 0.96).

Conclusion: The conclusion of the paper is that it has successfully constructed the traceless components of the energy-momentum tensor for QCD with 3 flavors on the lattice, satisfying the required Ward identities, and provided accurate non-perturbative determinations of the renormalization constants needed for the calculation.

Abstract: We construct the traceless components of the energy-momentum tensor on the lattice for QCD with $N_f=3$ flavours, such that their correlation functions satisfy the appropriate Ward identities in the continuum limit. To carry out this program, we define the theory on the lattice by the Wilson-plaquette and the $O(a)$-improved Wilson actions for gluons and quarks respectively. The discretization of the space-time entails that (i) the irreducible nonet representation of the SO($4$) group splits into a triplet and a sextet irreducible representations of the hypercubic group, and (ii) for each multiplet non-perturbative determinations of the the gluonic and fermionic renormalization constants are required. The bare gluonic components of the energy-momentum tensor are defined via the clover discretization of the field strength tensor, while the fermionic ones are discretized by appropriate combinations of symmetric covariant derivatives. Either for the triplet or the sextet representations, the two independent renormalization constants are then fixed non-perturbatively by imposing discretized versions of continuum Ward identities for one-point correlation functions in the presence of shifted boundary conditions and an imaginary chemical potential. The non-perturbative calculation is then carried out by Monte Carlo simulations, and the resulting renormalization constants are determined with a final accuracy of a few percent for values of the bare coupling constant squared in the range $0 \leq g_0^2\leq 0.96$.

Abstract: We investigate the phase transition of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) with three degenerate quark flavors at zero baryon chemical potential. ### [6] [Spectral densities from Euclidean correlators via integral transforms: theoretical framework](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28167)
*Leonardo Giusti,Matteo Saccardi,Diego Toniolo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a method for extracting spectral densities from Euclidean time correlation functions using analytic formulae and integral transforms, with applications in lattice field theory and beyond. It addresses the challenges of finite temporal extents on lattices and proposes a way to control uncertainties in the reconstructed spectral density.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a non-perturbative method for resolving dynamical properties of quantum field theories through spectral densities, which can be obtained from experimental measurements and correlation functions. The work aims to bridge the gap between continuum and discrete sampling of correlation functions, while also addressing practical limitations in lattice computations.

Method: The authors use integral transforms to derive analytic formulae for the inverse Laplace transform, allowing the extraction of spectral densities from both continuum and discrete samplings of Euclidean time correlation functions. They account for regulated and smeared spectral densities and analyze the convergence of the lattice solution to the continuum one. Additionally, they introduce incomplete integral transforms to handle the finite extent of lattices and establish bounds for the contribution of unknowns to the smeared spectral density.

Result: The result is a set of analytic formulae that enable the calculation of spectral densities with controlled precision, even when dealing with the finite extent of lattices. The authors demonstrate that the lattice solution approaches the continuum solution up to second-order effects in the lattice spacing, provided the lattice correlator is improved. They also show how to bound and control the uncertainty in the reconstructed spectral density due to incomplete knowledge of the correlation function.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the proposed method provides a robust framework for the non-perturbative resolution of spectral densities from Euclidean time correlation functions, applicable to lattice field theory and other areas. The ability to rigorously bound and control the contributions from unknown parts of the correlation function allows for precise planning of lattice simulations to achieve a desired level of accuracy in the spectral density reconstruction.

Abstract: Spectral densities link experimental measurements to dynamical properties of a quantum field theory which, in turn, can be resolved non-perturbatively from the Euclidean time-dependence of correlation functions. By making extensive use of integral transforms, we present analytic formulae to carry out the inverse Laplace transform so as to extract spectral densities from either the continuum or the discrete sampling of correlation functions in the Euclidean time. Formulae extend to regulated and/or smeared spectral densities as well. We explicitly show that the proposed lattice solution tends to its continuum counterpart up to $O(a^2)$ effects in the lattice spacing $a$ if the lattice correlator is $O(a)$-improved. In practical computations, lattices have necessarily a finite Euclidean temporal extent, a lack of knowledge which suggests to introduce incomplete integral transforms and the corresponding incomplete smeared spectral densities. The contribution from the unknowns to a smeared spectral density can then be rigorously bound and kept under control if the integral transform of the smearing function decays fast enough with the conjugate variable. Conversely, the bound can be used to plan lattices so as to achieve a given target precision on the reconstructed spectral density of interest. The formulae presented here in the context of lattice field theory can be easily applied or extended to other areas of research.
