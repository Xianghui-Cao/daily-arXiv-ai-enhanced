### [1] [Isospin breaking corrections to a lattice QCD calculation of $\varepsilon'$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26336)
*Norman H. Christ,Erik Lundstrum*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper examines the impact of electromagnetism and isospin-breaking light quark mass differences on the direct CP violation parameter ε' in kaon decay, showing that a lattice QCD+QED calculation with photon energy between 0.5-2.0 GeV can capture all enhanced effects due to the ΔI=1/2 rule, while low-energy photons have a negligible effect.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation for this study is to understand the significant (up to 25%) influence of electromagnetism and isospin-breaking light quark mass difference on the direct CP violation parameter ε', which is important for precision physics and tests of the Standard Model.

Method: A theoretical approach using lattice QCD+QED calculations is employed, focusing on an intermediate range of photon energies (0.5-2.0 GeV) where the ΔI=1/2 rule enhancement is most effective, and excluding short-distance and infrared photon contributions.

Result: The results show that the enhanced effects by the ΔI=1/2 rule are fully captured within the specified photon energy range, and the contribution from low-energy photons (E_γ ≲ 0.5 GeV) is estimated to be small, around 0.5% on ε'.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that a QCD+QED lattice calculation with photons in the energy range 0.5-2.0 GeV is sufficient to account for the dominant isospin-breaking corrections to ε', and the impact of lower energy photons can be considered negligible or controlled through chiral perturbation theory.

Abstract: Because of the $ΔI = 1/2$ rule, the effects of electromagnetism and the isospin-breaking light quark mass difference on the direct CP violation parameter $\varepsilon'$ may be as large as 25\% and are consequently of immediate interest. In a lattice QCD calculation the effects of isospin breaking on the various features of kaon decay can be clearly distinguished and those effects enhanced by the $ΔI=1/2$ rule on $\varepsilon'$ explicitly identified. We show that all such enhanced effects can be captured in a QCD + QED lattice calculation in which the exchanged photon has an energy in an accessible, intermediate range between 0.5-2.0 GeV. Short-distance effects ($2.0 \mathrm{\ GeV} \lesssim E_γ$), usually treated in QCD and electroweak perturbation theory, are not enhanced by the $ΔI=1/2$ rule, beyond the well-understood contribution of the two electroweak penguin operators. Infrared photons do not contribute to $\varepsilon'$ while low-energy photons ($E_γ\lesssim 0.5$ GeV) are not $ΔI=1/2$ rule enhanced or are suppressed by one order in chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). An explicit ChPT estimate of this low-energy-photon contribution, a contribution that is difficult to determine in a finite-volume lattice calculation, suggests that the effect on $\varepsilon'$ is on the order of 0.5\%.

### [2] [Performance of Low Mode Averaging on Twisted-Mass Fermion Ensembles at the physical pion mass point](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27072)
*Constantia Alexandrou,Simone Bacchio,Antonio Evangelista,Roberto Frezzotti,Francesca Margari,Francesco Sanfilippo,Christian Schneider*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study evaluates the performance of low-mode averaging (LMA) on twisted-mass fermion ensembles, focusing on its theoretical framework and practical cost-effectiveness. It examines light-quark meson and baryon observables, comparing two LMA implementations for mesons, and applies the exact LMA approach to baryons. The research also determines the renormalised chiral condensate and extracts a scale-independent low-energy constant.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to assess the effectiveness and cost-efficiency of low-mode averaging (LMA) in the context of modern lattice QCD, particularly with near-physical quark masses, and to provide insights into the hadronic vacuum polarisation contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment as well as other important physical constants.

Method: The researchers conducted a numerical study of light-quark meson and baryon observables using twisted-mass fermion ensembles. They compared an exact LMA approach based on explicit low modes with an approximate, high-statistics variant that uses multigrid techniques. For baryons, they focused on the exact LMA approach. Additionally, they calculated the eigenvalue density of the massless Wilson operator and used the Banks-Casher relation to determine the renormalised chiral condensate.

Result: The results include a comparison of noise and cost reductions for meson two-point functions at large Euclidean times between the two LMA approaches. For baryons, the exact LMA approach was shown to reduce noise and computational costs. The study also provided a value for the renormalised chiral condensate and extracted the scale-independent low-energy constant, $ar{h}_1=5.2(1.1)$. The uncertainty in the chiral condensate is mainly due to the chiral extrapolation process.

Conclusion: The study concludes that the low-mode averaging technique is effective for reducing noise and computational costs in the calculation of light-quark meson and baryon observables. The exact LMA method is found to be more precise, while the approximate LMA method offers a high-statistics alternative. Furthermore, the determined values for the renormalised chiral condensate and the low-energy constant contribute to the understanding of QCD at low energies.

Abstract: We study the performance of low-mode averaging (LMA) on twisted-mass fermion ensembles at near-physical quark masses, assessing both its theoretical framework and practical cost-effectiveness in modern lattice QCD. In particular, we present a numerical study of light-quark meson and baryon observables. For mesons, we analyse two-point functions, including the vector-vector correlator relevant for the hadronic vacuum polarisation contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, comparing two implementations of LMA: an exact approach based on explicit low modes and an approximate, high-statistics variant using multigrid techniques. For baryons, we restrict to the exact approach and study both two- and three-point functions, quantifying the resulting noise and cost reductions at large Euclidean times. In addition, we compute the eigenvalue density of the massless Wilson operator and determine the renormalised chiral condensate via the Banks-Casher relation, obtaining $\sqrt[3]{Σ_{\mathrm{R}}}=269.5(4.5)~\mathrm{MeV}$ for $N_f{=}2{+}1{+}1$ isospin-symmetric QCD at a scale $2~\mathrm{GeV}$ in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme, with an uncertainty dominated by the chiral extrapolation. Additionally, from the pion-mass dependence of $Σ_{\mathrm{R}}$, we extract the scale-independent low-energy constant $\bar{h}_1=5.2(1.1)$.
