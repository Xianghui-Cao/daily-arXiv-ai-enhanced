### [1] [Analyzing the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model with the Worldvolume HMC method](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14965)
*Masafumi Fukuma,Yusuke Namekawa*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method is successfully applied to the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model, overcoming the sign problem and providing physical observables with controlled statistical errors on an 8x8 lattice at specific temperature and interaction strength.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation of this paper is to address the severe sign problem in the doped two-dimensional Hubbard model, which hinders the use of standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo methods for certain parameter ranges. The authors aim to demonstrate the effectiveness of the WV-HMC method in such scenarios.

Method: The researchers apply the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method to the two-dimensional Hubbard model. This method is used to compute physical observables with controlled statistical errors for a system that is away from half-filling, where traditional quantum Monte Carlo methods typically fail due to the sign problem.

Result: The result of this study is that the WV-HMC method can predict physical observables with controlled statistical errors for the 8x8 lattice at a temperature T/t = 1/6.4 and interaction strength U/t = 8.0, conditions under which the standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo method fails.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from the research is that the WV-HMC method is a viable approach for simulating the doped two-dimensional Hubbard model, particularly in regions of parameter space where the sign problem makes conventional quantum Monte Carlo simulations ineffective.

Abstract: We apply the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method [arXiv:2012.08468] to the two-dimensional Hubbard model, which is known to suffer from a severe sign problem when the system is doped (away from half filling). We show that the method predicts physical observables with controlled statistical errors on an $8 \times 8$ lattice at temperature $T/t = 1/6.4 \approx 0.156$ and interaction strength $U/t = 8.0$ ($t$ is the hopping amplitude), for which the standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo fails.
