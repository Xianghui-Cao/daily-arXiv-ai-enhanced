### [1] [Reflection-Positive Construction of a Four-Dimensional SU(N) Yang-Mills Theory with Mass Gap and Confinement](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19362)
*Mir Faizal,Arshid Shabir*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper demonstrates how, starting from a reflection-positive lattice formulation of pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, one can derive a transfer operator with a uniform gap and large Wilson loops showing an area law. A multiscale analysis preserves these features at different scales, leading to a Minkowski theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian and a linear potential for static charges, indicative of confinement. The construction is universal and connects the continuum limits from both strong and weak coupling, suggesting that the mass gap and confinement are natural outcomes of non-Abelian gauge theories.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a rigorous mathematical framework for understanding the properties of non-Abelian gauge theories, specifically the emergence of a mass gap and the phenomenon of confinement, which are fundamental to the behavior of quarks and gluons in quantum chromodynamics (QCD).

Method: The method involves a reflection-positive lattice formulation of pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, followed by a multiscale analysis that maintains the positivity and locality of the system across different scales. This approach uses convergent character expansions and interlaced inequalities to ensure the persistence of exponential clustering and a positive string tension. The Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction is then applied to translate the Euclidean space results into a Minkowski space theory.

Result: The result is a Minkowski theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian, a spectral gap above the vacuum, and a linear potential between static charges, which indicates confinement. The construction is shown to be universal and independent of the specific regulator used, and it connects the continuum limits obtained from both strong and weak coupling regimes, providing a consistent picture of the non-Abelian vacuum.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn is that by adhering to the principles of positivity, locality, and renormalization, the mass gap and confinement emerge as natural properties of the non-Abelian vacuum, rather than being assumptions or coincidences. This provides a coherent theoretical basis for the observed phenomena in QCD.

Abstract: In the Euclidean view one must first require that positivity not be violated, and from this modest demand, together with locality, a great deal follows: starting from a reflection-positive lattice formulation of pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory we obtain a transfer operator with a uniform gap, while large Wilson loops already show an area law by means of convergent character (polymer) expansions; a finite-range, gauge-covariant multiscale analysis then carries these features from one scale to the next with interlaced inequalities whose small defects can be summed, so that exponential clustering and a strictly positive string tension endure in the continuum; the Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction turns these Euclidean facts into a Minkowski theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian, the spectral gap lying above the vacuum and the linear potential for static charges appearing, which gives a concrete picture of confinement; the construction depends on no special regulator, for a single-scale Lipschitz control and a telescoping argument bind all admissible reflection-positive slicings into a unique limiting measure and thus secure universality; moreover, the same framework admits entry from weak coupling, so that the continuum reached from strong coupling meets the one approached along an asymptotically free trajectory, yielding one and the same theory; in my view this is how mathematical clarity and physical insight cooperate: positivity, locality, and renormalization working together so that the mass gap and confinement are not marvels to be assumed, but natural properties of the non-Abelian vacuum.

### [2] [Kernel transformations and bounds for smeared spectral functions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19503)
*William I. Jay,Matteo Saccardi*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A framework for transforming between smeared spectral functions using different smearing kernels is developed, providing exact and approximate transformation methods, error propagation, and ways to enforce spectral positivity.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The need to transform information from one family of energy-smeared observables to another, as required by phenomenology or comparison with other calculations, drives the development of this work. It addresses the challenge of converting data that are available in a specific form to a different form necessary for further analysis or application.

Method: The paper establishes analytic conditions for exact transformations and convergence without arbitrary regularization, provides explicit expressions for certain kernel classes, and when exact solutions are not possible, it employs regulated maps with systematic error bounds. It also considers the propagation of errors and the enforcement of spectral positivity to refine the results.

Result: The research yields a set of tools and methods for transforming between different types of smeared spectral functions, including Cauchy-to-Gaussian and Gaussian-to-Cauchy width mixtures, and offers a way to handle the inverse problem with controlled systematic errors. Additionally, it allows for the tightening of bounds through the enforcement of spectral positivity.

Conclusion: The developed framework facilitates the transformation of smeared spectral functions, which is essential for cross-comparison and application in various physical scenarios, while also providing a method to control and quantify the associated errors.

Abstract: This work develops a framework for transforming between smeared spectral functions computed using different smearing kernels. The kernel-transformation problem naturally arises when information is available for one family of energy-smeared observables, while phenomenology or comparison with other calculations require a different smearing. For exact transformations, analytic conditions are established for the maps to exist and converge without arbitrary regularization. Explicit expressions are provided for several kernel classes of interest, including Cauchy-to-Gaussian transformations and Gaussian-to-Cauchy width mixtures. When exact transformations are unavailable, the inverse problem is tackled through regulated maps paired with bounds on the associated systematic error, directly computable from the given input data. Errors on the input smeared spectral functions, either statistical or in the form of pointwise rigorous bounds, are then propagated to the target observables. Enforcing spectral positivity can be used to tighten the bounds.

### [3] [Scalar diquark mass and quark--diquark potential from lattice QCD using the potential method with a static quark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19840)
*Kai-Wen Kelvin-Lee,Noriyoshi Ishii*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study uses a HAL QCD-inspired potential method to determine the scalar diquark mass and quark-diquark potential in a baryonic system, finding a diquark mass close to (2/3)m_N and a Cornell-type potential with a string tension consistent with Wilson Loop results.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To investigate the scalar diquark mass and the quark-diquark potential in a baryonic system using a HAL QCD-inspired potential method, and to compare the results with the naïve constituent quark estimate and static quark-antiquark potential from Wilson Loop calculations.

Method: The researchers applied a HAL QCD-inspired potential method to a baryonic system of a scalar diquark and a static quark. They performed numerical calculations using 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations on a lattice with specific parameters, and self-consistently determined the diquark mass by matching the p-wave baryonic spectrum from two-point correlators within the potential framework.

Result: A scalar diquark mass was obtained that is close to the naïve constituent quark estimate (2/3)m_N. The quark-diquark potential was found to be of the Cornell type, consisting of a Coulomb plus linear term. The string tension derived from the quark-diquark potential agreed within about 5% with the value obtained from the static quark-antiquark potential (Wilson Loop).

Conclusion: The analysis provided a scalar diquark mass and a quark-diquark potential that are consistent with theoretical expectations, and the string tension extracted from the potential is in agreement with the one from the static quark-antiquark potential, supporting the validity of the HAL QCD-inspired potential method for studying baryonic systems.

Abstract: We study the scalar diquark mass and the quark--diquark potential by applying a HAL QCD-inspired potential method to a baryonic system composed of a scalar diquark and a static quark. The diquark mass is determined self-consistently by requiring that the p-wave baryonic spectrum obtained from two-point correlators be reproduced within the potential framework. Numerical calculations are performed using $2+1$ flavor QCD gauge configurations generated by the PACS-CS Collaboration on a $L^{3} \times T = 32^{3} \times 64$ lattice with $a^{-1} \approx 2.176$ GeV and the pion mass, $m_π \approx 702$ MeV. From the analysis, we obtain a scalar diquark mass which is close to the naïve constituent quark estimate $ (2/3)m_{N}$, together with a quark--diquark potential of the Cornell type (Coulomb + linear). The string tension extracted from the quark--diquark potential agrees within approximately 5% with that obtained from the static quark--antiquark potential (Wilson Loop).

### [6] [Confining Flux Tube in the Trace Deformed (2+1) Dimensional SU(2) Gauge Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20270)
*Claudio Bonati,Michele Caselle,Alessio Negro,Dario Panfalone,Lorenzo Verzichelli*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study examines the confining flux tube in a reconfined phase of modified SU(2) Yang-Mills theory. It finds that the Nambu-Goto string description is inadequate, while the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution accurately describes the data. The chromo-electric flux tube's transverse profile and intrinsic width show significant deviations from standard behavior. Evidence for a transition in the nature of the reconfinement line with increasing deformation parameter is also presented.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the properties of the confining flux tube in the reconfined phase of a trace deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, which may exhibit different behavior compared to ordinary confinement, especially concerning the effective string description and the characteristics of the chromo-electric flux tube.

Method: Lattice simulations are used to analyze Polyakov-loop correlators and determine the ground state energy of the effective string above the deconfinement temperature. The researchers compare their findings with the Nambu-Goto effective string model and the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution, and they investigate the transverse profile and intrinsic width of the chromo-electric flux tube. An exploratory study of the phase diagram is also conducted to identify transitions as the deformation parameter is increased.

Result: The results indicate that the Nambu-Goto effective string model, even with higher-order corrections, does not fit the data well as the trace deformation increases, whereas the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution provides an accurate description. The chromo-electric flux tube shows substantial changes in its transverse profile and intrinsic width. Additionally, there is evidence for a change in the reconfinement line from continuous to first order as the deformation parameter grows.

Conclusion: The reconfined phase of the trace deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory appears to be characterized by a qualitatively different effective-string regime, with the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution being more appropriate than the Nambu-Goto model. The findings suggest that the nature of the confining flux tube and the reconfinement transition are significantly affected by the trace deformation, leading to a distinct physical picture from that of ordinary confinement.

Abstract: We study the confining flux tube in the reconfined phase of trace deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in (2+1) dimensions. Using lattice simulations above the standard deconfinement temperature, we analyze Polyakov-loop correlators and extract the ground state energy of the effective string. We show that the usual Nambu-Goto effective string description, including its standard higher-order corrections, fails to reproduce the data as the trace deformation is increased. Remarkably, deep in the reconfined regime the results are instead accurately described by the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution, corresponding to an effective string dominated by an extrinsic-curvature term. We further investigate the transverse profile of the chromo-electric flux tube and find significant deviations from the standard Yang-Mills behavior, including a substantial modification of the intrinsic width. Finally, we present an exploratory study of the phase diagram, finding evidence for a transition from a continuous to a first order reconfinement line as the deformation parameter increases. These results suggest that the reconfined phase realizes a qualitatively different effective-string regime from ordinary confinement.
