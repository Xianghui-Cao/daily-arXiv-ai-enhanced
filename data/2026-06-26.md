### [1] [Isospin breaking corrections to a lattice QCD calculation of $\varepsilon'$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26336)
*Norman H. Christ,Erik Lundstrum*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper explores the impact of isospin-breaking effects on the direct CP violation parameter $\varepsilon'$ in kaon decay, indicating that a QCD + QED lattice calculation with photon energy between 0.5-2.0 GeV can capture all significantly enhanced effects due to the $\Delta I=1/2$ rule, while suggesting that contributions from low-energy photons have a minor effect on $\varepsilon'$.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation for this study is to understand and quantify the potentially significant (up to 25%) effects of electromagnetism and the isospin-breaking light quark mass difference on the direct CP violation parameter $\varepsilon'$, particularly in the context of the $\Delta I = 1/2$ rule.

Method: A theoretical approach using lattice QCD calculations combined with QED, focusing on an intermediate range of photon energies (0.5-2.0 GeV) where the effects are most pronounced, and utilizing chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to estimate the contribution of low-energy photons.

Result: The main finding is that all the enhanced effects due to the $\Delta I=1/2$ rule on $\varepsilon'$ can be effectively captured within a specific range of photon energies, and the contribution from low-energy photons to $\varepsilon'$ is estimated to be around 0.5%.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from the analysis is that lattice QCD+QED calculations with photons of energies in the 0.5-2.0 GeV range are sufficient to capture the essential isospin-breaking effects on $\varepsilon'$, and short-distance and infrared effects do not significantly contribute to $\varepsilon'$ after considering the enhancement by the $\Delta I=1/2$ rule.

Abstract: Because of the $ΔI = 1/2$ rule, the effects of electromagnetism and the isospin-breaking light quark mass difference on the direct CP violation parameter $\varepsilon'$ may be as large as 25\% and are consequently of immediate interest. In a lattice QCD calculation the effects of isospin breaking on the various features of kaon decay can be clearly distinguished and those effects enhanced by the $ΔI=1/2$ rule on $\varepsilon'$ explicitly identified. We show that all such enhanced effects can be captured in a QCD + QED lattice calculation in which the exchanged photon has an energy in an accessible, intermediate range between 0.5-2.0 GeV. Short-distance effects ($2.0 \mathrm{\ GeV} \lesssim E_γ$), usually treated in QCD and electroweak perturbation theory, are not enhanced by the $ΔI=1/2$ rule, beyond the well-understood contribution of the two electroweak penguin operators. Infrared photons do not contribute to $\varepsilon'$ while low-energy photons ($E_γ\lesssim 0.5$ GeV) are not $ΔI=1/2$ rule enhanced or are suppressed by one order in chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). An explicit ChPT estimate of this low-energy-photon contribution, a contribution that is difficult to determine in a finite-volume lattice calculation, suggests that the effect on $\varepsilon'$ is on the order of 0.5\%.

### [2] [Performance of Low Mode Averaging on Twisted-Mass Fermion Ensembles at the physical pion mass point](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27072)
*Constantia Alexandrou,Simone Bacchio,Antonio Evangelista,Roberto Frezzotti,Francesca Margari,Francesco Sanfilippo,Christian Schneider*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper evaluates the performance of low-mode averaging (LMA) on twisted-mass fermion ensembles, focusing on its theoretical and practical aspects in lattice QCD. It assesses meson and baryon observables, comparing LMA implementations, and calculates the renormalized chiral condensate and a low-energy constant.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To study the effectiveness of low-mode averaging (LMA) in reducing noise and computational costs for light-quark meson and baryon observables in modern lattice QCD simulations, especially at near-physical quark masses.

Method: A numerical analysis is conducted on light-quark meson and baryon observables using twisted-mass fermion ensembles. The study compares two LMA implementations for mesons and applies the exact LMA approach to baryons, examining both two- and three-point functions. The eigenvalue density of the massless Wilson operator is computed, and the Banks-Casher relation is used to determine the renormalized chiral condensate.

Result: The research demonstrates that LMA can significantly reduce noise and computational costs at large Euclidean times for both meson and baryon systems. The value of the renormalized chiral condensate is found to be 269.5(4.5) MeV, with an uncertainty primarily due to the chiral extrapolation. Additionally, the scale-independent low-energy constant ̈h_1 is extracted as 5.2(1.1).

Conclusion: The application of low-mode averaging (LMA) in lattice QCD simulations is shown to be effective in improving the signal-to-noise ratio and cost-effectiveness for the calculation of light-quark observables, providing valuable insights into the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment and the determination of QCD parameters.

Abstract: We study the performance of low-mode averaging (LMA) on twisted-mass fermion ensembles at near-physical quark masses, assessing both its theoretical framework and practical cost-effectiveness in modern lattice QCD. In particular, we present a numerical study of light-quark meson and baryon observables. For mesons, we analyse two-point functions, including the vector-vector correlator relevant for the hadronic vacuum polarisation contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, comparing two implementations of LMA: an exact approach based on explicit low modes and an approximate, high-statistics variant using multigrid techniques. For baryons, we restrict to the exact approach and study both two- and three-point functions, quantifying the resulting noise and cost reductions at large Euclidean times. In addition, we compute the eigenvalue density of the massless Wilson operator and determine the renormalised chiral condensate via the Banks-Casher relation, obtaining $\sqrt[3]{Σ_{\mathrm{R}}}=269.5(4.5)~\mathrm{MeV}$ for $N_f{=}2{+}1{+}1$ isospin-symmetric QCD at a scale $2~\mathrm{GeV}$ in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme, with an uncertainty dominated by the chiral extrapolation. Additionally, from the pion-mass dependence of $Σ_{\mathrm{R}}$, we extract the scale-independent low-energy constant $\bar{h}_1=5.2(1.1)$.

</details>
