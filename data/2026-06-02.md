### [1] [Probing and graph coloring techniques for trace estimation in Lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00394)
*Mario Papace,Andreas Frommer,Jose Jimenez-Merchan,Bruno Lang,Gustavo Ramirez-Hidalgo,Christian Schneider*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a novel multiplier-based coloring scheme for the stochastic probing technique used to compute Tr[D^-1] in Lattice QCD, which reduces variance and improves accuracy compared to the hierarchical probing method.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to improve the computational efficiency and accuracy of estimating Tr[D^-1], a key quantity in Lattice QCD, by reducing the variance associated with the off-diagonal entries of D^-1 when using the Hutchinson estimator.

Method: The authors review the stochastic probing technique and introduce a new multiplier-based coloring scheme that constructs structured sampling vectors. This scheme uses fewer colors than the hierarchical probing method and exploits the exponential decay of D^-1's off-diagonal elements to reduce variance.

Result: The results show that the multiplier-based approach yields a strictly lower variance than the partial hierarchical estimator at intermediate colorings, and numerical experiments confirm an improvement in relative accuracy with a smooth and monotonic decrease in variance as the number of colors increases.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the proposed multiplier-based coloring scheme outperforms the traditional hierarchical probing technique, providing a more accurate and efficient method for the estimation of Tr[D^-1] in Lattice QCD applications.

Abstract: The computation of $\mathrm{Tr}[D^{-1}]$, where $D$ is the Wilson-Dirac matrix of Lattice QCD, is a fundamental and computationally demanding task with applications to disconnected hadronic correlation functions. Since $D^{-1}$ is a dense matrix of prohibitive size, its trace cannot be computed exactly, and one must resort to stochastic estimation via the Hutchinson estimator. The variance of the resulting estimation, however, can be large, as it is dominated by the off-diagonal entries of $D^{-1}$. We review the stochastic probing technique, which reduces the variance by constructing structured sampling vectors from distance-$d$ colorings of the graph associated with $D$, exploiting the exponential off-diagonal decay of $D^{-1}$ to eliminate dominant short-range contributions to the variance. We then present a novel multiplier-based coloring scheme, which achieves valid distance-$d$ colorings at arbitrary distances with significantly fewer colors than the established hierarchical probing construction. We prove that at any intermediate coloring falling between two consecutive hierarchical levels, the multiplier-based estimator achieves strictly lower variance than the partial hierarchical estimator, for large enough $d$. This is confirmed by numerical experiments showing that the multiplier-based variance decreases smoothly and monotonically with the number of colors, avoiding the irregular behavior affecting hierarchical probing at intermediate colorings, and achieving a substantial improvement in relative accuracy.

### [2] [Decomposition of the axial-vector current in a finite box](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02243)
*Felix Hermsen,Matthias F. M. Lutz,Rob G. E. Timmermans*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores the matrix element of the axial-vector current between two nucleon states in a finite box, revealing that the usual form factor decomposition is incomplete. It provides a full set of one-loop level form factors and confirms the axial Ward identity in the chiral limit, with significant finite-volume effects observed, especially due to the Δ-isobar, which has implications for lattice studies.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the finite-volume effects on the axial-vector current's matrix elements in a finite box, as well as to provide a complete description of the form factors at the one-loop level, which is essential for accurate lattice QCD calculations.

Method: The authors use the chiral Lagrangian density including nucleon and Δ-isobar degrees of freedom to study the finite-volume effects. They derive expressions for the complete set of form factors at one loop and check the validity of the axial Ward identity in the chiral limit, also presenting numerical results for flavor-SU(2) lattice ensembles.

Result: The result is that the standard form factor decomposition is found to be incomplete in a finite box, and the derived complete set of form factors at one loop shows significant finite-volume effects, particularly due to the presence of the Δ-isobar. The axial Ward identity is shown to hold in the chiral limit.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that full finite-box results are necessary for the precise determination of the form factors, and the role of the Δ-isobar is highlighted as an important contributor to finite-volume effects, which has consequences for the interpretation of lattice studies of the axial-vector current.

Abstract: We consider the matrix element of the axial-vector current between two nucleon states in a finite box. Starting from the chiral Lagrangian density with nucleon and $Δ$-isobar degrees of freedom, we study the finite-volume effects at the one-loop level. We show that the standard decomposition into the axial-vector and pseudoscalar form factor is incomplete in a finite box. We derive expressions for the complete set of form factors at one loop. We verify that the axial Ward identity holds in the chiral limit. Selected numerical results are shown for two flavor-SU(2) lattice ensembles. Sizable finite-volume effects are observed, with an important role for the $Δ$-isobar. We discuss the implications of our results for lattice studies of the axial-vector current. We conclude that full finite-box results are crucial for a precise determination of the form factors.

### [3] [Kinematic enhancement for nucleon interpolators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02447)
*Daniel Reitinger,Tobias Sizmann,Andreas Schäfer,Rui Zhang,Yong Zhao*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper benchmarks the code for nucleon quark matrix elements using kinematically enhanced interpolators, showing significant precision improvement and no dependence on lattice spacing, which is advantageous for parton physics calculations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation stems from the need for reliable treatment of highly boosted hadrons in lattice QCD applications, with a specific emphasis on the requirements for future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) projects in the US and China (EIcC).

Method: The researchers benchmark their code by calculating unpolarized isovector nucleon quark matrix elements at large source-sink separations, using kinematically improved operators, and compare results across three CLS ensembles with varying lattice spacings but consistent pion mass.

Result: The study finds an order of magnitude improvement in the precision of renormalized nucleon matrix elements at high momentum. There is no observed statistically significant dependence on the lattice spacing in the renormalized matrix elements at similar momentum values.

Conclusion: The use of kinematically improved operators is concluded to be highly beneficial for parton physics calculations, with the potential to become a standard component in modern lattice QCD studies, including a wider range of baryon observables.

Abstract: The reliable treatment of highly boosted hadrons is crucial for many lattice QCD applications. For all these cases the kinematically enhanced interpolators promise very significant improvements and are, therefore, ever more often used in recent calculations, especially for highly boosted mesons like the pion. Motivated by, e.g., the physics program of the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) in the US and Electron-Ion collider in China (EIcC), we systematically benchmark our code for the unpolarized isovector nucleon quark matrix elements extracted at large source-sink separations, where excited state artifacts are significantly suppressed. We find that the precision of the renormalized nucleon matrix elements is typically improved by an order of magnitude at momentum $P_z\sim2.5$ GeV. By comparing the results from three CLS ensembles with different lattice spacings $a$ but the same pion mass, we observe no statistically significant dependence on $a$ in the renormalized matrix elements at nearly identical values of $P_z$. These encouraging results suggest that the use of kinematically improved operators is highly advantageous for parton physics calculations and can be extended to a broader class of baryon observables, making them a promising candidate for a standard component of modern lattice QCD.
