### [1] [Phases of the $q$-deformed $\mathrm{SU}(N)$ Yang-Mills theory at large $N$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03843)
*Tomoya Hayata,Yoshimasa Hidaka,Hiromasa Watanabe*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper examines the (2+1)-dimensional q-deformed SU(N)_k Yang-Mills theory on a lattice, exploring how confinement and topological order vary with parameters N, g, and k. A mean-field analysis reveals that topological order is robust at large N, suggesting a more complex continuum limit than expected, which calls for further studies beyond mean-field theory.

### [2] [Three-particle scattering amplitudes from lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04147)
*Stephen R. Sharpe*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper reviews the recent advancements in calculating scattering amplitudes and resonance properties for three-particle systems using lattice QCD, with a focus on the transition from finite-volume spectrum input to integral equation solutions that can identify resonance poles, and it discusses future potential developments.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide an overview of the current state of research in using lattice QCD for the calculation of three-particle scattering amplitudes and resonances, and to highlight the importance of these calculations for understanding the dynamics of quantum chromodynamics (QCD).

Method: The method involves using lattice QCD to compute the finite-volume spectrum as input, which is then processed through the solution of integral equations to obtain scattering amplitudes. These amplitudes are analytically continued into the complex energy plane to locate resonance poles.

Result: The result is a set of methodologies and techniques that allow for the accurate calculation of three-particle scattering processes and the determination of resonance properties, providing a deeper insight into the non-perturbative regime of QCD.

Conclusion: The conclusion suggests that the methods described have been successful in advancing our understanding of three-particle interactions and that there is a promising outlook for further applications and extensions of this work.

Abstract: I review recent progress in calculating scattering amplitudes and resonance properties involving three particles using results from lattice QCD. The necessary input is the finite-volume spectrum, and the outputs -- via solutions of integral equations -- are scattering amplitudes that can be continued into the complex plane to search for resonance poles. I describe the outlook for future extensions and applications of this work.
