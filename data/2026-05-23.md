### [1] [Normalizing flows for all-orders QED corrections in lattice field theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22444)
*Nils Hermansson-Truedsson,Gurtej Kanwar*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A new framework using normalizing-flow transformations in lattice field theory is developed, allowing for all-orders QED corrections without additional Monte Carlo sampling. This method, tested on scalar QED in 2-4 dimensions with analytical and machine-learned flows, shows significantly reduced variance and the potential for application in more complex theories like Quantum Chromodynamics.

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation behind this paper is to introduce a novel approach that circumvents the necessity of complex Wick-contraction diagrams and extra Monte Carlo sampling for computing all-order QED corrections, thereby simplifying calculations in lattice field theory and potentially enabling more efficient computations in challenging theories such as Quantum Chromodynamics.

Method: The authors develop and apply a framework that utilizes normalizing-flow transformations to field configurations in lattice field theory. They test the method on lattice scalar QED across two, three, and four spacetime dimensions, employing both analytical and machine-learned flow techniques. The study also demonstrates the scalability of the trained flows from smaller to larger lattice geometries.

Result: The results show that the proposed method leads to a significant reduction in variance compared to traditional methods. Additionally, the flows trained on smaller lattices can be effectively used on much larger lattices, maintaining high efficiency, which indicates the robustness and versatility of the technique.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from the research is that the use of normalizing-flow transformations provides an efficient and accurate way to compute all-order QED corrections in lattice field theory. The findings suggest a promising direction for future applications, particularly in theories involving fermions and in the realm of Quantum Chromodynamics.

Abstract: This work develops a framework to apply normalizing-flow transformations of field configurations for all-orders Quantum Electrodynamics (QED) corrections in lattice field theory. This opens a new possibility to determine all-order corrections without the need for additional Monte Carlo sampling, generally bypassing the complexity in Wick-contraction diagrams needed at fixed order. The new method is applied to lattice scalar QED in two, three, and four spacetime dimensions, using both analytical and machine-learned flows, with both approaches yielding estimates with significantly reduced variance with respect to standard methods. It is further shown that flows can be trained using small lattice geometries and subsequently evaluated on much larger lattice geometries while maintaining good efficiency. A generalization to theories with fermions is envisaged, suggesting a path to applications in challenging field theories including lattice Quantum Chromodynamics.

### [2] [Complete lattice QCD calculation of $K^{-}\to \ell^{-}\barν_{\ell}\ell^{'+}\ell^{'-}$ form factors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22742)
*R. Di Palma,R. Frezzotti,G. Gagliardi,V. Lubicz,G. Martinelli,C. T. Sachrajda,F. Sanfilippo,S. Simula,N. Tantalo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents the first complete lattice QCD calculation of the four structure-dependent form factors for the rare charged kaon decay, with fully controlled uncertainties. The results enable the evaluation of decay rates and differential observables for all four channels, providing a benchmark for experimental comparison.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a precise theoretical prediction for the rare charged kaon decays, which can be used to test the Standard Model and potentially reveal new physics through comparison with experimental data.

Method: The authors use gauge ensembles from the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration with 2+1+1 flavors of Wilson-clover twisted-mass fermions, simulations at physical quark masses, and include quark-disconnected contributions. They employ the Spectral Function Reconstruction (SFR) method to handle dilepton invariant masses above the two-pion threshold, and investigate finite-volume effects and the continuum limit using multiple lattice spacings and spatial extents.

Result: The result is a comprehensive set of form factors for the rare charged kaon decays across the kinematical region, allowing for the calculation of decay rates and differential observables for all four channels, which can now be compared directly with experimental measurements.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the study provides a robust theoretical framework for the rare charged kaon decays, enabling accurate predictions within the Standard Model, and sets the stage for detailed phenomenological analysis in a companion paper.

Abstract: We present the first complete lattice QCD calculation of the four structure-dependent form factors governing the rare charged kaon decay $K^- \to \ell^- \barν_\ell \ell'^+ \ell'^-$, with fully controlled statistical and systematic uncertainties. Our calculation is based on gauge ensembles generated by the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration (ETMC) with $N_f = 2+1+1$ flavors of Wilson-clover twisted-mass fermions. Simulations are performed directly at the physical values of the light and strange quark masses, and include an estimate of the quark-disconnected contributions in which the virtual photon couples to sea quarks. All four form factors are determined across the kinematical region probed by experiments. The Spectral Function Reconstruction (SFR) method of Ref. [1] is employed to overcome the analytic continuation problem for dilepton invariant masses above the two-pion threshold. Finite-volume effects are investigated using ensembles with spatial extents $L\simeq [3.8,7.6]~\mathrm{fm}$, while the continuum limit is obtained from three lattice spacings in the range $a\in[0.057, 0.08]~\mathrm{fm}$. Our results for the form factors enable the evaluation of decay rates and differential observables for all four channels, $K^- \to e^- \barν_e e^+ e^-$, $K^- \to e^- \barν_e μ^+ μ^-$, $K^- \to μ^- \barν_μe^+ e^-$, and $K^- \to μ^- \barν_μμ^+ μ^-$, thereby providing first-principles Standard Model predictions against which existing and upcoming measurements can be directly compared. A detailed phenomenological analysis of the decay rates and associated observables is presented in a companion paper [2].
