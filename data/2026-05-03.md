### [1] [Topological Susceptibility and QCD at Finite Theta Angle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28035)
*Claudio Bonanno,Claudio Bonati,Massimo D'Elia*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper provides an educational introduction to the theoretical aspects of topology and θ-dependence in QCD, their relevance in the Standard Model and beyond, a summary of analytical predictions using various approaches, and recent numerical results from lattice QCD simulations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to offer a pedagogical overview of the topological and θ-dependent features of QCD, including their phenomenological significance in the context of the Standard Model and beyond, such as the strong CP problem and its axion solution.

Method: The method involves reviewing the main analytic predictions for θ-dependence using chiral effective theories, large-N arguments, and semiclassical methods, and presenting the most recent numerical results from Monte Carlo simulations of lattice QCD.

Result: The paper compiles a range of theoretical predictions and numerical simulation outcomes that contribute to the understanding of QCD's topological structure and θ-dependence, which are important for both the Standard Model and extensions like the axion solution to the strong CP problem.

Conclusion: The conclusion emphasizes the importance of studying QCD topology and θ-dependence for deepening our understanding of fundamental physics, including the resolution of the strong CP problem and the role of the axion, and highlights the complementarity between different theoretical and numerical approaches.

Abstract: In this chapter we provide a pedagogical introduction to the main theoretical aspects related to topology and $θ$-dependence in Quantum Chromo-Dynamics (QCD), and to their phenomenological relevance in the Standard Model ($η^\prime$ physics, neutron electric dipole moment) and beyond (strong CP problem and the axion solution). We then provide an overview of the main analytic predictions for $θ$-dependence obtained using several different approaches (chiral effective theories, large-$N$ arguments, semiclassical methods) and their regimes of validity, as well as a selection of the most recent numerical results about QCD topology obtained via Monte Carlo simulations of the lattice-discretized theory.
