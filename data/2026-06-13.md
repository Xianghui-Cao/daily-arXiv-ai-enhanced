### [1] [Numerical Hints for Dyon Condensation at $θ=2π$ via Wilson-'t Hooft Loops in $SU(2)$ Yang-Mills Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13428)
*Hiromasa Watanabe,Issaku Kanamori,Okuto Morikawa,Yuki Nagai,Yuya Tanizaki,Akio Tomiya*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores the nontrivial 2π periodicity of Yang-Mills theories at θ and θ+2π, using Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators and a 1-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow to provide numerical evidence for dyon condensation at θ=2π.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to examine the different symmetry-protected topological (SPT) states of confinement vacua at θ=0 and θ=2π in the context of generalized global symmetries, specifically focusing on the 1-form center symmetry.

Method: The method involves measuring Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators at θ=2π for the SU(2) Wilson lattice gauge action and analyzing their long-distance behaviors. A 1-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow is used to smear lattice gauge fields, which helps in identifying the gauge topological charge in the presence of defects.

Result: The result is numerical evidence that supports the theoretical prediction of dyon condensation rather than monopole condensation at θ=2π.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the confinement vacua at θ=2π exhibit characteristics of dyon condensation, which is consistent with the theoretical framework of SPT states and 1-form center symmetry.

Abstract: Yang-Mills theories at $θ$ and $θ+2π$ are unitarily equivalent, but their $2π$ periodicity has a nontrivial realization. Recent developments in generalized global symmetries show that confinement vacua at $θ=0$ and $2π$ should belong to different symmetry-protected topological (SPT) states with the $1$-form center symmetry. For its examination, we measure the Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators at $θ=2π$ for the $SU(2)$ Wilson lattice gauge action and discuss their long-distance behaviors. This requires us to identify the gauge topological charge in the presence of defects, and we employ the $1$-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow to smear lattice gauge fields. We then obtain numerical evidence consistent with dyon condensation at $θ=2π$, rather than monopole condensation, as theoretically predicted.

### [2] [New results on gauge field decomposition in SU(3) gluodynamics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13490)
*V. Bornyakov,V. Goy,I. Kudrov*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper investigates the decomposition of nonabelian gauge fields into Abelian and modified nonabelian components, after removing monopoles and fixing the Maximal Abelian gauge in SU(3) lattice gluodynamics. The static potential is computed for the original and decomposed fields, revealing that with optimal gauge fixing the sum of the decomposed potentials deviates from the original, but this deviation is reduced when using a different set of Gribov copies.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the behavior of the static potential derived from the nonabelian gauge field after it has been decomposed into its Abelian and modified nonabelian parts, and to investigate the impact of the choice of Gribov copies on the precision of the decomposition.

Method: The method involves decomposing the nonabelian gauge field into an Abelian component due to Abelian monopoles and a modified nonabelian component with monopoles removed, following the Maximal Abelian gauge fixing in SU(3) lattice simulations. The static potential for the original and decomposed fields is then calculated at two different lattice spacings.

Result: The results show that with optimal gauge fixing, the sum of the potentials from the decomposed fields (V_mon + V_mod) does not match the original static potential (V(r)). However, the decomposition is more accurate across all distances when using a different set of Gribov copies.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the decomposition of the static potential is sensitive to the choice of Gribov copies, and there exists a set of Gribov copies that allows for a more precise decomposition, indicating that the selection of Gribov copies plays a crucial role in the study of the nonabelian gauge field's properties.

Abstract: We study decomposition of the nonabelian gauge field into the Abelian component created by Abelian monopoles and the modified nonabelian components with monopoles removed after fixing the Maximal Abelian gauge in SU(3) lattice gluodynamics. We compute the static potential V (r) for the original gauge field and for its components V_mon and V_mod at two values of the lattice spacing. We confirm that with optimal gauge fixing the sum V_mon + V_mod deviates substantially from V(r). We show that this decomposition of the static potential is satisfied with good precision at all distances when we use another set of Gribov copies.

</details>
