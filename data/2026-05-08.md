### [1] [Lattice fermion formulation via Physics-Informed Neural Networks: Ginsparg-Wilson relation and Overlap fermions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06022)
*Tatsuhiro Misumi*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A novel framework using Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) for constructing lattice fermions is introduced, which can reproduce the overlap fermion operator and discover algebraic solutions to the Ginsparg-Wilson relation, including a new Fujikawa-type generalized version.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop a machine-learning-based method for formulating the Dirac operator that adheres to physical requirements such as symmetries, locality, and doubler-decoupling conditions, and to explore the potential of PINNs in discovering new algebraic relations within the context of lattice fermions.

Method: The method involves training a neural network to satisfy the Ginsparg-Wilson (GW) relation as a soft constraint, allowing it to learn an effective sign-function mapping. The framework is then extended to enable the neural network to autonomously find higher-order terms that converge to zero and recover known or new GW relations.

Result: The neural network reproduces the overlap fermion operator with high accuracy, learns the effective sign-function without explicit approximation, and discovers both the standard and a new Fujikawa-type generalized GW relation.

Conclusion: The proposed framework demonstrates the potential of PINNs in constructing lattice fermions and in the autonomous discovery of algebraic relations, offering a promising approach for future research in lattice field theory.

Abstract: We propose a novel, machine-learning-based framework for constructing lattice fermions using Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs). Our approach treats the formulation of the Dirac operator as an optimization problem guided by physical requirements, such as symmetries, locality and doubler-decoupling conditions. We first demonstrate that, when trained to satisfy the Ginsparg-Wilson (GW) relation as a soft constraint, a neural network reproduces the overlap fermion operator to high numerical accuracy and learns an effective sign-function mapping without explicitly using a prescribed polynomial or rational approximation. Secondly, we extend the framework from operator construction to machine-assisted algebraic discovery. Within a generalized polynomial ansatz, the network autonomously drives higher-order terms to zero and recovers the standard Ginsparg-Wilson relation. Remarkably, by changing the initial search bias, the same framework also finds a distinct solution corresponding to a Fujikawa-type generalized GW relation.

### [2] [Diffusion model for SU(N) gauge theories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06134)
*Javad Komijani,Marina K. Marinkovic,Lara Turgut*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper introduces a score-matching framework for SU(N) lattice gauge theories, demonstrating its effectiveness in generating high-quality samples from the Wilson gauge action using diffusion models. It also discusses the use of a corrector based on Hamiltonian molecular dynamics to improve sampling quality, despite the associated increase in computational cost, and suggests strategies for enhancing efficiency.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop a computationally efficient method for training diffusion models and generating high-quality samples from complex distributions, specifically within the context of SU(N) lattice gauge theories, which can be extended to other Lie groups.

Method: The authors apply a score-matching approach to SU(3) gauge configurations with the Wilson gauge action in 2D and 4D. They compare the generated samples with those from Hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) simulations. For large inverse coupling values, they introduce a predictor-corrector scheme with a corrector based on Hamiltonian molecular dynamics to ensure accurate reverse-time integration.

Result: The results show that the diffusion models can be effectively trained and used for sampling the Wilson gauge action. The introduction of the corrector significantly improves the quality of the samples, although it also increases the computational cost. The study outlines several strategies aimed at improving the overall sampling efficiency.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the proposed score-matching framework is successful in training diffusion models for SU(N) lattice gauge theories and generating high-quality samples. The use of a Hamiltonian molecular dynamics-based corrector enhances sample quality, but there is a trade-off with computational cost, leading to the need for further strategies to optimize sampling efficiency.

Abstract: Implicit score matching provides a computationally efficient approach for training diffusion models and generating high-quality samples from complex distributions. In this work, we develop a score-matching framework for SU(N) lattice gauge theories, which can be extended to other Lie groups. We apply the method to SU(3) gauge configurations with the Wilson gauge action in two and four dimensions and assess the quality of the generated samples by comparison with Hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) simulations. We show that the diffusion models can be successfully trained and applied for sampling the Wilson gauge action. For large values of inverse coupling, accurate reverse-time integration requires predictor-corrector schemes, for which we introduce a corrector based on Hamiltonian molecular dynamics. While the corrector significantly improves sampling quality, it also increases the computational cost. We outline several strategies for improving sampling efficiency.

### [3] [The strange and flavor-singlet axial form factors of the nucleon from lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06559)
*Alessandro Barone,Dalibor Djukanovic,Georg von Hippel,Harvey B. Meyer,Konstantin Ottnad,Hartmut Wittig*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a lattice QCD determination of the singlet axial form factor and the strange quark contribution to it, which is essential for understanding the nucleon's axial structure across different flavor channels.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a comprehensive understanding of the nucleon axial structure, including the singlet channel and the strange quark contribution, which are key components in the full flavor decomposition of the proton spin.

Method: The method involves using a set of $N_f = 2 + 1$ CLS gauge ensembles with $O(a)$-improved Wilson fermions to determine the singlet axial form factor and the strange quark contribution, with careful treatment of disconnected contributions and a complete error budget for extrapolation to physical limits.

Result: The results include a lattice QCD determination of the singlet axial form factor $G^{u+d+s}_A(Q^2)$, the strange quark contribution $G^{s}_A(Q^2)$, and a full error analysis, providing a more complete picture of the nucleon's axial structure across different flavors.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that this work, along with previous determinations of the isovector and isoscalar octet axial form factors, offers a comprehensive lattice QCD study of the nucleon's axial structure, contributing to the full flavor decomposition of the proton spin.

Abstract: The singlet axial form factor of the nucleon provides essential input for a complete understanding of the nucleon axial structure. Together with the isovector and isoscalar octet channels, in the forward limit it forms the basis for a full flavor decomposition of the proton spin. In this work we present a lattice QCD determination of the singlet axial form factor $G^{u+d+s}_A(Q^2)$ and related strange contribution $G^{s}_A(Q^2)$ using a set of $N_f = 2 + 1$ CLS gauge ensembles with $O(a)$-improved Wilson fermions, with a full error budget for the extrapolation to the chiral, continuum and infinite-volume limits. Particular focus is placed on the treatment of the disconnected contributions, which constitute the crucial element for the extraction of the strange component. Together with determinations of the isovector and isoscalar octet axial form factors, this work provides a comprehensive lattice QCD determination of the nucleon axial structure across different flavor channels.
