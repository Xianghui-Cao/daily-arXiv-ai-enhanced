### [1] [Hamiltonian Lattice QED$_3$ with One and Two Flavors of Wilson Fermions: Topological Structure and Response](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05616)
*Sriram Bharadwaj,Emil Rosanowski,Simran Singh,Alice di Tucci,Changnan Peng,Karl Jansen,Lena Funcke,Di Luo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper resolves the issue of time-reversal symmetry in staggered-fermion discretizations, which hinders the emergence of nontrivial topological phases, by using Wilson fermions in (2+1)D quantum electrodynamics. It shows that Wilson fermions can naturally enable topological regimes with nonzero Chern numbers and develops gauge-invariant diagnostics to probe topological response. The work is supported by exact diagonalization calculations, providing a foundation for quantum simulations of topological phases in lattice field theories.

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation behind this paper is to address the limitations posed by time-reversal symmetry in the commonly used staggered-fermion discretizations, which prevent the emergence of nontrivial topological phases in (2+1)D quantum electrodynamics. The authors aim to find an alternative method to realize these topological phenomena in near-term lattice experiments, which could be crucial for the development of quantum technologies and our understanding of fundamental physics.

Method: The authors systematically analyze fermion discretization effects in (2+1)D lattice Hamiltonians of fermions coupled to U(1) gauge fields that satisfy Gauss' law. They use Wilson fermions instead of staggered fermions, as they allow for the natural emergence of topological regimes with nonzero Chern numbers. Additionally, they develop gauge-invariant diagnostics such as many-body Chern numbers and current correlators to measure topological response. The theoretical findings are complemented by extensive exact diagonalization calculations to characterize the spectrum, correlators, and topological invariants.

Result: The results show that Wilson fermions, even in a minimal one-flavor theory, can lead to topological regimes with nonzero Chern numbers. The two-flavor extension at finite chemical potential further enriches the topological structure. The developed gauge-invariant diagnostics prove to be robust probes of topological response, even at weak coupling. The exact diagonalization calculations provide a detailed characterization of the system's properties, including the energy spectrum, correlators, and topological invariants, thereby offering a solid basis for future quantum simulations of topological phases in lattice field theories.

Conclusion: The conclusion of the paper is that Wilson fermions offer a viable approach to simulating topological phases in (2+1)D quantum electrodynamics, overcoming the limitations imposed by the time-reversal symmetry found in staggered-fermion discretizations. The work lays the groundwork for the experimental realization of these topological phenomena in near-term lattice experiments and provides a set of tools and diagnostics for characterizing topological states in lattice gauge theories.

Abstract: The quantum simulation of topological phases in (2+1)D quantum electrodynamics with Wilson fermions provides a promising route toward realizing topological phenomena in near-term lattice experiments. We show that the commonly used staggered-fermion discretizations in Hamiltonian gauge theories possesses an exact time-reversal symmetry, which forbids the emergence of nontrivial topological phases and has led to confusion in the existing literature. In this work, we resolve this obstacle by systematically analyzing fermion discretization effects in (2+1)D lattice Hamiltonians of fermions coupled to U(1) gauge fields that satisfy Gauss' law. We show that Wilson fermions, already in the minimal one-flavor theory, naturally enable topological regimes with nonzero Chern numbers, and that the two-flavor extension at finite chemical potential further enriches the accessible topological structure. We develop gauge-invariant diagnostics of topological response, including many-body Chern numbers and current correlators that remain robust probes at weak coupling. Finally, through extensive exact diagonalization calculations across both flavor settings, we characterize the spectrum, correlators, and topological invariants, providing a concrete foundation for near-term quantum simulations of topological phases in lattice field theories. The implications of this work for quantum simulations of lattice field theory are analyzed in a joint submission [1].

### [2] [A Lattice QCD study of $p-Λ$ scattering in continuum and chiral limits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05854)
*Hang Liu,Liuming Liu,Jin-Xin Tan,Wei Wang,Haobo Yan,Qian-Teng Zhu*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents the first systematic study of $I=1/2$ proton-$Λ$ scattering from lattice QCD, using seven sets of (2+1)-flavor lattice ensembles. The study provides scattering length, effective range, and cross sections in good agreement with experimental data, which is important for nuclear force theories and neutron star equations of state.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation of this paper is to provide a systematic study of $I=1/2$ proton-$Λ$ ($p$-$Λ$) scattering using lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), which is essential for understanding the unification of nuclear force theories and the construction of neutron star equations of state.

Method: The method used in this paper involves utilizing Lüsher's finite-volume method, effective range expansion, and chiral/continuum extrapolations on seven sets of (2+1)-flavor lattice ensembles with pion masses ranging from 135-317 MeV and three different lattice spacings.

Result: The results include the inverse of the scattering length and the effective range for the $^1S_0$ channel as 0.177(83) GeV and 2.9(1.4) fm, and for the $^3S_1$ channel as 0.016(76) GeV and 1.8(1.1) fm. The S-wave phase shifts are used to estimate the $p-Λ$ scattering cross section, and it is found that the $p-Λ$ system sustains attractive interactions.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from the study is that the derived scattering length, effective range, and cross sections are in good agreement with available experimental measurements, and these findings are critical for the development of nuclear force theories and the formulation of neutron star equations of state.

Abstract: We present a first systematic study of $I=1/2$ proton-$Λ$ ($p$-$Λ$) scattering from lattice QCD, using seven sets of $(2+1)$-flavor lattice ensembles with pion masses spanning 135-317 MeV and three lattice spacings with $a=(0.052,0.077, 0.105)$ fm. Using Lüscher's finite-volume method, effective range expansion and chiral/continuum extrapolations, we obtain the inverse of scattering length and effective range for the $^1S_0$ channel as 0.177(83) GeV and 2.9(1.4) fm, and for the $^3S_1$ channel as 0.016(76) GeV and 1.8(1.1) fm. From the derived S-wave phase shifts, we provide an estimate of the $p-Λ$ scattering cross section. Our results for scattering length, effective range and cross sections are in good agreement with available experimental measurements. We also find that the $p-Λ$ system sustains attractive interactions. These results provide critical input for the unification of nuclear force theories and the construction of neutron star equations of state.

### [3] [Masses of the conjectured H-dibaryon for different channels at different temperatures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05880)
*Liang-Kai Wu,Xi-Rui Zhao,Ning Li,Yong-Liang Hao,Xiao-Zhu Yu*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A lattice QCD study of the H dibaryon across 5 channels and 9 temperatures, showing mass variations and spectral function similarities. The mass differences between H-dibaryon and a Λ pair are positive for 27-plet and ΛΛ channels, and negative for singlet, NΞ, and ΣΣ channels at the lowest temperature examined.

Motivation: The motivation is to explore the properties of the conjectured H dibaryon, including its mass and stability, through a lattice QCD spectroscopy study over a range of temperatures and different flavor channels.

Method: The method involves using anisotropic lattices with 2+1 flavors of clover fermions, simulating five different H dibaryon channels at nine different temperatures, and analyzing the correlation functions' spectral functions to estimate the mass differences relative to a Λ pair.

Result: Results show that the H dibaryon's mass varies with channel, being largest in the 27-plet and smallest in the ΣΣ channel. The spectral density distributions are similar across all channels. At the lowest temperature, the mass difference Δm is positive for 27-plet and ΛΛ, indicating instability, while it is negative for singlet, NΞ, and ΣΣ, suggesting potential stability.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the H dibaryon's mass and stability depend on the flavor channel, with some channels (singlet, NΞ, ΣΣ) potentially allowing for a stable H dibaryon below a certain temperature, while others (27-plet, ΛΛ) do not support a bound state.

Abstract: We present a lattice QCD spectroscopy study of the conjectured H dibaryon for 5 different channels at nine different temperatures. The H dibaryon operator is constructed with five different channels which are flavor singlet, flavor 27-plet, $ΛΛ$, $N Ξ$ and $ΣΣ$. The nine different temperatures range from $T/T_c =0.24$ to $T/T_c = 1.90$. The simulations are performed on anisotropic lattice with $N_f=2+1$ flavours of clover fermion at quark mass which corresponds to $m_π=384(4) {\rm MeV} $. The thermal ensembles were provided by the FASTSUM collaboration and the zero temperature ensembles by the Hadspec collaboration. The simulations show that the mass of H-dibaryon for 27-plet channel is the largest at different temperatures, while the mass for $ΣΣ$ channel is the lightest. We also calculate the spectral function of the correlation function of H dibaryon for five channels. The spectral density distributions exhibit similar behavior for the five channels. The mass differences $Δm = m_H - 2\,m_Λ $ of H-dibaryon
  and
  $Λ$ pair at $T/T_c =0.24 $ for five channels are also estimated. The results show that $Δm = m_H - 2\,m_Λ $ for channels of 27-plet and $ΛΛ$ is positive, while $Δm = m_H - 2\,m_Λ $ for channels of singlet, $N Ξ$ and $ΣΣ$ is negative.
