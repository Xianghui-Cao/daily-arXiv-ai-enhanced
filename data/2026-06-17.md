### [1] [Precision renormalisation and improvement of $N_{\rm f}=3$ lattice QCD with Wilson fermions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17236)
*Patrick Fritzsch,Jochen Heitger,Simon Kuberski,Hubert Simma,Rainer Sommer*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a renormalization and improvement of various currents and quark masses in lattice QCD, with a focus on achieving high precision and confirming the restoration of chiral symmetry. It also provides results that can be used for solving multi-scale problems, such as those involving the b-quark mass or high-temperature conditions.

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to improve the accuracy of lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) calculations, particularly for flavour non-singlet axial current, pseudo-scalar density, vector current, tensor current, and quark masses, while ensuring the restoration of chiral symmetry. The work aims to provide precise results for use in B-physics and other multi-scale problems.

Method: The researchers use O(a) improved lattice QCD with three massless flavours and fine lattice spacings. They employ Schrödinger functional boundary conditions and tune lattices to lines of constant physics using gradient flow coupling. They also renormalize and improve the quark mass for additional heavy quarks relevant to B-physics studies.

Result: The study achieves high-precision confirmation of chiral symmetry restoration and flavor symmetry, leading to negligible uncertainties in observables. It provides accurate values for renormalization constants $Z_{\rm A}$ and $Z_{\rm V}$, with four to five significant digits. Additionally, the research explains the strong suppression of statistical variances in these constants.

Conclusion: The findings enable first-principles strategies for tackling multi-scale problems in particle physics, including the determination of the b-quark mass and high-temperature phenomena. The work contributes to the understanding of renormalization and chiral symmetry restoration in lattice QCD with Wilson fermions at small lattice spacing.

Abstract: We renormalise (and improve) the flavour non-singlet axial current, pseudo-scalar density, vector current and tensor current, as well as quark masses, in O(a) improved lattice QCD with three massless flavours and lattice spacings down to 0.01 fm. To this end, we tune a number of lattices with Schrödinger functional boundary conditions and resolutions $8\leq L/a\leq 64$ to lines of constant physics with massless quarks and fixed gradient flow coupling $\bar{g}_\mathrm{GF}^2(L_i),\; i=0,1,2$, corresponding to $L_0 \approx 0.25$ fm, $L_1=2L_0$ and $L_2=4L_0$. We further renormalise and improve the quark mass of additional heavy quarks for use in the B-physics programme of the collaboration (arXiv:2312.09811). Our somewhat technical results enable first-principles strategies for solving multi-scale problems involving, e.g., the b-quark mass (arXiv:2312.10017) or a large temperature (arXiv:2501.11603). Comparing also to other determinations of the axial current renormalisation constant $Z_{\rm A}$, we have a precise confirmation of how renormalisation and the restoration of chiral symmetry work out with Wilson fermions at small $a$. In particular, the accurate restoration of chiral symmetry and the exact flavour symmetry lead to practically negligible uncertainties in observables determined from Ward identities: four to five significant digits are achieved for $Z_{\rm A},Z_{\rm V}$. We provide an explanation for the strong suppression of their statistical variances.
