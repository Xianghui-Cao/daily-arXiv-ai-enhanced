### [1] [Reflection-Positive Construction of a Four-Dimensional SU(N) Yang-Mills Theory with Mass Gap and Confinement](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19362)
*Mir Faizal,Arshid Shabir*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper demonstrates how, starting from a reflection-positive lattice formulation of SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, one can derive a Minkowski theory with confinement and a mass gap, using positivity, locality, and renormalization. The approach is universal and connects the strong coupling and asymptotically free regimes.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to show that fundamental properties of non-Abelian gauge theories, such as the mass gap and confinement, can be derived from basic principles like positivity and locality, without the need for special regulators, and that these properties are consistent across different scales and couplings.

Method: The method involves developing a multiscale analysis with a transfer operator, proving area law for Wilson loops, ensuring exponential clustering and positive string tension, and applying Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction to obtain a Minkowski space theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian and linear potential for static charges. It also includes a proof of universality through a single-scale Lipschitz control and a telescoping argument.

Result: The results include a uniform spectral gap, an area law for large Wilson loops, exponential clustering, a strictly positive string tension, and a Minkowski theory with a mass gap and confinement. The uniqueness of the limiting measure is established, and it is shown that the continuum limit is the same whether approached from strong or weak coupling.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that by adhering to the principles of positivity, locality, and renormalization, the non-Abelian vacuum naturally exhibits a mass gap and confinement, which are key features of the theory, and this approach provides a mathematically clear and physically insightful framework for understanding these phenomena.

Abstract: In the Euclidean view one must first require that positivity not be violated, and from this modest demand, together with locality, a great deal follows: starting from a reflection-positive lattice formulation of pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory we obtain a transfer operator with a uniform gap, while large Wilson loops already show an area law by means of convergent character (polymer) expansions; a finite-range, gauge-covariant multiscale analysis then carries these features from one scale to the next with interlaced inequalities whose small defects can be summed, so that exponential clustering and a strictly positive string tension endure in the continuum; the Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction turns these Euclidean facts into a Minkowski theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian, the spectral gap lying above the vacuum and the linear potential for static charges appearing, which gives a concrete picture of confinement; the construction depends on no special regulator, for a single-scale Lipschitz control and a telescoping argument bind all admissible reflection-positive slicings into a unique limiting measure and thus secure universality; moreover, the same framework admits entry from weak coupling, so that the continuum reached from strong coupling meets the one approached along an asymptotically free trajectory, yielding one and the same theory; in my view this is how mathematical clarity and physical insight cooperate: positivity, locality, and renormalization working together so that the mass gap and confinement are not marvels to be assumed, but natural properties of the non-Abelian vacuum.

### [2] [Kernel transformations and bounds for smeared spectral functions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19503)
*William I. Jay,Matteo Saccardi*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A framework is developed for converting smeared spectral functions from one smearing kernel to another, with conditions and methods for both exact and approximate transformations, including error propagation and the use of spectral positivity to tighten bounds.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The need to transform between different smearing kernels arises when data is available in one form but required in another for comparison or further analysis. This work addresses the challenge of performing such transformations accurately and provides a method to handle cases where an exact transformation is not possible.

Method: Analytic conditions are derived for the existence and convergence of transformations without arbitrary regularization. For specific kernel classes, explicit transformation expressions are given. When exact solutions are unattainable, regulated maps along with systematic error bounds are used, and these bounds can be calculated directly from the input data. The method also includes error propagation and the application of spectral positivity to improve the accuracy of the results.

Result: The study provides a set of tools for transforming smeared spectral functions, which includes both exact and approximate methods. It offers a way to calculate the associated systematic errors and propagate uncertainties. The use of spectral positivity is shown to help in tightening the bounds on the transformed observables.

Conclusion: This research establishes a robust framework for the transformation of smeared spectral functions, enabling more flexible and accurate comparisons and analyses across different types of smearing. The provided methods for error handling and the use of spectral positivity contribute to the reliability of the transformed data.

Abstract: This work develops a framework for transforming between smeared spectral functions computed using different smearing kernels. The kernel-transformation problem naturally arises when information is available for one family of energy-smeared observables, while phenomenology or comparison with other calculations require a different smearing. For exact transformations, analytic conditions are established for the maps to exist and converge without arbitrary regularization. Explicit expressions are provided for several kernel classes of interest, including Cauchy-to-Gaussian transformations and Gaussian-to-Cauchy width mixtures. When exact transformations are unavailable, the inverse problem is tackled through regulated maps paired with bounds on the associated systematic error, directly computable from the given input data. Errors on the input smeared spectral functions, either statistical or in the form of pointwise rigorous bounds, are then propagated to the target observables. Enforcing spectral positivity can be used to tighten the bounds.

### [6] [Confining Flux Tube in the Trace Deformed (2+1) Dimensional SU(2) Gauge Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20270)
*Claudio Bonati,Michele Caselle,Alessio Negro,Dario Panfalone,Lorenzo Verzichelli*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study investigates the confining flux tube in the reconfined phase of trace deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in (2+1) dimensions, using lattice simulations. It finds that the Nambu-Goto effective string description fails at higher trace deformations, while the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution provides a better fit. The chromo-electric flux tube's transverse profile shows significant deviations from standard Yang-Mills behavior, and there is evidence for a transition in the nature of the reconfinement line as deformation increases.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the behavior of the confining flux tube in the reconfined phase of a deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, particularly how it differs from the standard confinement and what kind of effective string description applies in this regime.

Method: The method involves conducting lattice simulations above the deconfinement temperature to analyze Polyakov-loop correlators, extract the ground state energy of the effective string, and compare it with different theoretical models such as the Nambu-Goto and Polchinski-Yang descriptions. Additionally, the transverse profile of the chromo-electric flux tube is investigated, and an exploratory study of the phase diagram is performed.

Result: The results show that the Nambu-Goto effective string description, including its higher-order corrections, does not accurately describe the data as the trace deformation increases. In contrast, the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution, which includes an extrinsic-curvature term, matches the data well. The chromo-electric flux tube displays significant deviations from standard Yang-Mills behavior, with changes in the intrinsic width. There is also evidence for a change in the order of the reconfinement transition as the deformation parameter increases.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the reconfined phase of the trace deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory exhibits a qualitatively different effective-string regime compared to ordinary confinement, with the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution being a more appropriate description. The findings suggest a rich structure in the phase diagram and the possibility of a new type of stringy behavior in gauge theories.

Abstract: We study the confining flux tube in the reconfined phase of trace deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in (2+1) dimensions. Using lattice simulations above the standard deconfinement temperature, we analyze Polyakov-loop correlators and extract the ground state energy of the effective string. We show that the usual Nambu-Goto effective string description, including its standard higher-order corrections, fails to reproduce the data as the trace deformation is increased. Remarkably, deep in the reconfined regime the results are instead accurately described by the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution, corresponding to an effective string dominated by an extrinsic-curvature term. We further investigate the transverse profile of the chromo-electric flux tube and find significant deviations from the standard Yang-Mills behavior, including a substantial modification of the intrinsic width. Finally, we present an exploratory study of the phase diagram, finding evidence for a transition from a continuous to a first order reconfinement line as the deformation parameter increases. These results suggest that the reconfined phase realizes a qualitatively different effective-string regime from ordinary confinement.
