### [1] [Reconstructing the full kinematic dependence of GPDs from pseudo-distributions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21476)
*Hervé Dutrieux,Robert G. Edwards,Joe Karpie,Cédric Mezrag,Christopher Monahan,Kostas Orginos,Anatoly Radyushkin,David Richards,Eloy Romero,Savvas Zafeiropoulos*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a novel method for reconstructing the full dependence of unpolarized isovector proton GPDs from lattice QCD data using multidimensional Gaussian process regression, with an emphasis on extracting double distributions and assessing systematic uncertainties.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to enforce the Lorentz symmetry property of GPDs by directly extracting double distributions from lattice QCD data, which has not been done before. The authors also aim to improve the kinematic coverage and hadron momenta in their calculations, as well as to evaluate the impact of model dependence on the systematic uncertainty of the results.

Method: The method involves using multidimensional Gaussian process regression to regularize the inverse problem of reconstructing GPDs from lattice QCD data. The study uses a lattice ensemble with a specific pion mass and lattice spacing, and it includes larger hadron momenta up to 2.7 GeV to extract additional skewness-dependent moments of the GPDs.

Result: The result is the successful reconstruction of the full $(x, ξ, t)$ dependence of the unpolarized isovector proton GPDs $H^{u-d}$ and $E^{u-d}$. The authors were able to extract double distributions directly from the lattice data, which enforces the Lorentz symmetry property of GPDs. They also provide an assessment of the systematic uncertainty due to model dependence.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the proposed method provides a reliable way to reconstruct GPDs from lattice QCD data, while preserving the important physical properties such as Lorentz symmetry. The use of multidimensional Gaussian process regression allows for a robust treatment of the inverse problem, and the increased kinematic coverage enhances the precision of the extracted GPDs.

Abstract: We propose a reconstruction of the full $(x, ξ, t)$ dependence of unpolarized isovector proton generalized parton distributions (GPDs) $H^{u-d}$ and $E^{u-d}$ from lattice QCD data in the pseudo-distribution formalism. For the first time, we extract double distributions (DDs) directly from lattice data, enforcing therefore an important property of GPDs linked to Lorentz symmetry. We use the flexible framework of multidimensional Gaussian process regression to regularize the inverse problem and present an assessment of the impact of model dependence on the systematic uncertainty. Our lattice ensemble corresponds to a pion mass $m_π= 358$~MeV and a lattice spacing $a = 0.094$~fm. We use larger hadron momenta, up to 2.7~GeV, and kinematic coverage compared to our previous computations and extract additional skewness-dependent moments of the GPD.
