### [1] [Tame the Umklapp Processes in Real-Time Lattice Simulation for Hydrodynamics: An Ising Field Theory Study](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09984)
*Xiaojun Yao*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper calculates the real-time symmetric correlation function of the stress-energy tensor in a non-integrable Ising field theory using lattice Hamiltonian simulation, showing the emergence of sound modes and providing transport coefficients such as bulk viscosity and speed of sound.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to demonstrate the utility of real-time lattice Hamiltonian simulation for describing the process of hydrodynamization and for calculating transport coefficients without relying on perturbative methods.

Method: The authors used classical exact diagonalization and matrix product state tensor network methods to simulate the system and extract the ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density and the speed of sound at a specific temperature.

Result: The study found that Umklapp processes are suppressed in the scaling region, and it was able to extract a ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density (ζ/s) of 14.19 ± 0.90 and a speed of sound (c_s/c) of 0.76 ± 0.02 at a temperature T ≈ 7.14, relative to the mass of the lowest stable particle.

Conclusion: The research concludes with the successful demonstration of how real-time lattice Hamiltonian simulations can be used to describe the hydrodynamization process and to calculate transport coefficients nonperturbatively, providing a valuable tool for the study of quantum field theories.

Abstract: We calculate the real-time symmetric correlation function of the stress-energy tensor for a non-integrable Ising field theory consisting of three stable scalar particles via lattice Hamiltonian simulation. Using classical exact diagonalization and the matrix product state tensor network methods, we find that in the scaling region of the lattice theory, Umklapp processes are suppressed and the sound modes of relativistic hydrodynamics emerge at long wavelength and late time. The extracted ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density is $ζ/s=14.19\pm 0.90$ and the speed of sound is $c_s/c=0.76 \pm 0.02$ at the temperature $T\approx 7.14$ in units of the lowest stable particle's mass. Our study demonstrates the utility of real-time lattice Hamiltonian simulation for describing hydrodynamization and calculating transport coefficients nonperturbatively.

### [2] [Momentum Dependence of Heavy Quark Diffusion in a Thermal Gluonic Plasma on the Lattice](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10049)
*Harshit Pandey,Sayantan Sharma*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study investigates the behavior of a heavy quark in a thermal plasma of interacting gluons at high temperatures, using an effective QCD theory on a 3D lattice. A new numerical method is introduced to simulate the quark's dynamics for various initial momenta, enabling the extraction of drag and diffusion coefficients that depend on momentum in a non-perturbative, thermal, and non-Abelian plasma.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To understand the dynamics of a heavy quark within a thermal plasma of non-perturbatively interacting soft momentum gluons at high temperatures, and to determine the momentum dependence of the drag and diffusion coefficients in such a medium.

Method: An effective theory of QCD discretized on a three-dimensional lattice is used to model the thermal plasma. The researchers developed a numerical strategy to simulate the heavy quark's dynamics with varying initial momenta, allowing for the extraction of the momentum-dependent drag and diffusion coefficients in a non-Abelian plasma environment.

Result: The study presents a novel numerical approach to simulate the heavy quark's motion in a thermal plasma, which leads to the first-time extraction of the drag and diffusion coefficients as a function of the quark's momentum in a non-perturbative, thermal, and non-Abelian plasma setting.

Conclusion: The research provides a significant advancement in the simulation of heavy quark dynamics within a thermal plasma, offering insights into the momentum dependence of drag and diffusion coefficients, which are crucial for understanding the interaction between heavy quarks and the surrounding non-Abelian plasma.

Abstract: We study the dynamics of a heavy quark in a thermal plasma consisting of non-perturbatively interacting soft momentum gluons at high temperatures, described in terms of an effective theory of QCD discretized on a three-dimensional lattice. We propose a numerical strategy that allows us to simulate the dynamics of a heavy quark for different values of initial momenta in this thermalized plasma. This allows, for the first time, to extract the momentum dependence of the heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients in a non-perturbatively interacting thermal, non-Abelian plasma.

*Sinya Aoki,Hidenori Fukaya*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The authors rebut a comment by Matteo Giordano on their previous work, arguing that the counterexamples provided violate a key QCD assumption and contain a technical mistake.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Method: The authors analyze the counterexamples proposed by Giordano and identify that they do not adhere to the established QCD principle that gluonic observables must be analytic functions of the squared quark mass. They also highlight a specific technical error in Giordano's work.

Result: The authors demonstrate that the counterexamples are invalid due to the violation of the analyticity condition and the presence of a technical mistake, thereby upholding the integrity of their original argument.

Conclusion: The critiques from [1] are deemed invalid, as the counterexamples fail to satisfy the necessary conditions for QCD at high temperatures, and the identified technical mistake further undermines the opposing argument.

Abstract: We respond to the comment by Matteo Giordano [1] on our article [2]. We find -- and [1] itself acknowledges -- that the proposed counterexamples intended to refute our argument violate a crucial assumption of QCD at high temperatures, namely that every gluonic observable is an analytic function of the squared quark mass, $m^2$. We further point out a technical mistake found in [1]. We conclude that the arguments presented in [1] are not valid.

### [4] [Combined Analysis of Lattice QCD and Experimental Data on the Pion Transition Form Factor](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11181)
*Franziska Hagelstein,Danaheb Naomi Navarro Durán,Timon Esser,Vadim Lensky,Sotiris Pitelis,Vladyslava Sharkovska*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a feasibility study for combining lattice QCD and experimental data to evaluate the pion transition form factor, which is important for the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment. The combination of these datasets, using a global one-stage fitting approach, results in a significant reduction of uncertainty in the singly-virtual limit of the pion TFF, but a more modest improvement in the pion-pole contribution to the muon g-2 due to the dominance of the low-momentum transfer region.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation of this paper is to improve the precision of the pion transition form factor (TFF) for the accurate evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment. By leveraging the kinematic advantages of both LQCD and $e^+e^-$ scattering experiments, the authors aim to combine their complementary datasets into a statistically rigorous analysis.

Method: The method employed in this paper involves a global one-stage fitting approach based on the modified $z$-expansion, with synthetic jackknife replicate sampling and a normalized $χ^2$ weighting scheme. This approach allows for the integration of LQCD predictions for the doubly-virtual TFF and high-precision singly-virtual measurements from $e^+e^-$ scattering experiments.

Result: The result of the combined analysis shows that including experimental data significantly tightens the constraints on the pion TFF, reducing the uncertainty by up to a factor of three in the singly-virtual limit. However, the improvement in the pion-pole contribution to the muon g-2 is only a factor of 1.5, as the g-2 integral is dominated by the low-$Q^2$ region, which is already well constrained.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from this work is that the combination of LQCD and experimental data through a robust statistical method can lead to a considerable reduction in uncertainties for the pion TFF, particularly in the singly-virtual regime. Despite the smaller improvement in the pion-pole contribution to the muon g-2, the overall enhancement in precision contributes to a better understanding of the hadronic light-by-light scattering effect.

Abstract: The evaluation of the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment requires precise knowledge of the pion transition form factor (TFF). In this work, we present a feasibility study for a combined analysis of lattice QCD (LQCD) and experimental data. Our methodology is driven by the goal of combining complementary datasets to leverage their respective kinematic advantages: while LQCD provides robust predictions for the doubly-virtual TFF, $e^+e^-$ scattering experiments offer high-precision singly-virtual measurements up to large momentum transfers. To ensure a statistically rigorous combination, we implement a global one-stage fitting approach based on the modified $z$-expansion, utilizing synthetic jackknife replicate sampling and a normalized $χ^2$ weighting scheme. We demonstrate that the inclusion of experimental data substantially tightens the constraints on the pion TFF, yielding up to a factor of three reduction in uncertainty in the singly-virtual limit. In contrast, the uncertainty of the resulting pion-pole contribution to the muon $g-2$ improves by a factor of $1.5$. This more modest improvement reflects the fact that the $g-2$ integral is heavily dominated by the low-$Q^2$ region, which is already well constrained by physical normalization constraints.

</details>
