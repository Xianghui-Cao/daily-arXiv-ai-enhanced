### [1] [Gauge field flow for chiral gauge theories on a disk boundary](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05305)
*Jinlong Dang,Rohith Karur,Srimoyee Sen*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper proposes a concrete realization of the equation of motion flow for chiral gauge theories on a lattice, and demonstrates anomaly inflow and cancellation.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a non-perturbative formulation of 2n-dimensional chiral gauge theories by realizing chiral fermions on the boundary of a higher dimensional space and to extend this to a lattice setting while preserving gauge invariance and demonstrating anomaly cancellation.

Method: The authors use a square lattice to embed a disk manifold and apply an equation of motion flow. They also couple the flow gauge field to fermions on the lattice to show how anomalies are canceled through the inflow mechanism.

Result: The result is a successful demonstration of the anomaly inflow and cancellation mechanism on a lattice, which supports the proposed method for formulating chiral gauge theories non-perturbatively.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the proposed lattice-based approach can be used to model 2n-dimensional chiral gauge theories, with the potential to maintain gauge invariance and manage anomalies within the framework of a discrete spacetime.

Abstract: A recent non-perturbative formulation of $2n$ dimensional chiral gauge theories relies on realizing chiral fermions on the $2n$ dimensional boundary of a $2n+1$ dimensional disk manifold. It also requires extending boundary gauge configurations into the interior of the disk using some flow prescription that preserves 2n dimensional gauge invariance. In this paper we propose a concrete realization of the equation of motion flow with the disk embedded on a square lattice. In addition, we couple the flow gauge field to fermions and demonstrate the mechanism of anomaly inflow and anmaly cancellation at work on the lattice.

### [2] [Gauge field flow for chiral gauge theories on a slab](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05306)
*Jinlong Dang,Rohith Karur,Srimoyee Sen*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper discusses the formulation of chiral gauge theories using domain wall fermions on a lattice with a specific geometry, implementing this for n=1 and introducing an EOM flow. It also demonstrates current conservation and anomaly inflow.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to formulate chiral gauge theories in a way that decouples mirror fermions and to demonstrate the principles of current conservation and anomaly inflow on a lattice.

Method: The method involves using domain wall fermions on a 2n+1 dimensional Euclidean lattice with a slab geometry, extending gauge fields into an extra dimension via gradient flow, and implementing the construction for n=1 with background gauge fields. An additional EOM flow is also introduced and implemented.

Result: The results show that the proposed methods can be used to couple gauge fields to fermions, allowing for the demonstration of current conservation and anomaly inflow on the lattice.

Conclusion: The paper concludes by successfully demonstrating the lattice implementation of chiral gauge theories with the decoupling of mirror fermions and the successful illustration of current conservation and anomaly inflow mechanisms.

Abstract: The proposal to formulate chiral gauge theories using domain wall fermions on $2n+1$ dimensional Euclidean lattice with a slab geometry involves $2n$ dimensional dynamical gauge fields residing on one of the domain walls. The gauge fields are extended into the extra dimension using gradient flow decoupling the mirror fermions on the anti-wall. We implement this construction on the lattice for $n=1$ in the presence of $2n$ dimensional background gauge fields. We also formulate and implement an additional gauge field flow proposal, where the gauge fields satisfy $2n+1$ dimensional equation of motion away from the domain wall, known as the EOM (equation of motion) flow. In both cases, we couple the gauge fields to fermions and demonstrate how current conservation and anomaly inflow work on the lattice.

</details>
