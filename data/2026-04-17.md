### [1] [An efficient Wavelet-Based Hamiltonian Formulation of Quantum Field Theories using Flow-Equations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14594)
*Mrinmoy Basak,Debsubhra Chakraborty,Nilmani Mathur*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a new method for analyzing quantum field theories by using a Daubechies wavelet basis and similarity renormalization group (SRG) flow-equation methods, which reduces the Hamiltonian's dimensionality and decouples degrees of freedom. The technique is applied to free scalar field theory to efficiently compute its low-lying energy spectrum.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop an efficient framework for analyzing quantum field theories that can reduce the computational complexity when calculating the low-lying energy spectrum of such theories, particularly by decoupling degrees of freedom across different scales.

Method: The method involves reformulating the free scalar field theory using a Daubechies wavelet basis in position space, organizing the Hamiltonian into fixed-resolution blocks, and applying SRG flow-equation methods to decouple resolution modes, leading to a block diagonalized Hamiltonian.

Result: The result is a block-diagonalized Hamiltonian with each block associated with a fixed resolution, from which the low-energy spectrum can be extracted, even as the resolution increases, thus significantly reducing the computational cost.

Conclusion: The proposed effective Hamiltonian formulation, combined with SRG flow-equation methods, provides a computationally efficient way to analyze quantum field theories and calculate their low-lying energy spectra.

Abstract: We propose an effective Hamiltonian formulation of quantum field theories using a Daubechies wavelet basis in position space. Combined with flow-equation methods of the similarity renormalization group (SRG), this approach provides an efficient framework for analyzing quantum field theories by reducing the dimensionality of the Hamiltonian and systematically decoupling degrees of freedom across scales. As an application, the free scalar field theory has been reformulated within this framework to calculate the low-lying energy spectrum of the theory. These basis elements are known to transform the free scalar field theory into a theory of coupled localized oscillators, each of which is labeled by a location and a resolution index. In this representation, the Hamiltonian is naturally organized into fixed-resolution blocks, alongside blocks associated with the interactions between different resolutions. To decouple the different resolution modes and obtain a block diagonalized Hamiltonian with each block associated with a fixed resolution, the flow equation approach of SRG is applied. Finally, we demonstrate that with increasing resolution, the low-energy spectrum can be extracted from the effective lowest-resolution block of the Hamiltonian, leading to a significant reduction in computational cost.

### [2] [A minimal implementation of Yang-Mills theory on a digital quantum computer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15132)
*Georg Bergner,Masanori Hanada,Emanuele Mendicelli*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: Researchers have developed a minimal implementation of SU(N) pure Yang-Mills theory in 3+1 dimensions for digital quantum simulation, with the aim of achieving quantum advantage. They introduce simplified Hamiltonians and methods to improve convergence to the infinite mass limit, reducing resource requirements. The approach is validated through Monte Carlo simulations, supporting its practicality for simulating non-Abelian gauge theories.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to create a more efficient and less resource-intensive method for the digital quantum simulation of SU(N) pure Yang-Mills theory in 3+1 dimensions, aiming to achieve a quantum advantage and to make non-Abelian gauge theory simulations more practical.

Method: The researchers build on an orbifold lattice simulation protocol that allows for logarithmic scaling in local Hilbert space truncation. They introduce further simplified Hamiltonians and test methods to enhance convergence to the infinite mass limit, which reduces the need for a large scalar mass. For the SU(2) case, they exploit the embedding of SU(2) into R^4 to further cut down on resource requirements. Monte Carlo simulations are used to verify the accuracy of the new theoretical improvements.

Result: The results show that the introduced simplifications and methods effectively reduce the resources needed for the simulation while maintaining the accuracy of the theory. This is evidenced by the Monte Carlo simulations of the Euclidean path integral, which serve as a benchmark for the new analytical improvements.

Conclusion: The study concludes that the noncompact-variable-based approach, along with the newly introduced simplifications and improved convergence, provides a practical and efficient framework for the quantum simulation of non-Abelian gauge theories, such as SU(N) pure Yang-Mills theory.

Abstract: We present a minimal implementation of SU($N$) pure Yang-Mills theory in $3+1$ dimensions for digital quantum simulation, designed to enable quantum advantage. Building on the orbifold lattice simulation protocol with logarithmic scaling in the local Hilbert-space truncation, we introduce further simplified Hamiltonians. Furthermore, we test simple methods that improve the convergence to the infinite mass limit, thereby removing the requirement of a large scalar mass to obtain the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian. For the SU(2) theory, we can cut the resource requirement further by utilizing the embedding of $\mathrm{SU}(2)\cong\mathrm{S}^3$ into $\mathbb{R}^4$. Monte Carlo simulations of the Euclidean path integral were used to benchmark the accuracy of these new analytical improvements to the theory. These results provide further support for the noncompact-variable-based approach as a practical framework for quantum simulation of non-Abelian gauge theories.

### [3] [Charmonium radiative transitions to dileptons from lattice QCD: The case of $h_c \to η_c \ell^+\ell^-$ and $χ_{c1} \to J/ψ\,\ell^+\ell^-$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15256)
*D. Bečirević,R. Di Palma,R. Frezzotti,G. Gagliardi,V. Lubicz,F. Sanfilippo,N. Tantalo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a lattice QCD study of dilepton production in charmonium transitions, with results for $h_c \to \eta_c \ell^+ \ell^-$ and $\chi_{c1} \to J/\psi\ell^+ \ell^-$ decays. The authors provide the first fully dynamical lattice QCD predictions for these decay rates, including differential distributions and angular observables, which are compared to experimental data where available.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide benchmark theoretical predictions for dilepton decay rates and related observables in charmonium transitions, using lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) simulations, which can be used to guide and test future experimental studies.

Method: The method involves computing hadronic matrix elements using gauge field configurations from the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration with $N_f = 2+1+1$ dynamical Wilson--Clover twisted-mass fermions at four different lattice spacings. Simulations are conducted with physical quark masses, except on the coarsest lattice. A continuum extrapolation is performed to obtain final results.

Result: The results include the prediction of decay rates for $h_c \to \eta_c e^+ e^-$, $h_c \to \eta_c \mu^+ \mu^-$, $\chi_{c1} \to J/\psi e^+ e^-$, and $\chi_{c1} \to J/\psi \mu^+ \mu^-$. Additionally, differential decay widths as functions of the dilepton invariant mass and angular observables are provided. The $\chi_{c1}$ decay rates agree well with experimental data, while the $h_c \to \eta_c e^+ e^-$ rate is found to be about $3\sigma$ higher than the BESIII result.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the study provides the first fully dynamical lattice QCD predictions for dilepton decay rates in $h_c$ and $\chi_{c1}$ charmonium transitions, along with their differential distributions and angular observables. These predictions serve as benchmarks for future experimental studies, and the discrepancy with the BESIII result for the $h_c$ decay suggests a need for further investigation.

Abstract: We present a lattice QCD study of dilepton production in charmonium transitions, specifically focusing on the $1^{+-} \to 0^{-+}$ and $1^{++} \to 1^{--}$ processes: $h_c \to η_c \ell^+ \ell^-$ and $χ_{c1} \to J/ψ\ell^+ \ell^-$, where $\ell = e, μ$. The relevant hadronic matrix elements are computed using gauge field configurations generated by the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration with $N_f = 2+1+1$ dynamical Wilson--Clover twisted-mass fermions at four lattice spacings. Simulations are performed at physical dynamical $u$, $d$, $s$, and $c$ quark masses, except for the coarsest lattice, where the lightest sea quark mass corresponds to a slightly heavier pion mass. A controlled continuum extrapolation is carried out. In the continuum limit for the $h_c$ decays, we obtain $Γ(h_c \to η_c e^+ e^-) = 5.45(19)~\mathrm{keV}$, and $Γ(h_c \to η_c μ^+ μ^-) = 0.635(22)~\mathrm{keV}$. For the $χ_{c1}$ decays, we find: $Γ(χ_{c1} \to J/ψe^+ e^-)= 2.869(90)~\mathrm{keV}$, and $Γ(χ_{c1} \to J/ψμ^+ μ^-) = 0.1993(72)~\mathrm{keV}$. Our results for the $χ_{c1}$ decays show good compatibility with experimental data. However, our prediction for the $h_c \to η_c e^+ e^- $ decay rate is approximately $3σ$ larger than the BESIII result. We also present predictions for the differential decay widths as functions of the dilepton invariant mass, $q^2$, and for angular observables sensitive to longitudinal transition form factors, which are inaccessible in radiative decays with real photon emission. These results constitute the first fully dynamical lattice QCD predictions for dilepton decay rates in $h_c$ and $χ_{c1}$ charmonium transitions, including their differential distributions and angular observables. They provide benchmark predictions for future experimental studies.
