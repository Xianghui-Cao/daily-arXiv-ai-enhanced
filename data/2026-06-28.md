### [1] [Isospin breaking corrections to a lattice QCD calculation of $\varepsilon'$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26336)
*Norman H. Christ,Erik Lundstrum*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper discusses the significant impact of electromagnetism and isospin-breaking light quark mass difference on the direct CP violation parameter ε', which can be as large as 25%. It shows that all enhanced effects by the ΔI=1/2 rule can be captured in a QCD + QED lattice calculation for photon energies between 0.5-2.0 GeV, with short-distance and low-energy photons having minimal or no enhancement. An estimate suggests the effect of low-energy photons on ε' is around 0.5%.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand and quantify the effects of electromagnetism and isospin-breaking due to the light quark mass difference on the direct CP violation parameter (ε') in kaon decay, especially considering the enhancement by the ΔI = 1/2 rule, which may be as significant as 25%.

Method: A lattice QCD calculation is used to distinguish and identify the effects of isospin breaking on kaon decay features, particularly focusing on the range of photon energy between 0.5-2.0 GeV where the ΔI=1/2 rule enhancements are most relevant. The analysis also includes the treatment of short-distance and low-energy photons, as well as an estimation using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) for the contribution of low-energy photons.

Result: The results show that all ΔI=1/2 rule-enhanced effects can be accounted for within the specified photon energy range. Short-distance and infrared photons do not contribute significantly to ε', while low-energy photons have a minor effect, estimated to be about 0.5% based on ChPT. This indicates that the main contributions to ε' from isospin breaking and electromagnetism are concentrated in the intermediate photon energy range.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the QCD + QED lattice calculations can effectively capture the ΔI=1/2 rule-enhanced effects on the direct CP violation parameter ε' within a specific photon energy range. The overall impact of isospin breaking and electromagnetic effects on ε' is substantial, with the bulk of the effect coming from intermediate photon energies, and only a small contribution from low-energy photons.

Abstract: Because of the $ΔI = 1/2$ rule, the effects of electromagnetism and the isospin-breaking light quark mass difference on the direct CP violation parameter $\varepsilon'$ may be as large as 25\% and are consequently of immediate interest. In a lattice QCD calculation the effects of isospin breaking on the various features of kaon decay can be clearly distinguished and those effects enhanced by the $ΔI=1/2$ rule on $\varepsilon'$ explicitly identified. We show that all such enhanced effects can be captured in a QCD + QED lattice calculation in which the exchanged photon has an energy in an accessible, intermediate range between 0.5-2.0 GeV. Short-distance effects ($2.0 \mathrm{\ GeV} \lesssim E_γ$), usually treated in QCD and electroweak perturbation theory, are not enhanced by the $ΔI=1/2$ rule, beyond the well-understood contribution of the two electroweak penguin operators. Infrared photons do not contribute to $\varepsilon'$ while low-energy photons ($E_γ\lesssim 0.5$ GeV) are not $ΔI=1/2$ rule enhanced or are suppressed by one order in chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). An explicit ChPT estimate of this low-energy-photon contribution, a contribution that is difficult to determine in a finite-volume lattice calculation, suggests that the effect on $\varepsilon'$ is on the order of 0.5\%.

### [2] [Performance of Low Mode Averaging on Twisted-Mass Fermion Ensembles at the physical pion mass point](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27072)
*Constantia Alexandrou,Simone Bacchio,Antonio Evangelista,Roberto Frezzotti,Francesca Margari,Francesco Sanfilippo,Christian Schneider*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study evaluates the performance of low-mode averaging (LMA) on twisted-mass fermion ensembles, examining its theoretical framework and practical cost-effectiveness. It includes numerical studies of meson and baryon observables, comparisons between exact and approximate LMA methods, and the calculation of the renormalised chiral condensate, yielding specific values for Σ_R and the low-energy constant ḡ_1.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to assess the effectiveness and cost-efficiency of low-mode averaging (LMA) techniques in the context of modern lattice QCD, specifically with near-physical quark masses, and to provide a precise determination of the renormalised chiral condensate and related low-energy constants.

Method: The method involves a numerical study using twisted-mass fermion ensembles, comparing two implementations of LMA—an exact approach and an approximate variant—on meson two-point functions, including the vector-vector correlator. For baryons, the focus is on the exact LMA approach, studying both two- and three-point functions. Additionally, the eigenvalue density of the massless Wilson operator is computed to determine the renormalised chiral condensate via the Banks-Casher relation.

Result: The results show that the LMA technique provides noise and cost reductions at large Euclidean times, particularly for baryon systems. The renormalised chiral condensate is determined to be √[3]{Σ_R}=269.5(4.5) MeV, and the scale-independent low-energy constant is found to be ḡ_1=5.2(1.1).

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the low-mode averaging (LMA) technique is effective in reducing noise and costs in the computation of light-quark hadronic observables, and it has provided a precise value for the renormalised chiral condensate and the low-energy constant, which are important for understanding the non-perturbative aspects of QCD.

Abstract: We study the performance of low-mode averaging (LMA) on twisted-mass fermion ensembles at near-physical quark masses, assessing both its theoretical framework and practical cost-effectiveness in modern lattice QCD. In particular, we present a numerical study of light-quark meson and baryon observables. For mesons, we analyse two-point functions, including the vector-vector correlator relevant for the hadronic vacuum polarisation contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, comparing two implementations of LMA: an exact approach based on explicit low modes and an approximate, high-statistics variant using multigrid techniques. For baryons, we restrict to the exact approach and study both two- and three-point functions, quantifying the resulting noise and cost reductions at large Euclidean times. In addition, we compute the eigenvalue density of the massless Wilson operator and determine the renormalised chiral condensate via the Banks-Casher relation, obtaining $\sqrt[3]{Σ_{\mathrm{R}}}=269.5(4.5)~\mathrm{MeV}$ for $N_f{=}2{+}1{+}1$ isospin-symmetric QCD at a scale $2~\mathrm{GeV}$ in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme, with an uncertainty dominated by the chiral extrapolation. Additionally, from the pion-mass dependence of $Σ_{\mathrm{R}}$, we extract the scale-independent low-energy constant $\bar{h}_1=5.2(1.1)$.

</details>
