### [1] [Topological Susceptibility and QCD at Finite Theta Angle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28035)
*Claudio Bonanno,Claudio Bonati,Massimo D'Elia*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper provides an educational introduction to the theoretical aspects of topology and θ-dependence in QCD, their relevance in the Standard Model and beyond, and reviews analytical predictions and recent numerical results from lattice simulations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to offer a pedagogical introduction to the concepts of topology and θ-dependence in Quantum Chromo-Dynamics (QCD), and to highlight their importance in both the Standard Model and beyond, including unresolved issues like the strong CP problem and proposed solutions such as the axion.

Method: The authors use a combination of chiral effective theories, large-N arguments, semiclassical methods, and Monte Carlo simulations of lattice-discretized theory to analyze and predict θ-dependence in QCD. They also provide an overview of the latest numerical results concerning QCD topology.

Result: The paper delivers a comprehensive understanding of the theoretical underpinnings of QCD topology and θ-dependence, along with a summary of the main analytic predictions and the most recent numerical data from lattice QCD simulations.

Conclusion: The work concludes with a synthesis of the current state of knowledge on the topic, providing insights into the phenomenological implications for particle physics and suggesting avenues for future research.

Abstract: In this chapter we provide a pedagogical introduction to the main theoretical aspects related to topology and $θ$-dependence in Quantum Chromo-Dynamics (QCD), and to their phenomenological relevance in the Standard Model ($η^\prime$ physics, neutron electric dipole moment) and beyond (strong CP problem and the axion solution). We then provide an overview of the main analytic predictions for $θ$-dependence obtained using several different approaches (chiral effective theories, large-$N$ arguments, semiclassical methods) and their regimes of validity, as well as a selection of the most recent numerical results about QCD topology obtained via Monte Carlo simulations of the lattice-discretized theory.
