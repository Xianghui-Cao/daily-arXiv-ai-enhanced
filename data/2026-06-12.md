### [1] [Numerical Hints for Dyon Condensation at $θ=2π$ via Wilson-'t Hooft Loops in $SU(2)$ Yang-Mills Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13428)
*Hiromasa Watanabe,Issaku Kanamori,Okuto Morikawa,Yuki Nagai,Yuya Tanizaki,Akio Tomiya*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study explores the nontrivial realization of 2π periodicity in Yang-Mills theories, identifying different SPT states at θ=0 and 2π. Using Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators and a 1-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow, it finds numerical evidence for dyon condensation at θ=2π, supporting recent theoretical predictions.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to examine the distinct symmetry-protected topological (SPT) states of confinement vacua at θ=0 and 2π by investigating the behavior of Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators, as suggested by the theory of generalized global symmetries.

Method: The researchers used the SU(2) Wilson lattice gauge action to measure Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators at θ=2π, and they employed a 1-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow to identify the gauge topological charge in the presence of defects, which allowed them to analyze the long-distance behaviors of these operators.

Result: The result shows numerical evidence that supports the theoretical prediction of dyon condensation at θ=2π, rather than monopole condensation, indicating a nontrivial realization of 2π periodicity in Yang-Mills theories.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from the study is that there is a nontrivial distinction between the SPT states at θ=0 and 2π, with the observed dyon condensation at θ=2π aligning with the recent developments in the understanding of generalized global symmetries and their implications on the SPT states of confinement vacua.

Abstract: Yang-Mills theories at $θ$ and $θ+2π$ are unitarily equivalent, but their $2π$ periodicity has a nontrivial realization. Recent developments in generalized global symmetries show that confinement vacua at $θ=0$ and $2π$ should belong to different symmetry-protected topological (SPT) states with the $1$-form center symmetry. For its examination, we measure the Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators at $θ=2π$ for the $SU(2)$ Wilson lattice gauge action and discuss their long-distance behaviors. This requires us to identify the gauge topological charge in the presence of defects, and we employ the $1$-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow to smear lattice gauge fields. We then obtain numerical evidence consistent with dyon condensation at $θ=2π$, rather than monopole condensation, as theoretically predicted.

### [2] [New results on gauge field decomposition in SU(3) gluodynamics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13490)
*V. Bornyakov,V. Goy,I. Kudrov*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper examines the decomposition of nonabelian gauge fields into Abelian and modified nonabelian components in SU(3) lattice gluodynamics, finding that the sum of the potentials from these components differs significantly from the original potential, except when using a specific set of Gribov copies.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To understand the role of Abelian monopoles in the nonabelian gauge field and their contribution to the static potential after removing them through Maximal Abelian gauge fixing in SU(3) lattice theory.

Method: Decomposition of the nonabelian gauge field into an Abelian component (created by Abelian monopoles) and a modified nonabelian component (with monopoles removed), followed by computing the static potential for the original and decomposed fields at two different lattice spacings, and comparing the results with and without optimal gauge fixing.

Result: The study finds that the sum of the potentials from the Abelian and modified nonabelian components deviates substantially from the original static potential, but this deviation is reduced when using a particular set of Gribov copies, indicating a more accurate decomposition.

Conclusion: The decomposition of the nonabelian gauge field into its Abelian and modified nonabelian parts does not accurately reproduce the original static potential, suggesting that the treatment of Gribov copies is crucial for the precision of the decomposition.

Abstract: We study decomposition of the nonabelian gauge field into the Abelian component created by Abelian monopoles and the modified nonabelian components with monopoles removed after fixing the Maximal Abelian gauge in SU(3) lattice gluodynamics. We compute the static potential V (r) for the original gauge field and for its components V_mon and V_mod at two values of the lattice spacing. We confirm that with optimal gauge fixing the sum V_mon + V_mod deviates substantially from V(r). We show that this decomposition of the static potential is satisfied with good precision at all distances when we use another set of Gribov copies.

</details>
