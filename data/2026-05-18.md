### [1] [Spectral reconstruction from Euclidean lattice correlators through singular value decomposition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15674)
*Ryutaro Tsuji,Shoji Hashimoto*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper discusses a method for reconstructing spectral densities from Euclidean lattice correlators using singular value decomposition (SVD) to manage the ill-posed inverse Laplace transform, which allows for controlled uncertainties and bounded systematic errors. In the limit of large and continuous ranges, the SVD basis approaches the Mellin transform, providing a representation independent of lattice parameters.

Motivation: The motivation is to address the inherent ill-conditioning of the inverse Laplace transform when applied to numerical data with statistical uncertainties, in order to extract the maximum amount of information from the imaginary-time dependence of correlators.

Method: The authors use singular value decomposition (SVD) on the kernel function exp(-ωt) defined on discrete sets of imaginary times t and energies ω. By retaining only the significant components associated with the largest singular values, they reconstruct smeared spectral functions while controlling uncertainties and bounding the systematic error due to truncation.

Result: The results show that by using SVD, one can effectively reconstruct smeared spectral functions with controlled uncertainties and bound the systematic error arising from the truncation of less significant components. Additionally, as the ranges of t and ω become infinitely large and continuous, the SVD basis tends towards the Mellin transform, offering a parameter-independent representation of the spectrum.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the SVD-based approach provides a robust method for reconstructing spectral functions from Euclidean lattice correlators, with the ability to control uncertainties and bound systematic errors, and it converges to a Mellin-transform-like representation in the appropriate limit.

Abstract: Reconstructing spectral densities from Euclidean lattice correlators requires an inverse Laplace transform, which is inherently ill-conditioned when applied to numerical data with statistical uncertainties. The maximum amount of information that can be extracted from the imaginary-time dependence of correlators can be characterized by the singular value decomposition (SVD) of the kernel function $\exp(-ωt)$ defined on discrete sets of imaginary times $t$ and energies $ω$. The SVD provides orthogonal basis functions in both the $t$- and $ω$-spaces, while the singular values determine the magnitude of their contributions to the correlators. By retaining only the components associated with the largest singular values, for which the correlator data remain statistically significant, one can reconstruct smeared spectral functions with controlled uncertainties. The systematic error arising from the truncation can also be bounded under reasonable assumptions. In the limit where the ranges of $t$ and $ω$ become infinitely large and continuous, the SVD basis approaches the Mellin transform, allowing a representation of the smeared spectrum that is independent of the details of the lattice parameters.
