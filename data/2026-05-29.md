### [1] [Electromagnetic pion mass splitting using a Pauli-Villars-regulated photon propagator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29902)
*Alessandro De Santis,Dominik Erb,Harvey B. Meyer*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the charged-neutral pion mass splitting, employing a Pauli-Villars regulated photon propagator to manage finite-volume effects and UV cutoff. The result, 4.56(22) MeV, aligns with experimental data, validating the method for electromagnetic corrections in similar studies.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to validate a recently proposed framework using a Pauli-Villars (PV) regulated photon propagator for calculating the charged-neutral pion mass splitting at order O(α_em), which avoids power-law finite-volume effects and allows for an easier treatment of the infinite-volume limit.

Method: The authors use CLS ensembles to perform the calculation, studying finite-volume effects, the continuum limit, and extrapolating to the physical point for various values of the scale Λ. They also decompose the result into elastic and inelastic contributions using the Cottingham formula, before removing the cutoff scale Λ to obtain the final result.

Result: The final result after removing the cutoff scale Λ is M_π+ - M_π0 = 4.56(22) MeV, which is in good agreement with the experimental measurement, thus validating the formalism used in the study.

Conclusion: The calculation validates the effectiveness of the PV-regulated photon propagator in the context of lattice QCD, providing a well-controlled setting for electromagnetic corrections and offering insights into applying this method to other observables.

Abstract: We present a lattice QCD calculation of the charged-neutral pion mass splitting $M_{π^+} - M_{π^0}$ at $\mathcal{O}(α_\mathrm{em})$ using a recently proposed framework based on a Pauli-Villars (PV) regulated photon propagator defined in the continuum and infinite-volume limit, with $Λ$ acting as an additional UV cutoff scale. The use of this propagator avoids power-law finite-volume effects, allowing for a straightforward treatment of the infinite-volume limit. We perform the calculation using CLS ensembles, studying finite-volume effects, the continuum limit and the extrapolation to the physical point for several values of the scale $Λ$. By means of the Cottingham formula, we further decompose the result into elastic and inelastic contributions at fixed $Λ$. Our final result, after removing the cutoff scale $Λ$, is $M_{π^+} - M_{π^0} = 4.56(22)$ MeV, in good agreement with the experimental measurement. This calculation serves as a validation of the formalism in a well-controlled setting and offers useful insights into the application of electromagnetic corrections to other observables.

### [2] [Higher Mellin Moments of the Unpolarized PDF of the Pion and the Kaon from Lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.29998)
*Constantia Alexandrou,Simone Bacchio,Priyajit Jana,Marcus Petschlies,Luis Alberto Rodriguez Chacon,Gregoris Spanoudes,Fernanda Steffens,Carsten Urbach,Urs Wenger*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents the Mellin moments of the unpolarized parton distribution function (PDF) for pion and kaon up to the fourth order, using a specific gauge ensemble. The valence pion and kaon PDFs are reconstructed, and the results are compared with other lattice QCD and phenomenological determinations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a more accurate and detailed understanding of the parton distribution functions for pions and kaons, which are fundamental in particle physics, by computing their Mellin moments to a higher order than previously done and comparing them with existing studies.

Method: The method involves using a $N_f=2+1+1$ gauge ensemble of twisted mass fermions with quark masses set close to their physical values, and then reconstructing the valence pion and kaon PDFs from the connected contributions to the three Mellin moments.

Result: The results include the Mellin moments of the pion and kaon PDFs up to the fourth order, as well as the reconstructed valence PDFs, which are then compared with other lattice QCD calculations and phenomenological models.

Conclusion: The conclusion suggests that the computed Mellin moments and the reconstructed PDFs for pions and kaons are consistent with other theoretical and experimental data, contributing to the validation and refinement of the current understanding of hadron structure.

Abstract: We present results on the Mellin moments of the unpolarized parton distribution function (PDF) of the pion and kaon up to the fourth order. The computation is done using one $N_f=2+1+1$ gauge ensemble of twisted mass fermions with quark masses tuned to approximately their physical values. We reconstruct the valence pion and kaon PDFs using the connected contributions to the three Mellin moments. We compare our results on the Mellin moments and the reconstructed PDFs with other lattice QCD and phenomenological determinations.

### [3] [Mellin Moments of the Unpolarized Gluon PDF in the Proton from Nonlocal Operators in Lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30193)
*Joseph Delmar,Krzysztof Cichy,Martha Constantinou,Yong Zhao*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a lattice QCD determination of the Mellin moments of the unpolarized gluon parton distribution function in the proton, using an Nf=2+1+1 ensemble. The study extracts ratios of higher-order gluon moments to the gluon momentum fraction and investigates systematic effects, providing a determination of the ratio ⟨x^3⟩_g/⟨x⟩_g at 2 GeV with uncertainties.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a precise determination of the Mellin moments of the unpolarized gluon parton distribution function, which is essential for understanding the structure of the proton and improving the accuracy of high-energy physics processes predictions.

Method: The method involves using lattice QCD with an Nf=2+1+1 ensemble of maximally twisted mass fermions and Iwasaki-improved gauge action. The analysis uses nonlocal gluon operators with boosted proton states, applies the OPE to extract moment ratios, and studies systematic effects and scale evolution using DGLAP equations.

Result: The result is a determination of the ratio of the third Mellin moment to the first Mellin moment (⟨x^3⟩_g/⟨x⟩_g) of the gluon parton distribution function at a scale of 2 GeV, including a comprehensive assessment of uncertainties from both statistical and theoretical sources.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the study successfully provides a lattice QCD determination of the gluon parton distribution function's Mellin moments, with a focus on the ratio ⟨x^3⟩_g/⟨x⟩_g, and offers insights into the associated systematic and statistical uncertainties, contributing to the field of high-energy physics.

Abstract: We present a lattice QCD determination of the Mellin moments of the unpolarized gluon parton distribution function in the proton. The analysis is based on matrix elements of nonlocal gluon operators coupled to momentum-boosted proton states. The calculation relies on an $N_f=2+1+1$ ensemble of maximally twisted mass fermions with clover improvement and the Iwasaki-improved gauge action, at a pion mass of approximately 260 MeV. Working within the short-distance operator product expansion (OPE) of the reduced gluon Ioffe-time distribution, we extract ratios of higher-order gluon moments, $\langle x^n\rangle$ with $n>1$, to the gluon momentum fraction, $\langle x\rangle$. We investigate systematic effects associated with the truncation of the order of moment in the OPE, the choice of minimum and maximum Wilson-line separations entering the analysis, and the treatment of mixing with the quark-singlet under perturbative matching. The stability of the extracted moments is further studied under scale evolution using DGLAP equations, allowing us to assess uncertainties related to perturbative truncation by varying the scale. Our work provides a determination of the ratio $\langle x^3\rangle_g/\langle x\rangle_g$ at a scale of 2 GeV, with uncertainties that account for both statistical and the dominant theoretical systematic uncertainties.
