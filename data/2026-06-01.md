### [1] [Seeded bubble nucleation on the lattice](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31194)
*Simone Blasi,Andreas Ekstedt,Jaakko Hällfors,Kari Rummukainen*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents the first non-perturbative lattice calculation of the bubble nucleation rate caused by topological defects in a first-order phase transition, using the cubic anisotropy model. It finds good agreement with semi-classical predictions.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To determine the bubble nucleation rate seeded by topological defects during a first order phase transition, which is important for understanding the dynamics of phase transitions in the early universe and particle physics models, such as electroweak theory.

Method: The authors use the cubic anisotropy model to simulate a 2+1 dimensional spacetime where bubbles are initiated by domain walls. They perform a lattice simulation to calculate the nucleation rate and compare it with a semi-classical prediction that includes the fluctuation determinant beyond spherical symmetry.

Result: The results from the lattice simulation show a very good match with the semi-classical prediction over the range of parameters considered, validating the theoretical approach and providing a benchmark for future studies.

Conclusion: The study provides a novel non-perturbative method to compute the bubble nucleation rate and confirms the reliability of the effective field theory on domain walls, including fluctuations, for describing the process.

Abstract: We provide the first non-perturbative lattice determination of the bubble nucleation rate as seeded by topological defects during a first order phase transition. Our case of study is the cubic anisotropy model, which can mimic the Higgs-plus-singlet setup for the electroweak theory, in $d=2+1$ spacetime dimensions, where bubbles are seeded by (line-like) domain walls. We compare the nucleation rate from the lattice with the semi-classical prediction based on the effective field theory living on the domain walls, including for the first time the fluctuation determinant away from spherical symmetry. Our results show very good agreement across all the considered parameter space.
