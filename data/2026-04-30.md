### [1] [Study on the systematic effects on $b \to c$ inclusive semileptonic decays](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26381)
*Alessandro Barone,Ahmed Elgaziari,Shoji Hashimoto,Zhi Hu,Andreas Jüttner,Takashi Kaneko,Ryan Kellermann*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper discusses the use of lattice QCD to calculate the inclusive semileptonic decay $B_s \to X_c \, l ν_l$, aiming to resolve discrepancies in CKM matrix element $|V_{cb}|$ determinations. It examines systematic uncertainties and proposes a method to isolate excited-state contributions, with results from Chebyshev reconstruction.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to address the discrepancy between inclusive and exclusive determinations of the CKM matrix element $|V_{cb}|$ by calculating the inclusive semileptonic decay $B_s \to X_c \, l ν_l$ using lattice QCD and by investigating the main sources of systematic uncertainty.

Method: The method involves using lattice QCD for the calculation, examining the impact of Jacobi smearing, source-sink separation, and the intrinsic uncertainties of the inclusive reconstruction. The researchers also propose a technique to treat ground-state contributions as an exclusive decay and focus on reconstructing only the excited-state contributions using Chebyshev reconstruction.

Result: The result is that by isolating the excited-state contributions, the systematic effects are expected to be suppressed where these contributions are sub-dominant, leading to a more controlled computation of the inclusive decay rate. Preliminary results based on Chebyshev reconstruction are presented as part of a larger effort towards a complete computation in the continuum and infinite-volume limits.

Conclusion: The conclusion suggests that the approach taken can help in reducing systematic uncertainties in the determination of the CKM matrix element $|V_{cb}|$, potentially providing a more accurate value through the control of excited-state contributions and the use of advanced reconstruction techniques.

Abstract: We discuss the calculation of the inclusive semileptonic decay for the process $B_s \to X_c \, l ν_l$ using lattice QCD. This calculation could be decisive in understanding the long-standing tension between inclusive and exclusive determinations of the CKM matrix element, $|V_{cb}|$. In this talk, we investigate the main sources of systematic uncertainty in these decays, including the impact of Jacobi smearing at the source and sink, variations in source-sink separation, and the intrinsic uncertainties of the inclusive reconstruction method itself. In addition, we explain how we can restrict the reconstruction of the inclusive decay rate to just the excited-state contributions. This is achieved by treating the ground-state contributions as an exclusive decay with well-controlled conventional techniques. Systematic effects from the reconstruction then only affect excited-state contributions. Where these are sub-dominant, a suppression of systematic effects is expected. We show results based on Chebyshev reconstruction, which are part of a larger effort towards a first phenomenologically relevant computation of the inclusive decay rate in the continuum and infinite-volume limits.

### [2] [Thermodynamics of magnetized matter in hot and dense QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26715)
*Bastian B. Brandt,Gergely Endrodi*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This chapter offers an educational overview of quark and gluon behavior under extreme conditions, such as high temperature, nonzero isospin density, and strong electromagnetic fields, using lattice QCD simulations and effective theories, with a discussion on open questions and unexplored areas.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a pedagogical introduction to the physics of quarks and gluons in extreme conditions, which are pertinent to high-energy heavy-ion collisions, neutron stars, and the early Universe's evolution.

Method: The method involves the use of first-principles simulations through lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) and includes discussions on calculations from effective theories like chiral perturbation theory.

Result: The result is a comprehensive review that covers existing results and provides an outlook on open questions and uncharted territories within the field of QCD under extreme conditions.

Conclusion: The conclusion suggests that while significant progress has been made in understanding QCD under extreme conditions, there are still many open questions and unexplored parameter regions that require further investigation.

Abstract: This chapter, to appear in the section on QCD under extreme conditions within the Encyclopedia of Nuclear Physics, aims to provide a pedagogical introduction to the physics of quarks and gluons in the presence of high temperature, nonzero (isospin) density and strong background electromagnetic fields. Extreme conditions of these types are relevant for the description of high-energy heavy-ion collisions, neutron stars and their mergers, as well as the evolution of the early Universe in its first microsecond. Most of the existing results on this topic have been obtained by means of first-principles simulations of the discretized theory of the strong interactions, lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). This lays the focus of this review chapter, although various calculations within effective theories of QCD -- most notably chiral perturbation theory -- are also discussed. Furthermore, we provide an outlook concerning open questions and yet uncharted parameter regions within this fascinating system.
