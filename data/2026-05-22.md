### [1] [Normalizing flows for all-orders QED corrections in lattice field theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22444)
*Nils Hermansson-Truedsson,Gurtej Kanwar*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A novel framework using normalizing-flow transformations for field configurations is developed, enabling all-orders QED corrections in lattice field theory without the need for extra Monte Carlo sampling. It's demonstrated to be effective in 2, 3, and 4 dimensions, with potential for application in theories with fermions, such as Quantum Chromodynamics.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation of this paper is to provide a new method that can determine all-order Quantum Electrodynamics (QED) corrections in lattice field theory without the necessity of additional Monte Carlo sampling, thus bypassing the complexity associated with fixed order calculations involving Wick-contraction diagrams.

Method: The authors introduce a framework that employs normalizing-flow transformations on field configurations to calculate all-orders QED corrections. The method is tested in lattice scalar QED across different spacetime dimensions, utilizing both analytical and machine-learned flow methods, and showing reduced variance compared to traditional approaches. Additionally, the study demonstrates the scalability of the trained flows from smaller to larger lattice geometries.

Result: The results show that the proposed method yields estimates with significantly lower variance than standard methods. Moreover, it is shown that the normalizing flows can be effectively trained on small lattices and then successfully applied to much larger lattices, maintaining efficiency.

Conclusion: The conclusion of the paper suggests that the new approach opens up possibilities for determining all-order QED corrections more efficiently. The generalization to theories with fermions, including lattice Quantum Chromodynamics, is anticipated, indicating the broad applicability of the method.

Abstract: This work develops a framework to apply normalizing-flow transformations of field configurations for all-orders Quantum Electrodynamics (QED) corrections in lattice field theory. This opens a new possibility to determine all-order corrections without the need for additional Monte Carlo sampling, generally bypassing the complexity in Wick-contraction diagrams needed at fixed order. The new method is applied to lattice scalar QED in two, three, and four spacetime dimensions, using both analytical and machine-learned flows, with both approaches yielding estimates with significantly reduced variance with respect to standard methods. It is further shown that flows can be trained using small lattice geometries and subsequently evaluated on much larger lattice geometries while maintaining good efficiency. A generalization to theories with fermions is envisaged, suggesting a path to applications in challenging field theories including lattice Quantum Chromodynamics.

### [2] [Complete lattice QCD calculation of $K^{-}\to \ell^{-}\barν_{\ell}\ell^{'+}\ell^{'-}$ form factors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22742)
*R. Di Palma,R. Frezzotti,G. Gagliardi,V. Lubicz,G. Martinelli,C. T. Sachrajda,F. Sanfilippo,S. Simula,N. Tantalo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a complete lattice QCD calculation of the four form factors involved in the rare charged kaon decay, with controlled uncertainties. The results allow for the evaluation of decay rates and differential observables for all four channels, providing first-principles Standard Model predictions.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide the first complete lattice QCD calculation of the form factors that govern the rare charged kaon decays, with fully controlled statistical and systematic uncertainties, which can be used for precise comparison against experimental data.

Method: The authors use gauge ensembles generated by the ETMC with 2+1+1 flavors of Wilson-clover twisted-mass fermions, simulating at physical quark masses and including quark-disconnected contributions. They employ the SFR method to address the analytic continuation problem and investigate finite-volume effects using different spatial extents while taking the continuum limit from three lattice spacings.

Result: The result is a determination of all four form factors across the kinematical region relevant to experiments, enabling the evaluation of decay rates and differential observables for the four decay channels, which provides a benchmark for existing and upcoming measurements.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that this work provides first-principles Standard Model predictions for the rare charged kaon decays, which can be directly compared with experimental data, and a detailed phenomenological analysis is presented in a companion paper.

Abstract: We present the first complete lattice QCD calculation of the four structure-dependent form factors governing the rare charged kaon decay $K^- \to \ell^- \barν_\ell \ell'^+ \ell'^-$, with fully controlled statistical and systematic uncertainties. Our calculation is based on gauge ensembles generated by the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration (ETMC) with $N_f = 2+1+1$ flavors of Wilson-clover twisted-mass fermions. Simulations are performed directly at the physical values of the light and strange quark masses, and include an estimate of the quark-disconnected contributions in which the virtual photon couples to sea quarks. All four form factors are determined across the kinematical region probed by experiments. The Spectral Function Reconstruction (SFR) method of Ref. [1] is employed to overcome the analytic continuation problem for dilepton invariant masses above the two-pion threshold. Finite-volume effects are investigated using ensembles with spatial extents $L\simeq [3.8,7.6]~\mathrm{fm}$, while the continuum limit is obtained from three lattice spacings in the range $a\in[0.057, 0.08]~\mathrm{fm}$. Our results for the form factors enable the evaluation of decay rates and differential observables for all four channels, $K^- \to e^- \barν_e e^+ e^-$, $K^- \to e^- \barν_e μ^+ μ^-$, $K^- \to μ^- \barν_μe^+ e^-$, and $K^- \to μ^- \barν_μμ^+ μ^-$, thereby providing first-principles Standard Model predictions against which existing and upcoming measurements can be directly compared. A detailed phenomenological analysis of the decay rates and associated observables is presented in a companion paper [2].
