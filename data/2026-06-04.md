### [1] [A note on momentum subtraction schemes for quark bilinears and semileptonic operators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04729)
*P. A. Boyle,M. Bruno,M. Gorbahn,S. Jäger,C. Lehner,F. Moretti,J. Parrino*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper explores a family of regularization invariant (RI) symmetric momentum (SMOM) schemes for semi-leptonic operators in massless QCD, extending the original RI/SMOM scheme to include projectors that can be applied to semi-leptonic cases and demonstrating their equivalence with another reference, which is significant for perturbative calculations.

Motivation: The motivation is to extend the applicability of the RI/SMOM scheme to semi-leptonic operators by creating a set of projectors that can handle these cases, while also ensuring consistency with existing work on flavor-changing vector currents renormalization protected by the Ward identity.

Method: The method involves working within the framework of chirally symmetric and massless QCD, relating semi-leptonic operators to flavor-changing vector currents, and then extending the RI/SMOM scheme to a new set of projectors suitable for semi-leptonic scenarios. The authors demonstrate the equivalence of their extended scheme with another recent study.

Result: The result is the successful extension of the RI/SMOM scheme to a broader class of projectors that are applicable to semi-leptonic cases, and it is shown that this extended scheme is equivalent to the one described in Gorbahn et al. 2023, which is important for the calculation of Wilson coefficients in perturbation theory.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the newly developed family of RI/SMOM projectors for semi-leptonic operators is a valid and useful tool for perturbative calculations, as it maintains the desirable properties of the original scheme and is consistent with other established methods.

Abstract: In this work we examine a family of regularization invariant (RI) symmetric momentum (SMOM) schemes for semi-leptonic operators. By working with chirally symmetric and massless QCD, we relate the semi-leptonic operators with their corresponding flavor-changing vector currents, whose renormalization in pure QCD is protected by the Ward identity. For the latter, we extend the original RI/SMOM scheme [Sturm et al. 2009] to a family projectors suitable to be promoted to the semi-leptonic case and demonstrate their equivalence to Ref. [Gorbahn et al. 2023], relevant in particular for the perturbative calculation of the corresponding Wilson coefficients.
