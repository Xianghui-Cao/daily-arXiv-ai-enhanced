### [1] [Observation of glueball excitations and string breaking in a $2+1$D $\mathbb{Z}_2$ lattice gauge theory on a trapped-ion quantum computer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07435)
*Kaidi Xu,Umberto Borla,Kevin Hemery,Rohan Joshi,Henrik Dreyer,Enrico Rinaldi,Jad C. Halimeh*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: Researchers have used a trapped-ion quantum computer to simulate a 2+1 dimensional Z2 lattice gauge theory, demonstrating the dynamics of confining strings and glueballs, and showing genuine 2+1D nonequilibrium dynamics that cannot be reduced to 1+1D physics. The experiment involved over 1000 entangling gates on a 6x5 lattice with 56 qubits, and observed phenomena such as string breaking and matter creation.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to use quantum simulation to study real-time, nonperturbative, far-from-equilibrium quantum processes in high-energy physics (HEP), specifically focusing on quark confinement and related phenomena like hadronization in QCD. The goal is to understand the dynamics of confining strings and glueballs, which are key aspects of quark confinement, through a controllable first-principles approach.

Method: The method involves implementing a depth-6 Trotter circuit on a 6x5 matter-site square lattice using all 56 qubits available on a Quantinuum System Model H2 trapped-ion quantum computer. Researchers prepared initial string configurations far from equilibrium and then quenched them across a range of parameters to observe the formation of closed-loop excitations and multi-order string breaking. The system's 2+1D dynamics were confirmed by observing string snapshots over time that could not be mapped to 1+1D physics.

Result: The results include the observation of rich dynamical phenomena, such as the formation of gauge-invariant closed-loop excitations, which are similar to glueballs in QCD, and multi-order string breaking accompanied by spontaneous matter creation. The researchers also demonstrated that the system exhibits genuine 2+1D dynamics, which was evidenced by the string snapshots over time that could not be trivially mapped to 1+1D physics.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the digital quantum simulations of nonequilibrium dynamics in a higher-dimensional lattice gauge theory have been successfully achieved, providing an experimental setting for studying phenomena related to confinement physics. This work represents a significant step towards probing nonperturbative, far-from-equilibrium quantum processes in HEP, such as hadronization in QCD.

Abstract: A major goal of the quantum simulation of high-energy physics (HEP) is to probe real-time nonperturbative far-from-equilibrium quantum processes underlying phenomena such as hadronization in quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The quantum simulation of the dynamics of confining strings and glueballs, both essential aspects of quark confinement, in a controllable first-principles way is an important step towards this goal. Here, we realize a $\mathbb{Z}_2$ lattice gauge theory in $2+1$D with a tunable plaquette term on a \texttt{Quantinuum System Model H2} trapped-ion quantum computer. We implement a shallow depth-6 Trotter circuit on a $6 \times 5$ matter-site square lattice utilizing all $56$ available qubits to execute over $1000$ entangling gates. We prepare far-from-equilibrium initial string configurations that we quench across a range of parameters to observe rich dynamical phenomena, such as the formation of gauge-invariant closed-loop excitations reminiscent of glueballs in QCD and multi-order string breaking accompanied by spontaneous matter creation. We further demonstrate experimentally that the system displays genuine $2+1$D dynamics, as evidenced by string snapshots over time that cannot be trivially mapped to $1+1$D physics. Our results demonstrate digital quantum simulations of nonequilibrium dynamics in a higher-dimensional lattice gauge theory and provide an experimentally accessible setting for phenomena related to confinement physics.

### [2] [Lattice determination of the higher-order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon $g-2$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08351)
*Arnau Beltran,Alessandro Conigli,Simon Kuberski,Harvey B. Meyer,Konstantin Ottnad,Hartmut Wittig*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the NLO HVP contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment with a precision below 1%. The result is more precise than previous estimates and shows a significant tension with data-driven evaluations based on hadronic cross section measurements.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To provide a highly precise calculation of the next-to-leading order (NLO) hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, which is critical for understanding the discrepancy between the Standard Model prediction and experimental measurement of this quantity.

Method: The authors used the time-momentum representation combined with the spatially summed vector correlator computed on CLS ensembles with $N_{\mathrm{f}}=2+1$ flavors of $\mathrm{O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions. They spanned six lattice spacings and a range of pion masses, including the physical value, and accounted for finite-size corrections and isospin-breaking effects.

Result: The calculation resulted in $a_μ^{\mathrm{hvp,\,nlo}} = (-101.57 \pm 0.26_{\rm stat} \pm 0.54_{\rm syst}) \times 10^{-11}$, corresponding to a total relative error of 0.6%. This result is 1.4$σ$ below the estimate of the 2025 White Paper update and shows a 4.6$σ$ tension with data-driven evaluations based on hadronic cross-section measurements prior to the CMD-3 result.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn from the research is that the new lattice QCD calculation provides a sub-percent precision for the NLO HVP contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, which is twice as precise as the 2025 White Paper update. It also highlights a significant discrepancy with earlier data-driven approaches, indicating the need for further investigation into the source of this tension.

Abstract: We present the first lattice QCD calculation of the next-to-leading order (NLO) hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment with sub-percent precision. We employ the time-momentum representation combined with the spatially summed vector correlator computed on CLS ensembles with $N_{\mathrm{f}}=2+1$ flavors of $\mathrm{O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions, spanning six lattice spacings ($0.039$-$0.097\,$fm) and a range of pion masses including the physical value. After accounting for finite-size corrections and isospin-breaking effects, we obtain in the continuum limit $a_μ^{\mathrm{hvp,\,nlo}} = (-101.57 \pm 0.26_{\rm stat} \pm 0.54_{\rm syst}) \times 10^{-11}$, corresponding to a total relative error of 0.6$\%$. Our result lies 1.4$σ$ below the estimate of the 2025 White Paper update and is two times more precise. It also shows a tension of $4.6σ$ with data-driven evaluations based on hadronic cross section measurements prior to the CMD-3 result.
