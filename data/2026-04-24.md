### [1] [Reconstructing the full kinematic dependence of GPDs from pseudo-distributions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21476)
*Hervé Dutrieux,Robert G. Edwards,Joe Karpie,Cédric Mezrag,Christopher Monahan,Kostas Orginos,Anatoly Radyushkin,David Richards,Eloy Romero,Savvas Zafeiropoulos*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents a novel method for reconstructing the full dependence of unpolarized isovector proton GPDs using lattice QCD data, directly extracting double distributions and assessing systematic uncertainties with multidimensional Gaussian process regression.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to enforce an important property of GPDs related to Lorentz symmetry by extracting double distributions directly from lattice data, which has not been done before. The work also aims to improve upon previous computations by using larger hadron momenta and extended kinematic coverage, as well as to evaluate model dependence on the systematic uncertainty.

Method: The authors use the pseudo-distribution formalism and multidimensional Gaussian process regression to regularize the inverse problem and extract double distributions (DDs) from lattice QCD data. They analyze an ensemble corresponding to a pion mass of 358 MeV and a lattice spacing of 0.094 fm, with hadron momenta up to 2.7 GeV.

Result: The result is a reconstruction of the full $(x, ξ, t)$ dependence of $H^{u-d}$ and $E^{u-d}$ GPDs, including additional skewness-dependent moments, with an assessment of the impact of model dependence on the systematic uncertainty.

Conclusion: The conclusion would be that the new approach allows for a more accurate and reliable extraction of GPDs from lattice QCD data, with a better understanding of the associated systematic uncertainties, and represents a significant step forward in the field of hadronic physics.

Abstract: We propose a reconstruction of the full $(x, ξ, t)$ dependence of unpolarized isovector proton generalized parton distributions (GPDs) $H^{u-d}$ and $E^{u-d}$ from lattice QCD data in the pseudo-distribution formalism. For the first time, we extract double distributions (DDs) directly from lattice data, enforcing therefore an important property of GPDs linked to Lorentz symmetry. We use the flexible framework of multidimensional Gaussian process regression to regularize the inverse problem and present an assessment of the impact of model dependence on the systematic uncertainty. Our lattice ensemble corresponds to a pion mass $m_π= 358$~MeV and a lattice spacing $a = 0.094$~fm. We use larger hadron momenta, up to 2.7~GeV, and kinematic coverage compared to our previous computations and extract additional skewness-dependent moments of the GPD.
