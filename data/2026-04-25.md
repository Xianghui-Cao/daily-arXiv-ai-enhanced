### [1] [Reconstructing the full kinematic dependence of GPDs from pseudo-distributions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21476)
*Hervé Dutrieux,Robert G. Edwards,Joe Karpie,Cédric Mezrag,Christopher Monahan,Kostas Orginos,Anatoly Radyushkin,David Richards,Eloy Romero,Savvas Zafeiropoulos*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a method for reconstructing the full dependence of unpolarized isovector proton GPDs from lattice QCD data using multidimensional Gaussian process regression, with an assessment of systematic uncertainty and improved hadron momenta and kinematic coverage.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a more accurate and comprehensive reconstruction of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) by directly extracting double distributions from lattice QCD data, which enforces Lorentz symmetry, and to evaluate the model dependence on systematic uncertainty.

Method: The authors use multidimensional Gaussian process regression to solve the inverse problem and regularize the extraction of double distributions (DDs) from lattice QCD data. They work with a specific lattice ensemble characterized by a pion mass and lattice spacing, and they improve upon their previous work by using larger hadron momenta and obtaining additional skewness-dependent moments of the GPDs.

Result: The result is a reconstruction of the full $(x, ξ, t)$ dependence of the GPDs $H^{u-d}$ and $E^{u-d}$. The authors also assess the impact of model dependence on the systematic uncertainty of the extracted GPDs, providing a more robust analysis.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that this novel approach of using multidimensional Gaussian process regression for the extraction of GPDs from lattice QCD data, while accounting for Lorentz symmetry, yields a reliable reconstruction of GPDs and provides a better understanding of the associated systematic uncertainties.

Abstract: We propose a reconstruction of the full $(x, ξ, t)$ dependence of unpolarized isovector proton generalized parton distributions (GPDs) $H^{u-d}$ and $E^{u-d}$ from lattice QCD data in the pseudo-distribution formalism. For the first time, we extract double distributions (DDs) directly from lattice data, enforcing therefore an important property of GPDs linked to Lorentz symmetry. We use the flexible framework of multidimensional Gaussian process regression to regularize the inverse problem and present an assessment of the impact of model dependence on the systematic uncertainty. Our lattice ensemble corresponds to a pion mass $m_π= 358$~MeV and a lattice spacing $a = 0.094$~fm. We use larger hadron momenta, up to 2.7~GeV, and kinematic coverage compared to our previous computations and extract additional skewness-dependent moments of the GPD.
