### [1] [$\bar{D}$-meson Nucleon Scattering from Lattice QCD at the Physical Point](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12251)
*Wren Yamada,Yan Lyu,Kotaro Murakami,Takumi Doi*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The first lattice QCD study of s-wave scattering of the D-meson and the nucleon is conducted at physical point, revealing a repulsive core and an attractive pocket in both I=0 and I=1 channels. The I=0 channel is more attractive than the I=1, and the DN potential shows more attraction compared to the KN potential. No pentaquark state is found in the s-wave DN system.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To explore the interaction between the D-meson and the nucleon through s-wave scattering using lattice QCD, which could provide insights into the nature of the interaction and the possible existence of pentaquark states.

Method: The researchers used (2+1)-flavor configurations with a pion mass of 137 MeV and a lattice spacing of 0.084 fm. They applied the HAL QCD method to the four-point correlation function of the DN system to obtain the leading-order potential and extract the s-wave phase shifts for low-energy DN scattering.

Result: The s-wave phase shifts indicate that the I=0 channel has a weakly attractive behavior with a positive scattering length, while the I=1 channel is repulsive with a negative scattering length. Both channels feature a short-range repulsive core and a shallow attractive long-range potential, with the I=0 being more attractive. The DN potential also exhibits more attraction than the KN potential. No bound states are observed, suggesting no pentaquark state in the s-wave DN system.

Conclusion: The study provides evidence for the structure of the DN potential and the absence of a pentaquark state in the s-wave DN system, contributing to the understanding of the strong interaction dynamics between mesons and baryons.

Abstract: We report the first lattice QCD study of the $s$-wave scattering of the $\bar{D}$-meson and the nucleon at the physical point, utilizing (2+1)-flavor configurations generated by the HAL QCD collaboration with a pion mass of $m_π\simeq 137$ MeV and a lattice spacing of $a\simeq0.084$ fm. By applying the HAL QCD method to the four-point correlation function of the $\bar{D}N$ system, we obtain a leading-order potential of the derivative expansion of the interaction kernel, which is then used to extract the $s$-wave phase shifts of low-energy $\bar{D}N$ scattering. Both the isospin $I=0$ and $I=1$ channels have a short-range repulsive core and a shallow attractive pocket in the intermediate to long-range region, though the $I=0$ channel is more attractive than the $I=1$ channel. We also observe that the $\bar{D}N$ potential exhibits more attraction than the $KN$ potential, which is its analog in the strange sector. In terms of the $s$-wave phase shifts, the $I=0$ channel shows a weak attractive behavior in the low-energy region with a positive scattering length of $0.246 \pm 0.105 (_{-0.051}^{+0.084})$ fm, whereas the $I=1$ channel shows repulsion with a negative scattering length of $-0.086 \pm 0.050 (_{-0.001}^{+0.037})$ fm. No bound states are found in both isospin channels, indicating the absence of a pentaquark state in the $s$-wave $\bar{D}N$ system.
