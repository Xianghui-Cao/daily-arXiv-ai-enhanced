### [1] [Scalar glueball-$s\bar{s}$ mixing in one flavor lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.17294)
*Long-Cheng Gui,Wei Sun,Ying Chen,Ming Gong,Geng Li,Zhaofeng Liu*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study explores the mixing between the lowest-lying scalar glueball and the $s\overline{s}$ meson using lattice QCD, finding a significant mixing angle and energy, which provides insights into the nature of isoscalar scalar mesons. Further investigation on the continuum limit and quark mass dependence is suggested.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To understand the mixing between the lowest-lying scalar glueball and the $s\overline{s}$ meson in the context of lattice QCD, which can provide non-perturbative inputs for the nature of experimental isoscalar scalar mesons.

Method: Utilized an anisotropic $16^3 \times 128$ lattice ensemble with a specific lattice spacing to solve a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) for optimized glueball and $s\overline{s}$ scalar operators in the $J^\text{PC} = 0^{++}$ channel, determining the masses of the two lowest-lying eigenstates and their couplings to the operators.

Result: Determined the masses of the two lowest-lying eigenstates as $m_1 = 1.290(20)\,\mathrm{GeV}$ and $m_2 = 1.777(49)\,\mathrm{GeV}$, a substantial mixing angle $|\theta| \approx 40.7(2.7)^\circ$, and a large mixing energy $x_s = 239(24)$ MeV, indicating strong glueball-$s\bar{s}$ mixing in the scalar sector.

Conclusion: The results show a strong mixing between the scalar glueball and the $s\bar{s}$ meson, providing important non-perturbative data for the understanding of isoscalar scalar mesons. The authors suggest further studies on the continuum limit and the quark mass dependence of the mixing energy.

Abstract: We investigate the mixing between the lowest-lying scalar glueball and the $s\overline{s}$ meson in $N_f=1$ lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) utilizing an anisotropic $16^3 \times 128$ lattice ensemble at a lattice spacing $a_s\approx 0.148\,\rm{fm}$. By solving a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) for the optimized glueball and $s\overline{s}$ scalar operators in the $J^\text{PC} = 0^{++}$ channel, the masses of the two lowest-lying eigenstates are determined to be $m_1 = 1.290(20)\,\mathrm{GeV}$ and $m_2 = 1.777(49)\,\mathrm{GeV}$. By extracting the couplings of these mass eigenstates to the glueball and $s\bar{s}$ operators, we determine a substantial mixing angle $|θ| \approx 40.7(2.7)^\circ$ and a large mixing energy $x_s = 239(24)$ MeV. These results indicate a strong glueball-$s\bar{s}$ mixing in the scalar sector, providing important non-perturbative inputs for understanding the nature of the experimental isoscalar scalar mesons. The continuum limit of the mixing energy and its quark mass dependence need to be investigated in the future.

### [2] [Renormalization of meson susceptibilities and RG-invariant symmetry ratios in QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.17816)
*Ting-Wai Chiu*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper analyzes the ultraviolet divergence structure of meson susceptibilities in finite-temperature QCD with exact chiral symmetry. It identifies additive divergences and a multiplicative renormalization factor, and shows that a symmetry ratio built from temperature-subtracted susceptibilities is renormalization-group invariant and scheme-independent. The study also contrasts this divergence structure with that of Wilson fermions, where chiral symmetry breaking affects the equality of Z-factors.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation for this paper is to understand and analyze the ultraviolet divergence structure of meson susceptibilities within the context of finite-temperature Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) when lattice formulations preserve exact chiral symmetry. The authors aim to provide insights into the renormalization process and the implications of chiral symmetry on the divergence structure.

Method: The method involves theoretical analysis of the bare susceptibility, which is separated into additive divergences and a multiplicative renormalization factor. The authors examine the temperature independence of the additive divergences and their removal through temperature subtraction. They also explore the multiplicative renormalization, which depends logarithmically on the lattice spacing. Additionally, they construct a symmetry ratio from temperature-subtracted susceptibilities and prove its invariance under the renormalization group.

Result: The results show that the additive divergences are temperature independent and can be removed by temperature subtraction, while the multiplicative renormalization factor has a logarithmic dependence on the lattice spacing. A key finding is that the symmetry ratio constructed from temperature-subtracted susceptibilities is exactly renormalization-group invariant and scheme-independent. Furthermore, it is shown that the equality of Z-factors holds for any number of flavors and quark masses, unaffected by spontaneous symmetry breaking or the U(1)_A anomaly, contrasting with the case of Wilson fermions where chiral symmetry breaking spoils this equality.

Conclusion: In conclusion, the paper provides a detailed analysis of the divergence structure of meson susceptibilities in the context of finite-temperature QCD with exact chiral symmetry. It establishes the robustness of the symmetry ratio as a renormalization-group invariant quantity and highlights the importance of the equality of Z-factors for the construction of such a ratio, which is not maintained in theories with explicit chiral symmetry breaking, like those involving Wilson fermions.

Abstract: We analyze the ultraviolet divergence structure of meson susceptibilities in finite-temperature QCD, for lattice formulations with exact chiral symmetry. The bare susceptibility separates into additive divergences and a multiplicative renormalization $Z_Γ^{2}$. The additive divergences are temperature-independent, and are removed by the temperature subtraction. They consist of the leading power divergence $α_Γ/(2a^2)$ from the identity operator, together with a mass-dependent logarithmic term $\propto m^2\ln(1/(am))$. The latter is present already for chirally symmetric fermions. Exact chiral symmetry forbids all mass-dependent \emph{power} divergences of the susceptibility. The multiplicative factor $Z_Γ^{2}$ has a logarithmic dependence on the lattice spacing, controlled by the operator anomalous dimension. We show that the symmetry ratio $κ_{AB} = (χ_A^{\rm reg} - χ_B^{\rm reg})/ (χ_A^{\rm reg} + χ_B^{\rm reg})$, built from temperature-subtracted susceptibilities of symmetry partners, is exactly renormalization-group invariant and scheme-independent. The additive divergence is removed by the subtraction, and the multiplicative factor cancels through the equality $Z_A = Z_B$. This equality holds for any number of flavors and any quark masses in a mass-independent scheme, unaffected by spontaneous symmetry breaking or the $U(1)_A$ anomaly. We derive the complete $Z$-factor chains for all meson channels and contrast the divergence structure with that of Wilson fermions, for which the explicit chiral-symmetry breaking induces a chiral-odd power-divergent mixing and spoils the equality $Z_A = Z_B$ on which the construction relies.
