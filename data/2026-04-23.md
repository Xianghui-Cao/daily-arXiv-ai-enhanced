### [1] [Heavy quark thermodynamics with anisotropic lattices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20449)
*Jon-Ivar Skullerud,Rachel Horohan D'Arcy,Gert Aarts,Chris Allton,M. Naeem Anwar,Timothy J. Burns,Ben Page,Ryan Bignell,Sinéad M. Ryan,Benjamin Jäger,Seyong Kim,Maria Paola Lombardo,Alexander Rothkopf,Antonio Smecca*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The FASTSUM collaboration used anisotropic lattice QCD to investigate the spectral properties of heavy quarkonia and open heavy flavour systems at high temperatures, finding a small but significant negative mass shift and an increasing thermal width for heavy quarkonium, as well as presenting novel results on B mesons and preliminary findings on the static quark potential.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the behavior of heavy quarkonia and open heavy flavor systems in a high-temperature environment, which is relevant for understanding the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) and the conditions present in the early universe or in high-energy collisions.

Method: Anisotropic lattice QCD simulations are employed to study the spectral properties of these systems. The researchers use different methods to calculate the mass shifts, thermal widths, and spectral functions, and they also carry out a high-precision calculation of the static quark potential.

Result: Results indicate a small but significant negative mass shift and an increasing thermal width for heavy quarkonia at high temperature. Additionally, the study provides the first lattice QCD results for the masses and spectral functions of B mesons at high temperature, along with initial results from a high-precision static quark potential calculation.

Conclusion: The findings contribute to the theoretical framework of QCD under extreme conditions and provide insights into the behavior of heavy quark states within the QGP, offering a basis for comparison with experimental data and further theoretical work.

Abstract: We present recent results from the FASTSUM collaboration, using anisotropic lattice QCD to study spectral properties of heavy quarkonia and open heavy flavour systems at high temperature. For heavy quarkonium, our results using a number of different methods suggest a small but significant and robust negative mass shift as well as an increasing thermal width. We present the first lattice results for masses and spectral functions of B mesons at high temperature, and preliminary results for a high-precision calculation of the static quark potential.
