### [1] [Normalizing flows for all-orders QED corrections in lattice field theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22444)
*Nils Hermansson-Truedsson,Gurtej Kanwar*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A new framework using normalizing-flow transformations for field configurations in lattice QED allows all-order corrections without additional Monte Carlo sampling, reducing the complexity of Wick-contraction diagrams and variance in estimates. The method, tested in 2, 3, and 4 dimensions with both analytical and machine-learned flows, shows potential for application in theories with fermions, such as lattice Quantum Chromodynamics.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop a more efficient method for determining all-order QED corrections in lattice field theory, which traditionally requires complex Wick-contraction diagrams and additional Monte Carlo sampling, by applying normalizing-flow transformations that simplify the process and reduce the variance in the estimation.

Method: The method involves developing and applying a framework that uses normalizing-flow transformations on field configurations to calculate all-order QED corrections. This was tested in lattice scalar QED across 2, 3, and 4 spacetime dimensions, using both analytical and machine-learned flow methods, and it also demonstrated the transferability of trained flows from small to larger lattice geometries.

Result: The results show that the use of normalizing-flow transformations can provide estimates with significantly reduced variance compared to standard methods, and that the trained flows remain efficient even when applied to much larger lattice geometries than those used during training.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the new method offers a promising approach for calculating all-order QED corrections with improved efficiency and reduced complexity, and it has the potential to be generalized to other field theories, including those with fermions, such as lattice Quantum Chromodynamics.

Abstract: This work develops a framework to apply normalizing-flow transformations of field configurations for all-orders Quantum Electrodynamics (QED) corrections in lattice field theory. This opens a new possibility to determine all-order corrections without the need for additional Monte Carlo sampling, generally bypassing the complexity in Wick-contraction diagrams needed at fixed order. The new method is applied to lattice scalar QED in two, three, and four spacetime dimensions, using both analytical and machine-learned flows, with both approaches yielding estimates with significantly reduced variance with respect to standard methods. It is further shown that flows can be trained using small lattice geometries and subsequently evaluated on much larger lattice geometries while maintaining good efficiency. A generalization to theories with fermions is envisaged, suggesting a path to applications in challenging field theories including lattice Quantum Chromodynamics.

### [2] [Complete lattice QCD calculation of $K^{-}\to \ell^{-}\barν_{\ell}\ell^{'+}\ell^{'-}$ form factors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22742)
*R. Di Palma,R. Frezzotti,G. Gagliardi,V. Lubicz,G. Martinelli,C. T. Sachrajda,F. Sanfilippo,S. Simula,N. Tantalo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents the first complete lattice QCD calculation of the four structure-dependent form factors for the rare charged kaon decay, with controlled uncertainties. The results allow for the evaluation of decay rates and differential observables for all four channels, providing a basis for comparison with experimental data.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide the first complete lattice QCD calculations of the four structure-dependent form factors for the rare charged kaon decay, which allows for the direct comparison between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements, thereby testing the Standard Model.

Method: The method involves using gauge ensembles from the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration (ETMC) with 2+1+1 flavors of Wilson-clover twisted-mass fermions, simulations at physical quark masses, including quark-disconnected contributions, and employing the Spectral Function Reconstruction (SFR) method. The study also investigates finite-volume effects and takes the continuum limit from three different lattice spacings.

Result: The result is the determination of all four form factors across the kinematical region relevant to experiments, enabling the computation of decay rates and differential observables for the four charged kaon decay channels, which can be directly compared with existing and upcoming experimental data.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the presented form factor calculations provide first-principles Standard Model predictions that can be used to evaluate the decay rates and differential observables for the rare charged kaon decays, offering a benchmark for current and future experimental analyses.

Abstract: We present the first complete lattice QCD calculation of the four structure-dependent form factors governing the rare charged kaon decay $K^- \to \ell^- \barν_\ell \ell'^+ \ell'^-$, with fully controlled statistical and systematic uncertainties. Our calculation is based on gauge ensembles generated by the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration (ETMC) with $N_f = 2+1+1$ flavors of Wilson-clover twisted-mass fermions. Simulations are performed directly at the physical values of the light and strange quark masses, and include an estimate of the quark-disconnected contributions in which the virtual photon couples to sea quarks. All four form factors are determined across the kinematical region probed by experiments. The Spectral Function Reconstruction (SFR) method of Ref. [1] is employed to overcome the analytic continuation problem for dilepton invariant masses above the two-pion threshold. Finite-volume effects are investigated using ensembles with spatial extents $L\simeq [3.8,7.6]~\mathrm{fm}$, while the continuum limit is obtained from three lattice spacings in the range $a\in[0.057, 0.08]~\mathrm{fm}$. Our results for the form factors enable the evaluation of decay rates and differential observables for all four channels, $K^- \to e^- \barν_e e^+ e^-$, $K^- \to e^- \barν_e μ^+ μ^-$, $K^- \to μ^- \barν_μe^+ e^-$, and $K^- \to μ^- \barν_μμ^+ μ^-$, thereby providing first-principles Standard Model predictions against which existing and upcoming measurements can be directly compared. A detailed phenomenological analysis of the decay rates and associated observables is presented in a companion paper [2].
