### [1] [Monte Carlo reconstruction of symmetry-twisted partition function ratios: the critical 3D Ising](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00106)
*José Matos*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A Monte Carlo method is presented for estimating partition function ratios in lattice theories, focusing on the 3D Ising model. This method enables a direct calculation of the thermodynamic Casimir difference without requiring lattice derivatives or bulk subtractions, and it provides insights into CFT compactification data.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop a more direct approach for calculating the thermodynamic Casimir difference and to gain selective numerical access to conformal field theory (CFT) compactification data, such as the effective thermal screening scale and the energy gap in the  ℤ2-odd sector, which can be used to probe tensions in periodic sector observables.

Method: The authors introduce a Monte Carlo strategy that extends the configuration space to include an interpolating family between distinct global sectors, using flat histogram methods to estimate the free energy difference. The method is applied specifically to the three-dimensional Ising model at criticality, comparing the untwisted periodic and ℤ2-twisted antiperiodic sectors on a specific geometry.

Result: The result is a precise estimation of the symmetry-twisted thermodynamic Casimir difference, Δℤ2=0.327(2), obtained through large-volume and aspect ratio extrapolations. Additionally, the method yields estimates for the effective thermal screening scale and the ℤ2-odd sector energy gap on T^2.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the new Monte Carlo method effectively calculates the thermodynamic Casimir difference directly and provides a way to obtain CFT compactification data, thus offering an independent check on tensions observed in periodic sector observables.

Abstract: We introduce a Monte Carlo strategy for directly estimating partition function ratios between distinct global sectors of a lattice theory. It enlarges the configuration space to sample an interpolating family whose endpoints are the desired sectors, and uses flat histogram methods to reconstruct the corresponding free energy difference. Although the construction is more general, we focus here on the three-dimensional Ising model on the slab $\mathbb{R}^{2}\times S^{1}_{L_{z}}$ at the bulk critical point, comparing the untwisted periodic sector with the $\mathbb{Z}_{2}$-twisted antiperiodic sector. A large-volume and aspect ratio extrapolation gives the symmetry-twisted thermodynamic Casimir difference $Δ_{\mathbb{Z}_{2}}=0.327(2)$ directly, without lattice derivatives or bulk subtractions. This provides an independent twisted sector probe of tensions observed in periodic sector thermodynamic Casimir observables. More generally, the method gives direct but selective numerical access to CFT compactification data, including estimates of the effective thermal screening scale and the $\mathbb{Z}_{2}$-odd sector energy gap on $T^{2}$.

### [2] [Gradient Flow Renormalization Schemes for Composite Fermion Operators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00493)
*Matthew Black,Anna Hasenfratz,Oliver Witzel*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper introduces gradient flow (GF) normalization prescriptions for fermionic composite operators, using the partially conserved axial charge or the conserved vector current. The A and V schemes are defined through flowed two-point correlation functions, which can be matched to the MS-bar scheme at short flow times. The method is implemented with ratios of two-point functions, allowing for nonperturbative determinations of renormalization factors, anomalous dimensions, and evolution factors. Demonstrated on RBC-UKQCD domain-wall fermion ensembles, it includes a GF determination of Z_V/Z_A and the strange quark mass.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop a nonperturbative method for normalizing fermionic composite operators in lattice QCD, which avoids the backward-flow construction required by local ringed-scheme definitions and provides a way to connect lattice results to perturbative calculations reliably.

Method: The method involves defining A and V schemes for fermionic composite operators based on gradient flow and using the partially conserved axial charge or conserved vector current. The normalization is achieved through standard flowed two-point correlation functions. The method also allows for the matching to the MS-bar scheme using known SFTX coefficients at short flow times. It is applied to RBC-UKQCD domain-wall fermion ensembles to determine the ratio of matching factors Z_V/Z_A and the renormalized strange quark mass.

Result: The result is a new nonperturbative method for determining renormalization factors, anomalous dimensions, and evolution factors for fermionic composite operators that can be matched to the MS-bar scheme. The method was successfully applied to obtain the ratio of matching factors Z_V/Z_A and a new determination of the renormalized strange quark mass using RBC-UKQCD domain-wall fermion ensembles.

Conclusion: The paper concludes that the proposed gradient flow normalization prescriptions for fermionic composite operators provide a practical and reliable way to perform nonperturbative renormalization in lattice QCD, facilitating the connection between lattice results and perturbative calculations.

Abstract: We introduce gradient flow (GF) normalization prescriptions for fermionic composite operators in which the flowed fermion wavefunction renormalization factor is fixed nonperturbatively using either the partially conserved axial charge or the conserved vector current. The resulting $A$ and $V$ schemes are defined through standard flowed two-point correlation functions and therefore avoid the backward-flow construction required by local ringed-scheme definitions. In the short-flow-time limit, the $A$ and $V$ schemes can be matched to $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ using known ringed-scheme short-flow-time expansion (SFTX) coefficients. We show how these schemes can be implemented through ratios of two-point correlation functions, leading to simple nonperturbative determinations of renormalization factors, anomalous dimensions, and evolution factors which connect lattice-accessible flow times to shorter flow times where perturbative matching is reliable. We illustrate the method with RBC-UKQCD domain-wall fermion ensembles, including a GF determination of the ratio of matching factors $Z_V/Z_A$, and a new GF determination of the renormalized strange quark mass.

### [3] [Isospin-breaking effects in inclusive hadronic $τ$ data for the muon $(g-2)$ from first principles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00564)
*Mattia Bruno,Taku Izubuchi,Christoph Lehner,Aaron S. Meyer,Julian Parrino,Xin-Yu Tuo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a strategy for calculating isospin-breaking effects in hadronic τ decays using Lattice QCD+QED, aiming to improve the precision of (g-2)μ determination. It categorizes radiative corrections, provides analytic expressions, and discusses renormalization and challenges with non-factorizable contributions.

Motivation: The motivation is to enhance the precision of the Hadronic-Vacuum-Polarization contribution to (g-2)μ by accurately accounting for isospin-breaking effects in hadronic τ decays, which are necessary for high-precision determinations from experimental τ data.

Method: The method involves a fully inclusive setup using first-principles Lattice QCD+QED simulations. Radiative corrections are separated into three infrared-safe classes, each studied individually. Analytic expressions are provided for initial-state effects, and a strategy is proposed for final-state corrections in Euclidean space. Non-factorizable contributions and their challenges during analytic continuation are also examined, alongside a prescription for the renormalization of terms at first order in isospin-breaking parameters.

Result: The results include a comprehensive strategy for the calculation of isospin-breaking effects, providing insights into the treatment of radiative corrections, and addressing the issues related to non-factorizable contributions. Abstract: The knowledge of isospin-breaking effects in hadronic $τ$ decays is required for a high-precision determination of the Hadronic-Vacuum-Polarization contribution to $(g-2)_μ$ from experimental $τ$ data. In this work we present a strategy for their calculation in a fully inclusive setup from first-principles Lattice QCD+QED simulations. We separate radiative corrections in three infrared safe classes, which we study individually. We provide analytic expressions for their effects in the initial state and propose a strategy for final-state corrections directly in Euclidean space. We also examine the non-factorizable contributions and highlight the challenges associated with their analytic continuation from Euclidean to Minkowski space. By studying short-distance corrections in the context of momentum schemes, we provide a prescription for the renormalization of the individual terms at first order in the ispospin-breaking parameters.
