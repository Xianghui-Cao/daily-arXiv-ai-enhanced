### [1] [Topological Susceptibility and QCD at Finite Theta Angle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28035)
*Claudio Bonanno,Claudio Bonati,Massimo D'Elia*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This chapter offers a comprehensive introduction to the theoretical aspects of topology and θ-dependence in QCD, their relevance in the Standard Model and beyond, and reviews both analytic predictions from various approaches and recent numerical results from lattice QCD simulations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a pedagogical overview of the topological and θ-dependent features in QCD, which are crucial for understanding phenomena within the Standard Model, such as η' physics and the neutron electric dipole moment, as well as addressing the strong CP problem with potential solutions like the axion.

Method: The authors use a combination of chiral effective theories, large-N arguments, semiclassical methods to derive analytic predictions, and they also review recent numerical results obtained through Monte Carlo simulations of lattice-discretized QCD.

Result: The chapter presents a synthesis of theoretical frameworks and their respective validity regimes for θ-dependence in QCD, alongside a discussion of the latest numerical findings from lattice QCD that explore the topological structure of the theory.

Conclusion: The work concludes with an up-to-date summary of the understanding of topological effects and θ-dependence in QCD, highlighting the interplay between different theoretical approaches and the importance of lattice QCD in providing non-perturbative insights into these phenomena.

Abstract: In this chapter we provide a pedagogical introduction to the main theoretical aspects related to topology and $θ$-dependence in Quantum Chromo-Dynamics (QCD), and to their phenomenological relevance in the Standard Model ($η^\prime$ physics, neutron electric dipole moment) and beyond (strong CP problem and the axion solution). We then provide an overview of the main analytic predictions for $θ$-dependence obtained using several different approaches (chiral effective theories, large-$N$ arguments, semiclassical methods) and their regimes of validity, as well as a selection of the most recent numerical results about QCD topology obtained via Monte Carlo simulations of the lattice-discretized theory.
