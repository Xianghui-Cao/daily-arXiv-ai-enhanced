*Ivan Horváth*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper introduces a regularized formula for glue scalar density using Dirac spectral density and explores its implications on the relation between glue and quark scalar densities, IR phase, symmetry breaking, and UV-IR relations in QCD.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a new perspective on the understanding of the glue scalar density (gluon condensate) through a novel formula, which can shed light on its connection with quark scalar densities, infrared (IR) phase, and the mechanisms of symmetry breaking in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD).

Method: The method involves deriving a regularized formula for the glue scalar density based on the Dirac spectral density, as presented in [arXiv:2509.03509].

Result: The result is a clearer understanding of the interplay between glue and quark scalar densities, insights into the IR phase, clarification on the distinction between anomalous and spontaneous symmetry breaking, and an improved comprehension of the relationship between ultraviolet (UV) and IR scales in QCD.

Conclusion: The conclusion highlights the significance of the new formula in advancing the theoretical framework of QCD, particularly in elucidating the complex dynamics of scalar densities, phases, and symmetry breaking mechanisms.

Abstract: I derive the regularized formula for glue scalar density (gluon condensate) in terms of Dirac spectral density [arXiv:2509.03509], and elaborate on its uses and meaning. Particular attention is given to understanding of what this new formula reveals about the relation between glue and quark scalar densities, how it relates to IR phase, how it clarifies the distinction between anomalous and spontaneous ways of breaking symmetries, and what it says about the relation between UV and IR in QCD.

### [2] [Extraction of spectral densities from lattice correlators: decoupling signal from noise](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14652)
*Alessandro Lupo,Nazario Tantalo*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper introduces an alternative method to the Backus-Gilbert regularization for extracting smeared spectral densities from Euclidean correlators, focusing on a decomposition of the solution that allows for optimal truncation to manage statistical noise and improve signal quality.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a new approach to the problem of extracting smeared spectral densities from Euclidean correlators that does not depend on the Backus-Gilbert regularization, which can be limited by its own systematic uncertainties. The new method aims to better control the balance between signal and noise.

Method: The authors propose a solution decomposition akin to singular value decomposition (SVD), where terms contributing most to statistical noise have the least impact on the central value of the smeared spectral density. The key is to optimally truncate this summation to maximize the signal before the noise overwhelms it. The method is also tested as a complementary tool to the stability analysis used in the Backus-Gilbert method.

Result: The results show that the proposed method can effectively separate the signal from the noise, leading to a more reliable extraction of smeared spectral densities. The systematic errors associated with the inverse problem are managed through the optimal truncation of the decomposed solution, allowing for a clearer signal.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the new method provides a viable alternative to the Backus-Gilbert regularization, offering a way to extract smeared spectral densities with improved control over systematic errors and noise. It can be used independently or in conjunction with the Backus-Gilbert method to enhance the stability analysis and achieve a more accurate result.

Abstract: We expand the treatment of the problem of the extraction of smeared spectral densities from Euclidean correlators introduced in [Phys. Rev. D 99, 094508], providing an alternative which does not rely on the Backus-Gilbert regularization. This is possible due to the observation that the solution can be decomposed into a sum of terms, in the spirit of the singular value decomposition, where those with the largest contribution to the statistical noise happen to contribute the least to the central value of the smeared spectral density. The analysis of the systematics of the inverse problem is then shifted to finding the optimal truncation of such summation, so that the signal is saturated before the noise explodes. We scrutinise the performance and systematics of this approach either as a standalone procedure, or to complement the stability analysis required to extrapolate the unbiased result in the Backus-Gilbert regulated version of the solution.

</details>


### [3] [Analyzing the two-dimensional doped Hubbard model with the Worldvolume HMC method](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14965)
*Masafumi Fukuma,Yusuke Namekawa*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The WV-HMC method is successfully applied to the 2D Hubbard model, overcoming the severe sign problem and providing physical observables with controlled statistical errors for a specific lattice size, temperature, and interaction strength.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation of this paper is to address the severe sign problem encountered in the two-dimensional Hubbard model when it's doped (away from half filling). This problem hinders the use of standard methods like the determinant quantum Monte Carlo. The authors aim to show that their Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method can overcome this issue and provide reliable predictions for physical observables under these challenging conditions.

Method: The method used in this paper is the application of the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) to the two-dimensional (2D) Hubbard model. This approach is designed to handle the significant sign problem that arises when simulating the 2D Hubbard model at non-half-filling dopant levels, where traditional methods such as the determinant quantum Monte Carlo fail to produce useful results.

Result: The result of applying the WV-HMC method to the 2D Hubbard model on an $8 \times 8$ lattice at $T/t = 1/6.4 \approx 0.156$ and $U/t = 8.0$ shows that it can predict physical observables with controlled statistical errors. This is a significant achievement because, under these conditions, the conventional determinant quantum Monte Carlo method fails to provide accurate predictions due to the sign problem.

Conclusion: The conclusion of this paper is that the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method is effective in dealing with the sign problem in the 2D Hubbard model and can be used to calculate physical observables with controlled statistical errors, even for systems away from half filling, where other methods struggle.

Abstract: We apply the Worldvolume Hybrid Monte Carlo (WV-HMC) method [arXiv:2012.08468] to the two-dimensional Hubbard model, which is known to suffer from a severe sign problem when the system is doped (away from half filling). We show that the method predicts physical observables with controlled statistical errors on an $8 \times 8$ lattice at temperature $T/t = 1/6.4 \approx 0.156$ and interaction strength $U/t = 8.0$ ($t$ is the hopping amplitude), for which the standard determinant quantum Monte Carlo fails.

</details>
