### [1] [An Update on the Isospin-Breaking Effects in the Pion Decay Constant with Staggered Quarks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19430)
*Alessandro Cotellucci,Davide Giusti*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The BMW Collaboration provides an update on the computation of isospin-breaking effects in the Pion Decay Constant, using Nf=2+1+1 staggered quarks with a near-physical pion mass and QED_L. Preliminary results for the valence-valence contribution to the axial-pseudoscalar correlator are presented, alongside plans for future work.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To improve the accuracy of the Pion Decay Constant calculation by accounting for isospin-breaking effects, which are important for precision tests of the Standard Model and for understanding the fundamental properties of pions.

Method: The method involves using Nf=2+1+1 staggered quarks with a near-physical pion mass and lattice Quantum Electrodynamics (QED_L) to calculate the isosymmetric value of the Pion Decay Constant and the gradient-flow scale w0. The study also includes preliminary calculations of the valence-valence contribution to the axial-pseudoscalar correlator across different volumes and lattice spacings.

Result: Preliminary results for the valence-valence contribution to the axial-pseudoscalar correlator have been obtained for various volumes and lattice spacings. The current determination of the isosymmetric value and the gradient-flow scale w0 has been updated, although specific values are not provided in the abstract.

Conclusion: The BMW Collaboration continues to make progress in calculating isospin-breaking corrections to the Pion Decay Constant, with the next steps including further refinement of the calculations and analysis of the data. The ongoing work aims to provide a more accurate determination of the Pion Decay Constant that includes isospin-breaking effects.

Abstract: We present an update on the ongoing computation of the isospin-breaking effects in the Pion Decay Constant from the BMW Collaboration. The calculation is carried out with N$_f$=2+1+1 staggered quarks with a near-physical pion mass and QED$_{\text{L}}$. We give an update on the isosymmetric value and the current determination used to compute the gradient-flow scale $w_{0}$, then we present some preliminary results of the valence-valence contribution to the axial-pseudoscalar correlator for different volumes and lattice spacings. We also discuss the next steps and plans.

### [2] [Exotic $T^*_{csJ}$ and $T^*_{c\bar{s}J}$ states and coupled-channel scattering at the $SU(3)$ flavour symmetric point from lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19553)
*J. Daniel E. Yeo,Christopher E. Thomas,David J. Wilson*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents the first lattice QCD study of coupled-channel scattering in the flavour-exotic sectors, identifying six flavour-exotic poles and suggesting the existence of an isospin-1/2 partner for the observed T*cs0(2870)0 and T*c̅s0(2900).


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to investigate the recent experimental observations of the flavour-exotic T*cs0(2870)0 and T*c̅s0(2900) by studying coupled-channel scattering using lattice QCD.

Method: The method involves using five volumes with mπ ≈ 700 MeV, large bases of meson-meson operators, and the Lüscher formalism to extract finite-volume spectra and constrain infinite-volume scattering amplitudes.

Result: Six flavour-exotic poles are identified in the energy region constrained, with a virtual bound state and a resonance in J^P = 0+ that is associated with T*cs0(2870)0 and T*c̅s0(2900), and additional partners in J^P = 1+ and 2+. No well-determined poles were found in the flavour 15̅ sector.

Conclusion: The study concludes with the identification of several flavour-exotic states and suggests the existence of an isospin-1/2 partner for the observed T*cs0(2870)0 and T*c̅s0(2900) in the SU(3)f flavour symmetric limit.

Abstract: Motivated by recent experimental observations of the flavour-exotic $T^*_{cs0}(2870)^0$ and $T^*_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$, we present the first lattice QCD study of coupled-channel scattering of a charm meson with a light meson in the flavour-exotic sectors at the $SU(3)_f$ flavour symmetric point. Utilising five volumes with $m_π\approx 700$ MeV and employing large bases of meson-meson operators, finite-volume spectra are extracted and used to constrain infinite-volume scattering amplitudes with $J^P = \{0, 1, 2, 3, 4\}^+$ via the Lüscher formalism. In the flavour $\mathbf{6}$ sector, each $S$-wave channel considered is found to be attractive with the scattering amplitudes having an associated pole singularity on an unphysical sheet below threshold, giving six flavour-exotic poles in the energy region constrained. In $J^P = 0^+$ there is a virtual bound state and a resonance. The latter is identified with the $T^*_{cs0}(2870)^0$ and $T^*_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$, appearing as one state in the $SU(3)_f$ flavour symmetric limit, and suggests the existence of an isospin-$\frac{1}{2}$ partner. In $J^P =1^+$ there are three poles, one of which is identified as a $J^P =1^+$ partner of the $T^*_{cs0}(2870)^0$ and $T^*_{c\bar{s}0}(2900)$, and $J^P =2^+$ contains one pole which is identified as their $J^P =2^+$ partner. Only mild interactions and no poles are seen in the $J^P = \{3, 4\}^+$ scattering amplitudes. In the flavour $\overline{\mathbf{15}}$ sector, weak interactions are observed in $J^P = \{0, 1, 2, 3, 4\}^+$ with no well-determined poles in the energy region constrained.

### [3] [Finite-density equation of state of hot QCD using the complex Langevin equation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19649)
*Michael Mandl,Dénes Sexty,Daniel Unterhuber*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents QCD equation of state results from lattice simulations at high baryon densities and temperatures, using the complex Langevin equation, with agreement to previous studies and perturbative calculations.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To explore the QCD equation of state at unprecedentedly high baryon densities and above the crossover temperature, ensuring that the method used (complex Langevin dynamics) is reliable and converges correctly.

Method: Lattice QCD simulations using the complex Langevin equation, with continuum extrapolation, to compute the baryon density and pressure as functions of the baryon chemical potential and temperature.

Result: The study finds agreement between the computed QCD equation of state and previous lattice studies at smaller chemical potentials, as well as with high-temperature perturbative hard-thermal-loop calculations. The complex Langevin dynamics are shown to be under control and provide correct convergence.

Conclusion: The complex Langevin approach is validated for simulating QCD at high baryon densities and temperatures, contributing to a better understanding of the QCD phase diagram in regions not previously accessible.

Abstract: We present the results of continuum-extrapolated lattice simulations of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) above the crossover temperature and for unprecedentedly high baryon densities at the physical point, employing the complex Langevin equation. In particular, we determine the QCD equation of state by computing the baryon density as well as the pressure as functions of the baryon chemical potential and the temperature. Potential issues with wrong convergence of complex Langevin dynamics are under control and we indeed find agreement with previous lattice studies working at smaller chemical potentials, as well as with perturbative hard-thermal-loop calculations at high temperatures.

</details>
