### [1] [Finite-volume analysis of the $H$-dibaryon including left-hand-cut effects](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22957)
*Arkaitz Rodas,Lin Qiu,César Fernández-Ramírez,Vincent Mathieu,Glòria Montaña,Alessandro Pilloni,Adam P. Szczepaniak*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper uses the finite-volume $N/D$ method to analyze two-baryon interactions from lattice QCD data, incorporating a left-hand cut from one-pion exchange, and compares it with Lüscher's quantization condition, finding that the inclusion of the left-hand cut has a small but significant impact on the binding energy of the H-dibaryon.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to study the influence of including the left-hand cut, induced by one-pion exchange, on the analysis of two-baryon interactions, particularly for the H-dibaryon, using lattice QCD data at an SU(3)$_\text{F}$-symmetric point with a pion mass of around 417 MeV.

Method: The researchers implement the finite-volume $N/D$ representation to study the interactions, and they incorporate the left-hand cut due to one-pion exchange. They also compare their findings with those obtained using the Lüscher quantization condition and effective-range expansions.

Result: The results indicate a mild yet statistically significant effect on the binding energy of the H-dibaryon when the left-hand cut is included in the analysis.

Conclusion: The inclusion of the left-hand cut in the $N/D$ formalism leads to a subtle but important change in the binding energy of the H-dibaryon, suggesting the importance of this feature in the analysis of two-baryon systems.

Abstract: We implement the finite-volume $N/D$ representation to study two-baryon interactions from lattice QCD data. We include the left-hand cut induced by one-pion exchange in this formalism, and study the $H$-dibaryon at the SU(3)$_\text{F}$-symmetric point, with a pion mass around $417$ MeV. The $N/D$ formalism is then compared to the Lüscher quantization condition, used to describe the same system via effective-range expansions. The results show a mild but statistically significant effect produced by the inclusion of the left-hand cut, especially on the binding energy of the $H$-dibaryon.
