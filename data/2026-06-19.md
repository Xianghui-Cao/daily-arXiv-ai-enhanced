### [1] [Reflection-Positive Construction of a Four-Dimensional SU(N) Yang-Mills Theory with Mass Gap and Confinement](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19362)
*Mir Faizal,Arshid Shabir*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper demonstrates how positivity, locality, and renormalization can lead to the natural emergence of a mass gap and confinement in non-Abelian gauge theories, by starting from a lattice formulation of SU(N) Yang-Mills theory and using multiscale analysis to carry over key features to the continuum limit, resulting in a Minkowski theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian that exhibits confinement. The approach is universal and works for both strong and weak coupling regimes, providing a clear mathematical framework for understanding these phenomena.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a mathematically rigorous and physically insightful explanation for the emergence of the mass gap and confinement in non-Abelian gauge theories, such as pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, without assuming these properties but rather deriving them from fundamental principles like positivity, locality, and renormalization.

Method: The method begins with a reflection-positive lattice formulation of pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, which leads to a transfer operator with a uniform gap and area law behavior for large Wilson loops. A finite-range, gauge-covariant multiscale analysis is then applied to propagate these characteristics through different scales, ensuring exponential clustering and a positive string tension in the continuum. Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction is used to transform the Euclidean results into a Minkowski space theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian. The construction is independent of any specific regulator due to a single-scale Lipschitz control and a telescoping argument, which ensures the uniqueness of the limiting measure and the universality of the results. The same framework allows for an examination of both the strong and weak coupling limits, showing that they converge to the same theory.

Result: The result is a demonstration that, under the conditions of positivity and locality, and through the process of renormalization, it is possible to derive the mass gap and confinement as natural properties of the non-Abelian vacuum, without having to assume them. This leads to a Minkowski theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian, a spectral gap above the vacuum, and a linear potential between static charges, indicating confinement. The approach also establishes the universality of the results across different admissible lattice slicings and shows that the continuum theory is consistent whether approached from the strong or weak coupling regime.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that by adhering to the principles of positivity, locality, and employing renormalization, one can rigorously establish the existence of a mass gap and the phenomenon of confinement within the context of non-Abelian gauge theories. These are not assumed but are shown to be inherent properties of the theory, providing a unified description of the continuum limit that is valid regardless of the initial coupling strength, thus offering a coherent picture of the non-Abelian vacuum.

Abstract: In the Euclidean view one must first require that positivity not be violated, and from this modest demand, together with locality, a great deal follows: starting from a reflection-positive lattice formulation of pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory we obtain a transfer operator with a uniform gap, while large Wilson loops already show an area law by means of convergent character (polymer) expansions; a finite-range, gauge-covariant multiscale analysis then carries these features from one scale to the next with interlaced inequalities whose small defects can be summed, so that exponential clustering and a strictly positive string tension endure in the continuum; the Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction turns these Euclidean facts into a Minkowski theory with a self-adjoint Hamiltonian, the spectral gap lying above the vacuum and the linear potential for static charges appearing, which gives a concrete picture of confinement; the construction depends on no special regulator, for a single-scale Lipschitz control and a telescoping argument bind all admissible reflection-positive slicings into a unique limiting measure and thus secure universality; moreover, the same framework admits entry from weak coupling, so that the continuum reached from strong coupling meets the one approached along an asymptotically free trajectory, yielding one and the same theory; in my view this is how mathematical clarity and physical insight cooperate: positivity, locality, and renormalization working together so that the mass gap and confinement are not marvels to be assumed, but natural properties of the non-Abelian vacuum.

### [2] [Kernel transformations and bounds for smeared spectral functions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19503)
*William I. Jay,Matteo Saccardi*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A framework for transforming between different smearing kernels in smeared spectral functions is presented, with exact and approximate transformation methods, error propagation, and the use of spectral positivity to tighten bounds.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The need to convert information from one type of energy-smeared observable to another, as required by phenomenology or comparison across calculations, drives the development of this kernel-transformation framework.

Method: The authors establish analytic conditions for the existence and convergence of exact transformations, provide explicit expressions for specific kernel classes, and address the inverse problem using regulated maps with computable systematic error bounds. They also propagate input errors to target observables and utilize spectral positivity to refine the bounds.

Result: Explicit expressions for kernel transformations, such as Cauchy-to-Gaussian and Gaussian-to-Cauchy, are provided, along with a method to handle cases where exact transformations cannot be performed. The approach includes error propagation and the tightening of bounds through the enforcement of spectral positivity.

Conclusion: The work offers a comprehensive solution for transforming between smeared spectral functions, which is applicable to various types of smearing kernels, and provides a robust treatment of associated errors and systematic uncertainties.

Abstract: This work develops a framework for transforming between smeared spectral functions computed using different smearing kernels. The kernel-transformation problem naturally arises when information is available for one family of energy-smeared observables, while phenomenology or comparison with other calculations require a different smearing. For exact transformations, analytic conditions are established for the maps to exist and converge without arbitrary regularization. Explicit expressions are provided for several kernel classes of interest, including Cauchy-to-Gaussian transformations and Gaussian-to-Cauchy width mixtures. When exact transformations are unavailable, the inverse problem is tackled through regulated maps paired with bounds on the associated systematic error, directly computable from the given input data. Errors on the input smeared spectral functions, either statistical or in the form of pointwise rigorous bounds, are then propagated to the target observables. Enforcing spectral positivity can be used to tighten the bounds.

### [3] [Scalar diquark mass and quark--diquark potential from lattice QCD using the potential method with a static quark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19840)
*Kai-Wen Kelvin-Lee,Noriyoshi Ishii*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study uses a HAL QCD-inspired potential method to determine the scalar diquark mass and quark-diquark potential, finding a diquark mass close to (2/3)m_N and a Cornell-type potential with a string tension consistent with Wilson Loop results.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To investigate the scalar diquark mass and the quark-diquark potential in a baryonic system, aiming to self-consistently reproduce the p-wave baryonic spectrum within a potential framework using lattice QCD calculations.

Method: A HAL QCD-inspired potential method is applied to a system of a scalar diquark and a static quark. The calculations are performed on a 32^3 x 64 lattice with 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations, using a pion mass of approximately 702 MeV and an inverse lattice spacing of about 2.176 GeV.

Result: The scalar diquark mass obtained is near (2/3)m_N, and the quark-diquark potential is found to be of the Cornell type, consisting of Coulomb and linear terms. The string tension from this potential is in agreement with that from the static quark-antiquark potential (Wilson Loop) to within around 5%.

Conclusion: The research provides a self-consistent determination of the scalar diquark mass and the quark-diquark potential, which supports the use of the HAL QCD-inspired potential method for studying baryonic systems and confirms the consistency of the string tension with conventional methods.

Abstract: We study the scalar diquark mass and the quark--diquark potential by applying a HAL QCD-inspired potential method to a baryonic system composed of a scalar diquark and a static quark. The diquark mass is determined self-consistently by requiring that the p-wave baryonic spectrum obtained from two-point correlators be reproduced within the potential framework. Numerical calculations are performed using $2+1$ flavor QCD gauge configurations generated by the PACS-CS Collaboration on a $L^{3} \times T = 32^{3} \times 64$ lattice with $a^{-1} \approx 2.176$ GeV and the pion mass, $m_π \approx 702$ MeV. From the analysis, we obtain a scalar diquark mass which is close to the naïve constituent quark estimate $ (2/3)m_{N}$, together with a quark--diquark potential of the Cornell type (Coulomb + linear). The string tension extracted from the quark--diquark potential agrees within approximately 5% with that obtained from the static quark--antiquark potential (Wilson Loop).

### [6] [Confining Flux Tube in the Trace Deformed (2+1) Dimensional SU(2) Gauge Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20270)
*Claudio Bonati,Michele Caselle,Alessio Negro,Dario Panfalone,Lorenzo Verzichelli*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study investigates the confining flux tube in a reconfined phase of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in 2+1 dimensions. It finds that the Nambu-Goto effective string description does not fit the data well as deformation increases, whereas the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution fits better. The transverse profile of the chromo-electric flux tube shows significant deviations from standard behavior, and there is evidence for a change in the order of the reconfinement transition as the deformation parameter increases.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To understand the nature of the confining flux tube in the reconfined phase of a deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory and to explore if the usual effective string descriptions are valid in this regime.

Method: Lattice simulations were performed above the deconfinement temperature to analyze Polyakov-loop correlators and extract the ground state energy of the effective string. The researchers also investigated the transverse profile of the chromo-electric flux tube and explored the phase diagram for different orders of the reconfinement transition.

Result: The Nambu-Goto effective string description was found to be inadequate at higher deformations, while the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution provided an accurate description. There were notable differences in the intrinsic width and the shape of the chromo-electric flux tube compared to the standard Yang-Mills case. An exploratory study of the phase diagram suggested a transition from a continuous to a first-order reconfinement line with increasing deformation.

Conclusion: The reconfined phase of the trace deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory exhibits a distinct effective-string behavior that differs from ordinary confinement, characterized by a rigid-string solution and a modified flux tube profile, indicating a new regime of strong interactions.

Abstract: We study the confining flux tube in the reconfined phase of trace deformed SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in (2+1) dimensions. Using lattice simulations above the standard deconfinement temperature, we analyze Polyakov-loop correlators and extract the ground state energy of the effective string. We show that the usual Nambu-Goto effective string description, including its standard higher-order corrections, fails to reproduce the data as the trace deformation is increased. Remarkably, deep in the reconfined regime the results are instead accurately described by the Polchinski-Yang rigid-string solution, corresponding to an effective string dominated by an extrinsic-curvature term. We further investigate the transverse profile of the chromo-electric flux tube and find significant deviations from the standard Yang-Mills behavior, including a substantial modification of the intrinsic width. Finally, we present an exploratory study of the phase diagram, finding evidence for a transition from a continuous to a first order reconfinement line as the deformation parameter increases. These results suggest that the reconfined phase realizes a qualitatively different effective-string regime from ordinary confinement.
