### [1] [Numerical Hints for Dyon Condensation at $θ=2π$ via Wilson-'t Hooft Loops in $SU(2)$ Yang-Mills Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13428)
*Hiromasa Watanabe,Issaku Kanamori,Okuto Morikawa,Yuki Nagai,Yuya Tanizaki,Akio Tomiya*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper examines the long-distance behaviors of Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators at $θ=2π$ for the $SU(2)$ Wilson lattice gauge action, using a 1-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow. The results support dyon condensation at $θ=2π$, as predicted by theory.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to explore the nontrivial realization of $2π$ periodicity in Yang-Mills theories and to investigate whether confinement vacua at $θ=0$ and $2π$ belong to different SPT states with 1-form center symmetry, as suggested by recent developments in generalized global symmetries.

Method: The method involves measuring the Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators at $θ=2π$ and identifying the gauge topological charge in the presence of defects. The authors use a 1-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow to smear lattice gauge fields and analyze their long-distance behaviors.

Result: The numerical evidence obtained supports the theoretical prediction of dyon condensation at $θ=2π$, rather than monopole condensation.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the numerical findings are consistent with the theoretical expectation of dyon condensation at $θ=2π$ within the context of Yang-Mills theories and the examination of different SPT states with 1-form center symmetry.

Abstract: Yang-Mills theories at $θ$ and $θ+2π$ are unitarily equivalent, but their $2π$ periodicity has a nontrivial realization. Recent developments in generalized global symmetries show that confinement vacua at $θ=0$ and $2π$ should belong to different symmetry-protected topological (SPT) states with the $1$-form center symmetry. For its examination, we measure the Wilson-'t Hooft loop operators at $θ=2π$ for the $SU(2)$ Wilson lattice gauge action and discuss their long-distance behaviors. This requires us to identify the gauge topological charge in the presence of defects, and we employ the $1$-form covariant DBW2 gradient flow to smear lattice gauge fields. We then obtain numerical evidence consistent with dyon condensation at $θ=2π$, rather than monopole condensation, as theoretically predicted.

### [2] [New results on gauge field decomposition in SU(3) gluodynamics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13490)
*V. Bornyakov,V. Goy,I. Kudrov*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper investigates the decomposition of a nonabelian gauge field into an Abelian part, due to monopoles, and a modified nonabelian part without monopoles, in the context of SU(3) lattice gluodynamics. It examines the static potential for these components and finds that the sum of their potentials does not match the original potential when using optimal gauge fixing, but the decomposition is accurate at all distances with a different set of Gribov copies.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the role of Abelian monopoles in the nonabelian gauge field and how they contribute to the static potential, which is fundamental for understanding the dynamics of quarks and gluons in quantum chromodynamics (QCD).

Method: The authors use lattice QCD simulations, specifically SU(3) lattice gluodynamics, and apply Maximal Abelian gauge fixing to decompose the gauge field. They then calculate the static potential for the original gauge field as well as its Abelian and modified nonabelian components, at two different lattice spacings, and compare the results.

Result: The study shows that with optimal gauge fixing, the sum of the potentials from the Abelian and modified nonabelian components does not accurately reproduce the static potential of the original gauge field. However, by using a different set of Gribov copies, the decomposition becomes more accurate across all distances.

Conclusion: The conclusion suggests that the choice of Gribov copies significantly affects the accuracy of the decomposition of the static potential, indicating that the method of gauge fixing and the selection of Gribov copies are critical for correctly representing the contributions of Abelian and nonabelian components to the static potential.

Abstract: We study decomposition of the nonabelian gauge field into the Abelian component created by Abelian monopoles and the modified nonabelian components with monopoles removed after fixing the Maximal Abelian gauge in SU(3) lattice gluodynamics. We compute the static potential V (r) for the original gauge field and for its components V_mon and V_mod at two values of the lattice spacing. We confirm that with optimal gauge fixing the sum V_mon + V_mod deviates substantially from V(r). We show that this decomposition of the static potential is satisfied with good precision at all distances when we use another set of Gribov copies.

</details>
