### [1] [Higher-order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon $g-2$ from lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06806)
*Arnau Beltran,Alessandro Conigli,Simon Kuberski,Harvey B. Meyer,Konstantin Ottnad,Hartmut Wittig*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the next-to-leading order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, achieving sub-percent precision. The result is more precise than previous estimates and shows a 4.8σ tension with data-driven evaluations based on hadronic cross section measurements, excluding recent CMD-3 results.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation for this study is to provide a highly precise calculation of the hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, which is critical for testing the Standard Model and searching for new physics. Previous estimates have not reached the level of precision achieved in this work, and discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements could indicate new physics beyond the Standard Model.

Method: The authors use lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) with $N_{\mathrm{f}}=2+1$ flavors of $\mathrm{O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions across six different lattice spacings and a range of pion masses including the physical value. They employ the time-momentum representation for the space-like kernel and combine it with the spatially summed vector correlator. The analysis also accounts for finite-size corrections and isospin-breaking effects.

Result: The final, continuum-extrapolated result for the next-to-leading order HVP contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment is $a_μ^{\mathrm{hvp,\,nlo}}=-101.69(25)_{\mathrm{stat}}(53)_{\mathrm{syst}}\times10^{-11}$. This value is two times more precise than the estimate from the 2025 White Paper of the Muon $(g-2)$ Theory Initiative and lies below it by 1.5$σ$. There is a significant 4.8$σ$ tension with data-driven evaluations based on hadronic cross section measurements, except for the recent CMD-3 result.

Conclusion: The conclusion of the paper is that the calculated next-to-leading order HVP contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment provides a more precise value compared to previous theoretical estimates. The discrepancy with certain data-driven evaluations suggests the need for further investigation into the source of the tension, which may have implications for our understanding of the Standard Model and the potential discovery of new physics.

Abstract: We present the first lattice QCD calculation of the next-to-leading order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment with sub-percent precision. We employ the time-momentum representation for the space-like kernel, which is combined with the spatially summed vector correlator computed on CLS ensembles with $N_{\mathrm{f}}=2+1$ flavors of $\mathrm{O}(a)$-improved Wilson fermions, covering six lattice spacings between $0.039$ and $0.097\,$fm and a range of pion masses including the physical value. After accounting for finite-size corrections and isospin-breaking effects, we obtain as our final, continuum-extrapolated result $a_μ^{\mathrm{hvp,\,nlo}}=-101.69(25)_{\mathrm{stat}}(53)_{\mathrm{syst}}\times10^{-11}$. It lies below the estimate provided by the 2025 White Paper of the Muon $(g-2)$ Theory Initiative by 1.5$σ$ but is two times more precise. It also exhibits a strong tension of 4.8$σ$ with data-driven evaluations based on hadronic cross section measurements excluding the recent result by CMD-3.

### [2] [Studying the QCD phase diagram using pressure derivatives from lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07140)
*Sipaz Sharma*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper explores the use of QCD pressure derivatives in lattice QCD to construct observables that investigate the QCD phase diagram at physical quark masses, focusing on how (2+1)-flavor chiral phase transition impacts energy-like and magnetization-like observables, as well as discussing deconfinement and the importance of Taylor expansion convergence for finding the QCD critical endpoint.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the QCD phase diagram and related phenomena, such as the chiral crossover and deconfinement, by using the derivatives of the QCD pressure. The authors aim to provide insights into the behavior of these observables at both zero and non-zero chemical potentials, which can help in locating the QCD critical endpoint.

Method: The method involves calculating the derivatives of the QCD pressure within the lattice QCD framework and using these to construct observables that are sensitive to the QCD phase structure. The analysis includes the examination of the (2+1)-flavor chiral phase transition's influence on the observables and the exploration of the convergence properties of the Taylor series expansion of the QCD pressure.

Result: The results show how the energy-like and magnetization-like observables are affected by the (2+1)-flavor chiral phase transition. Additionally, the paper discusses the characteristics of the chiral crossover at different chemical potentials and the relevance of the Taylor expansion convergence for identifying the QCD critical endpoint.

Conclusion: The conclusion emphasizes the significance of the QCD pressure derivatives in probing the QCD phase diagram and the potential for using the Taylor expansion convergence to locate the QCD critical endpoint, which is a key feature of the phase structure of QCD.

Abstract: We summarize the application of derivatives of the QCD pressure, calculated within the framework of lattice QCD, in constructing observables that probe aspects of the QCD phase diagram at physical quark masses. We outline how the behavior of energy-like and magnetization-like observables at physical quark masses is influenced by the $(2+1)$-flavor chiral phase transition. We describe features of the chiral crossover at vanishing and non-vanishing chemical potentials and discuss deconfinement at zero chemical potential. We address the relevance of the convergence properties of the Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure in the search for the QCD critical endpoint.

### [3] [General Hamiltonian Approach to the $\mathbf{N}$-Body Finite-Volume Formalism: Extracting the $\mathbfω$ Resonance Parameters from Lattice QCD](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07205)
*Kang Yu*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: A nonperturbative Hamiltonian framework (NPHF) is introduced to solve the general N-body problem, connecting lattice QCD spectra to experimental scattering data. The method is demonstrated by extracting resonance parameters of the ω meson from isoscalar 3π and isovector 2π systems using lattice QCD data, providing a unified approach for resonance dynamics with broad applications in particle, nuclear, and astrophysical physics.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to develop a rigorous connection between finite-volume spectra obtained from lattice QCD and the scattering observables measured in experiments, which is essential for understanding the dynamics of resonances and multi-particle systems, especially in the context of three-body interactions that are prevalent in exotic hadrons, halo nuclei, and neutron star matter.

Method: The authors introduce a nonperturbative Hamiltonian framework (NPHF) that unifies the treatment of single-particle, two-particle, and three-particle dynamics within a single unitary formalism. They use this framework to analyze isoscalar 3π and isovector 2π systems, fitting the lattice QCD spectra provided by the Chinese Lattice QCD Collaboration at specific pion masses to extract the resonance parameters of the ω meson.

Result: The application of the NPHF to the lattice QCD data allows for an accurate description of the lattice spectra and provides robust determinations of the pole positions for both the ρ and ω mesons, thus demonstrating the framework's capability to extract resonance parameters from finite-volume spectra.

Conclusion: The work establishes a foundational approach for extracting resonance dynamics from finite-volume lattice QCD spectra, offering a versatile tool for studying complex many-body systems across different fields of physics, including particle, nuclear, and astrophysics.

Abstract: We present a nonperturbative Hamiltonian framework (NPHF) to address the general $N$-body problem. This framework rigorously connects finite-volume spectra from lattice QCD to scattering observables from experiment. To demonstrate its applicability, we extract the resonance parameters of the $ω$ meson by simultaneously analyzing the isoscalar $3π$ and isovector $2π$ systems. The Hamiltonian unifies single-particle $ω$, two-particle $ρπ$, and three-particle $πππ$ dynamics within a single unitary formalism. Using leading lattice QCD spectra from the Chinese Lattice QCD Collaboration at $m_π$ = 208 and 305 MeV, we perform a fit in the isovector and isoscalar channels, accurately describe the lattice spectra and obtain robust determinations of the $ρ$ and $ω$ pole positions. This work establishes a foundational approach for extracting resonance dynamics from finite-volume spectra. Given the ubiquity of three-body dynamics in exotic hadrons, halo nuclei, and neutron star matter, this general formalism holds broad relevance across particle, nuclear, and astrophysical physics.

### [4] [Lattice Determination of the Baryon Junction Mass in $(2+1)$ Dimensions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08268)
*Dario Panfalone,Michele Caselle,Nicodemo Magnoli,Lorenzo Verzichelli*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper explores baryonic flux tube configurations in $SU(3)$ Yang--Mills theory, using high-precision simulations to determine the baryon junction mass parameter. It also tests the Svetitsky--Yaffe conjecture, finding agreement with 2D three-state Potts model predictions near the deconfinement transition.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to investigate and understand the behavior of baryonic flux tubes within the $SU(3)$ Yang--Mills theory by utilizing next-to-leading-order results from Effective String Theory (EST) that include corrections related to the baryon junction mass. Additionally, the study aims to test the Svetitsky--Yaffe conjecture in the context of these systems at high temperatures.

Method: The method involves a non-perturbative determination of the baryon junction mass parameter through high-precision simulations of the three-point Polyakov-loop in the open string channel. The authors use recent next-to-leading-order results from the EST framework, which account for corrections up to order $1/R^2$. They also examine the high-temperature regime to test the Svetitsky--Yaffe conjecture, comparing their lattice results with the two-dimensional three-state Potts model predictions.

Result: The results show that the non-perturbative determination of the baryon junction mass is feasible through the described simulations. Furthermore, the analysis of the high-temperature regime supports the Svetitsky--Yaffe conjecture, as the correlators obtained from the lattice are in close agreement with the predictions made by the two-dimensional three-state Potts model near the deconfinement transition.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the baryon junction mass can be accurately determined using high-precision simulations and that the Svetitsky--Yaffe conjecture holds true for the baryonic system under study, particularly in the vicinity of the deconfinement transition, where the behavior of the system aligns with the 2D three-state Potts model.

Abstract: This contribution investigates baryonic flux tube configurations in $SU(3)$ Yang--Mills theory in $(2+1)$ dimensions. Leveraging recent next-to-leading-order results within the Effective String Theory (EST) framework, which explicitly include corrections proportional to the baryon junction mass $M$ up to order $1/R^2$, we carry out a non-perturbative determination of this parameter, through high-precision simulations of the three-point Polyakov-loop in the open string channel. In addition, the high-temperature regime of the baryonic system is examined in order to test the Svetitsky--Yaffe conjecture. Close to the deconfinement transition, the lattice results for the correlators show close agreement with the predictions of the two-dimensional three-state Potts model.
