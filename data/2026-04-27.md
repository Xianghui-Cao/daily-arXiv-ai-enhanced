### [1] [Inclusive semileptonic $D_s\to X_s\ell\barν$ decays from lattice QCD: continuum and chiral extrapolation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22201)
*Ryan Kellermann,Alessandro Barone,Ahmed Elgaziari,Shoji Hashimoto,Zhi Hu,Andreas Jüttner,Takashi Kaneko*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper reports on the inclusive semileptonic decay rate of $D_s 	o X_s \ell\bar{\nu}$ using lattice QCD, with chiral and continuum extrapolations. The results are consistent with experimental data, achieving a few percent level of error.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide a precise theoretical prediction for the inclusive semileptonic $D_s$ meson decay rate, which can be compared with experimental measurements to test the Standard Model and constrain its parameters.

Method: The method involves using lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) with 2+1 flavors of Möbius domain-wall fermions to perform chiral and continuum extrapolations, while also addressing systematic errors from the integration over all possible final states.

Result: The result is a theoretical determination of the $D_s \to X_s \ell\bar{\nu}$ decay rate that is in agreement with the current experimental data, with an achieved precision of a few percent.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the lattice QCD calculations provide a reliable and accurate prediction for the inclusive semileptonic $D_s$ decays, which can be used to support or challenge the predictions of the Standard Model and to refine our understanding of quark dynamics.

Abstract: We present results for the inclusive semileptonic $D_s \to X_s \ell\barν$ decay rate from lattice QCD. Chiral and continuum extrapolations are performed using gauge ensembles generated with 2+1 flavours of Möbius domain-wall fermions. Systematic errors are fully addressed including those from the integral over all possible final states. Our results are in agreement with currently available experimental data, with an error at the few-percent level.

### [3] [Quark Number Susceptibilities and Conserved Charge Fluctuations in $(2+1)$-flavor QCD with Möbius domain-wall fermions (MDWF)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22514)
*Jishnu Goswami,Yasumichi Aoki,Hidenori Fukaya,Shoji Hashimoto,Issaku Kanamori,Takashi Kaneko,Yoshifumi Nakamura,David Ward,Yu Zhang*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper presents calculations of second- and selected fourth-order conserved-charge fluctuations in (2+1)-flavor QCD using Möbius domain-wall fermions, comparing results with hadron resonance gas model predictions, particularly around the pseudocritical temperature and at different quark mass ratios.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To explore the behavior of conserved-charge fluctuations in (2+1)-flavor QCD at various temperatures and quark mass ratios, and to compare the lattice QCD results with predictions from the hadron resonance gas model, especially across the crossover region near the pseudocritical temperature.

Method: The authors use Möbius domain-wall fermions to calculate conserved-charge fluctuations for two light-to-strange quark-mass ratios (m_l/m_s = 1/10 and 1/27.4) on lattices with different temporal extents (N_τ = 12 and 16). The calculations are performed along a line of constant physics, enabling an examination of both lattice-spacing effects and the dependence on the light-quark mass down to the physical point.

Result: Below the pseudocritical temperature, the calculated second-order electric-charge, strangeness, and off-diagonal conserved-charge fluctuations agree with the QMHRG2020 hadron resonance gas calculations. In the crossover region, these observables increase rapidly and approach their Stefan-Boltzmann limits. For the physical pion mass, some fourth-order cumulants were also computed, allowing for a preliminary comparison with hadron resonance gas predictions, despite the statistical challenges.

Conclusion: The study provides a detailed analysis of conserved-charge fluctuations in (2+1)-flavor QCD, demonstrating consistency with hadron resonance gas model predictions below the pseudocritical temperature, and a rapid rise towards the Stefan-Boltzmann limit in the crossover region. The results contribute to the understanding of the QCD phase diagram and the properties of strongly interacting matter at finite temperature.

Abstract: We calculate second- and selected fourth-order conserved-charge fluctuations in $(2+1)$-flavor QCD using Möbius domain-wall fermions (MDWF) along a line of constant physics. Gauge ensembles were generated for two light-to-strange quark-mass ratios, $m_l/m_s=1/10$ and $1/27.4$, corresponding to heavier-than-physical and physical pion masses, respectively. For $m_l/m_s=1/10$, calculations were carried out on lattices with temporal extents $N_τ=12$ and $16$, enabling an assessment of lattice-spacing effects at heavier pion mass. For $m_l/m_s=1/27.4$, calculations were performed at $N_τ=12$, allowing us to study the light-quark-mass dependence down to the physical point. Below the pseudocritical temperature, second-order electric-charge, strangeness, and off-diagonal conserved-charge fluctuations are consistent with QMHRG2020 hadron resonance gas calculations. Across the crossover region, these observables rise rapidly and tend toward their Stefan--Boltzmann limits. Selected fourth-order cumulants were also computed at the physical pion mass. Although these observables are statistically more demanding, several channels with controlled uncertainties permit a first comparison with hadron resonance gas calculations.
