### [1] [Towards determination of the strong coupling $α_s(m_Z)$ from four-flavor lattice QCD using the continuous $β$-function method](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21059)
*Yash Mandlecha,Alexei Bazavov,Akhil Chauhan,Mingwei Dai,Carleton DeTar,Aida El-Khadra,Steven Gottlieb,Anna Hasenfratz,Leon Hostetler,Andreas S. Kronfeld,Ethan T. Neil,Curtis T. Peterson,James Simone*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper discusses a program aimed at determining the value of the strong coupling $\alpha_s(m_{Z})$ with high precision using lattice QCD simulations, and it outlines the next steps for achieving an accurate determination.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to achieve a highly precise (approximately 0.3%) determination of the strong coupling constant $\alpha_s(m_{Z})$ at the Z-boson mass, which is crucial for high-energy phenomenology and precision tests of quantum chromodynamics (QCD).

Method: The method involves using the renormalization group $\beta$-function in the infinite volume gradient flow scheme based on lattice QCD simulations. The researchers analyze four-flavor HISQ ensembles and consider both tree-level cutoff effects and finite-mass effects.

Result: The results include the status of the program targeting a 0.3% determination of $\alpha_s(m_{Z})$, with plans for further analysis including infinite-volume and continuum extrapolations necessary for a more precise determination.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the outlined program and next steps, such as addressing infinite-volume and continuum limits, are essential for the precise determination of $\alpha_s(m_{Z})$. This will contribute to the accuracy of high-energy physics predictions and QCD tests.

Abstract: The precise value of the strong coupling $α_s(m_{Z})$ at the $Z$-boson mass $m_{Z}$ is essential for high-energy phenomenology and precision tests of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). We present the status of a program targeting a $\sim 0.3\%$ determination of $α_s(m_{Z})$ using the renormalization group $β$-function in the infinite volume gradient flow scheme based on lattice QCD simulations of degenerate four-flavor highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) ensembles. In particular, we analyze both tree-level cutoff effects and finite-mass effects. We also outline the next steps of the analysis, including the infinite-volume and continuum extrapolations required for a precise determination of $α_s(m_Z)$.

### [2] [Exact center symmetry and first-order phase transition in QCD with three degenerate dynamical quarks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22046)
*Gergely Endrodi,Guy D. Moore,Adam Pieczynski,Alessandro Sciarra*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The study uses lattice simulations to explore QCD with three degenerate flavors of quarks, finding a first-order deconfinement phase transition at a specific imaginary isospin chemical potential. The research also examines the QCD behavior in the mass-isospin chemical potential plane, providing insights into chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to understand the phase structure of QCD, particularly how chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement are related, by studying the theory under conditions where an exact center symmetry can be maintained, akin to pure gauge theory, and then observing the changes as temperature increases.

Method: The method involves using first-principles lattice QCD simulations with stout-smeared rooted staggered quarks. The team analyzes the finite-size scaling of the Polyakov loop distribution using a multi-histogram approach to identify the nature of the phase transition. They also vary the quark masses to map out the QCD phase diagram in the mass-isospin chemical potential plane.

Result: The results show that there is a first-order deconfinement phase transition for a specific choice of imaginary isospin chemical potential. The study also provides a sketch of the QCD behavior in the mass-isospin chemical potential plane, offering new perspectives on the interplay between chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement phenomena.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that at low temperatures, the exact center symmetry is preserved, while it is spontaneously broken at high temperatures, leading to a first-order deconfinement phase transition. The work advances the understanding of the fundamental phase diagram of strong interactions and the connection between chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement.

Abstract: We study QCD with three degenerate flavors of dynamical quarks using first-principles lattice simulations. For a specific choice of imaginary isospin chemical potential, this theory possesses an exact center symmetry, just like pure gauge theory. This exact symmetry is expected to be intact at low temperatures and spontaneously broken in the high-temperature regime. By analyzing the finite-size scaling of the Polyakov loop distribution, obtained with a dedicated multi-histogram approach, we demonstrate that there is a first-order deconfinement phase transition in between. Our results are obtained employing stout-smeared rooted staggered quarks at one lattice spacing. Using simulations at different quark masses we sketch the behavior of QCD in the mass-isospin chemical potential plane, shedding new light on this corner of the fundamental phase diagram of the strong interactions and the relationship between chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement.

### [3] [Lattice study of the critical bubble in $\mathrm{SU(8)}$ deconfinement transition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22088)
*Kari Rummukainen,Riikka Seppä,David J. Weir*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: This paper investigates the confinement-deconfinement phase transition in a 4D SU(8) pure gauge model using multicanonical Monte Carlo, aiming to determine the critical bubble probability for the first time in a pure Yang-Mills model. The study compares the obtained results with those from thin wall calculations and assesses the effectiveness of different lattice pseudo-order parameters in resolving the condensation transition.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: The motivation is to provide accurate information on the bubble nucleation rate, which is essential for determining the expected gravitational wave signal from a first order phase transition. This is particularly relevant for strongly coupled theories, such as dark matter candidates, that can undergo first order thermal phase transitions and potentially produce a stochastic gravitational wave background. Previous studies have relied on semiclassical methods, and this work seeks to improve upon these by using a more precise approach.

Method: The method employed is the use of multicanonical Monte Carlo simulations to study the confinement-deconfinement phase transition in a 4D SU(8) pure gauge model. The authors aim to resolve the critical bubble, determine its probability, and compare it with theoretical predictions from thin wall calculations. They also evaluate various lattice pseudo-order parameters to identify which ones are most effective in distinguishing the metastable phase from the critical bubble branch.

Result: The result is the determination of the critical bubble probability in a pure Yang-Mills model for the first time. The authors successfully compare their findings with thin wall approximation results and highlight the importance of selecting an appropriate order parameter to accurately distinguish between the metastable phase and the critical bubble configurations.

Conclusion: The conclusion drawn is that the choice of order parameter is crucial for accurately resolving the critical configurations during the phase transition. The study provides a foundation for future work on determining the nucleation rate in strongly coupled models, which could lead to a better understanding of the gravitational wave signals from first order phase transitions.

Abstract: Strongly coupled theories are of phenomenological interest, for example as dark matter candidates. Theories that can undergo first order thermal phase transitions are particularly appealing as potential sources of a stochastic gravitational wave background. Determining the expected gravitational wave signal from a first order phase transition requires accurate information on the bubble nucleation rate, but thus far for strongly coupled models these have relied on semiclassical methods. As a first step towards determining the nucleation rate, in this paper we study the confinement-deconfinement phase transition in a 4D SU(8) pure gauge model, using multicanonical Monte Carlo. Resolving the critical bubble for the first time in a pure Yang-Mills model, we determine the critical bubble probability and compare it to results from thin wall calculations. We also compare the effectiveness of different lattice pseudo-order parameters at resolving the condensation transition between the metastable phase and critical bubble branch, and point out the choice of order parameter is crucial to accurately resolve the critical configurations.

</details>
