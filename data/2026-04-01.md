### [1] [The Casimir Effect for Lattice Fermions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28789)
*Yash V. Mandlecha*

Main category: hep-lat

TL;DR: The paper examines the Casimir effect for photons and Dirac fermions in (D+1)-dimensional spacetime, both analytically and numerically. It implements MIT bag boundary conditions on a lattice and confirms that as the lattice spacing approaches zero, the results align with continuum theory. The study also explores periodic and antiperiodic boundary conditions, showing that Wilson and overlap fermions match the expected continuum behavior, while naive fermions show oscillatory patterns. Additionally, the paper discusses the implications for negative-mass fermions and their relation to topological insulators.

Motivation: The motivation is to explore and understand the Casimir effect for different types of fermions in various dimensions and under different boundary conditions, as well as to investigate the behavior of fermion formulations on a lattice and their convergence to the continuum limit. The work aims to clarify the universality of the Casimir effect for Dirac fermions and to connect it with bulk and surface states in topological insulators.

Method: The authors use analytical methods such as the Abel-Plana formula to compute the Casimir energy for free naive and Wilson fermions in (1+1) dimensions, and numerical simulations for higher dimensions. The study considers MIT bag, periodic, and antiperiodic boundary conditions and compares the results from the lattice calculations with the continuum theory.

Result: The results show that the lattice calculations agree with the continuum expressions for the Casimir energy when the lattice spacing goes to zero, for all types of fermions. Naive fermions exhibit doubling effects and oscillatory behavior, particularly under periodic and antiperiodic boundary conditions. Wilson and overlap fermions reproduce the expected continuum behavior. The paper also finds that naive fermions can indeed capture the Casimir effect for Dirac fermions in the continuum limit, which is consistent with the concept of universality. The work further discusses the connection between negative-mass fermions and the physics of topological insulators.

Conclusion: The conclusion is that the Casimir effect for Dirac fermions can be accurately described using lattice fermions, including naive, Wilson, and overlap fermions, provided that the appropriate continuum limit is taken. The study confirms the universality of the Casimir effect across different fermion formulations and highlights the importance of considering boundary conditions. The research also provides insights into the role of negative-mass fermions in the context of topological insulators.

Abstract: The Casimir effect for photons and Dirac fermion fields, and its generalization to $(D+1)$-dimensional spacetime in the continuum, is studied. We implement MIT bag boundary conditions on the lattice by treating the system as a confined fermionic slab with perfectly conducting parallel plates. Using the formalism developed for lattice fermions, we compute the Casimir energy for free naive and Wilson fermions analytically in $(1+1)$ dimensions using the Abel-Plana formula, and numerically in higher dimensions. The Casimir energy for overlap fermions with a Möbius domain wall kernel is also evaluated numerically. For MIT bag boundary conditions, the lattice results agree with the continuum expressions in the limit of vanishing lattice spacing for all fermion formulations. Fermion doubling effects are observed for naive fermions. No oscillatory behavior of the Casimir energy is observed in this case for either massive or massless fermions. We also study periodic and antiperiodic boundary conditions. In these cases, Wilson and overlap fermions reproduce the expected continuum behavior, while naive fermions exhibit oscillations with even and odd lattice sizes and approach different limits. Contrary to earlier claims, our numerical results indicate that naive fermions can reproduce the Casimir effect for Dirac fermions in the appropriate continuum limit, consistent with universality. Finally, we comment on extensions to negative-mass Wilson and overlap fermions and their connection to bulk and surface states in topological insulators.
